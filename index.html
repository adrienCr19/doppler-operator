Overview

A series of recent papers by Chluba, Ravenni, Rosenberg, Hoey & Long [CR25, CR26, RC26, HLC26] introduced the boost operator \({}^d_s\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,\beta)\): the exact transformation law for frequency-dependent, spin-weighted spherical-harmonic coefficients of radiation fields between frames in relative motion. Its power comes from a small set of recurrence relations that generate any element from a few seeds, turning otherwise cumbersome radiative-transfer computations (relativistic SZ effect, Kompaneets equation, CMB frame transformations) into mechanical algebra.

For actual scattering problems the boost operator always appears in a specific round-trip combination — boost into the electron rest frame, project on a scattering multipole, boost back. [RC26] named this combination the Doppler operator \(\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta)\), tabulated its thermal averages, and observed unexplained structure in its coefficients, noting that this “hints at another way to simplify matters, a problem we will leave to future work.”

This page takes up exactly that problem. Section 1 catalogs all known properties and identities of the boost operator, with equation-level citations, emphasizing the recursions that generate higher index values from lower ones. Section 2 defines the Doppler operator, and Section 3 presents the new recursion relations and symmetries we derived for it — including a closed rapidity ODE that generates all of its elements, and a reflection symmetry \(\Oh \to 3-\Oh\) that proves the empirically observed diffusion-operator structure. Section 4 develops the structure theory behind those relations (their phase-space origin, the thermal-average function class and its calculus, the generating function, the recoil lattice), and Section 5 applies the framework to polarization, the kinematic dipole, and the assembled exact thermal SZ operator. Every relation links to a complete proof page and was verified independently by numerical quadrature.

0  Notation and conventions

Electron speed \(\beta = v/c\), Lorentz factor \(\gamma = (1-\beta^2)^{-1/2}\), momentum \(p = \gamma\beta\), rapidity \(\eta = \tanh^{-1}\beta\) (so \(\gamma = \cosh\eta\), \(p = \sinh\eta\)). Direction cosines \(\mu = \cos\theta\) with respect to the boost axis \(\hat{\boldsymbol\beta} \parallel \hat z\). Frequencies transform as \(\nu' = \gamma\nu(1-\beta\mu)\), \(\nu = \gamma\nu'(1+\beta\mu')\), and angles as \(\mu' = \frac{\mu-\beta}{1-\beta\mu}\), \(\mu = \frac{\mu'+\beta}{1+\beta\mu'}\) [CR25, Eq. 5].

The energy-shift generator is \(\Oh = -\nu\partial_\nu\) [CR25, Eq. 3]; power laws are its eigenfunctions, \(\Oh\, \nu^{q} = -q\,\nu^{q}\). The photon diffusion operator of Kompaneets theory is \(\Dv \equiv \nu^{-2}\partial_\nu \nu^4 \partial_\nu = \Oh^2 - 3\Oh\) [CR26, Eq. 14]. A quantity has Doppler weight \(d\) if it transforms with \(d\) powers of the Doppler factor: occupation number \(n(\nu)\) has \(d=0\), thermodynamic temperature \(d=1\), specific intensity \(d=3\).

Aberration kernel (defining convention of this site) verified [DC14; CR25, Eq. 7; RC26, Eq. B4]
\[ {}^{d}_{s}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) \;=\; \int \dd^2\hat{y}'\; \frac{{}_{-s}Y^*_{\ell m}(\hat y')\, {}_{-s}Y_{\ell' m}(\hat y[\hat y'])}{\left[\gamma(1+\beta\mu')\right]^{d}}, \qquad \mu[\mu'] = \frac{\mu'+\beta}{1+\beta\mu'} , \]

with \({}^{d}_{s}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\) obtained by \(\beta\to-\beta\). It describes how multipole coefficients of a frequency-independent, Doppler-weight-\(d\) field mix under a Lorentz boost: \(X'_{\ell m} = \sum_{\ell'} {}^{d}_{-s}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,\beta)\, X_{\ell' m}\). Coupling coefficients throughout: \({}_s C^m_\ell = \frac{1}{\ell}\sqrt{\frac{(\ell^2-m^2)(\ell^2-s^2)}{4\ell^2-1}}\); for \(s=0\) we write \(C^m_\ell = \sqrt{\frac{\ell^2-m^2}{4\ell^2-1}}\). We state results for \(s = 0\) (the intensity/occupation sector relevant to the unpolarized problem) and cite the spin-weighted generalizations where established.

Different papers use observer vs. field conventions that amount to \(\beta \leftrightarrow -\beta\) and transposition. Every identity on this page is written in the single convention above and was independently re-verified in it; cited equation numbers indicate provenance, not verbatim transcription.

1  The boost operator: properties and identities

The boost operator extends the aberration kernel to frequency-dependent fields \(X(\nu,\hat y)\):  \({}_s X'_{\ell m}(\nu) = \sum_{\ell'} {}^{d}_{-s}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,\beta)\, {}_s X_{\ell'm}(\nu)\) [CR25, Eq. 2]. Below, each boxed identity carries its citation and a verification badge (verified = re-derived and confirmed numerically by our harness; literature = quoted from the cited source).

1.1 Definition and reduction to the aberration kernel

(B1) Fundamental identity verified [CR25, Eqs. 3, 12; precursor YP17]
\[ {}^{d}_{s}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,\beta) \;=\; {}^{\,d+\Oh}_{\;\;s}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta). \]

The boost operator is the aberration kernel with its Doppler weight promoted to the operator \(d + \Oh\). Every kernel identity that is analytic in \(d\) therefore lifts to a boost-operator identity. On power laws it reduces to a scalar-weight kernel: \({}^{d}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,\beta)\,\nu^q = {}^{d-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta)\,\nu^q\) (see Lemma L1).

(B2) Series representation literature [CR25, Eqs. 9–11]
\[ {}^{d}_{s}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,\beta) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} {}^{d,k}_{\;\;s}\mathcal{H}^m_{\ell\ell'}(\beta)\, \frac{\nu^k\partial_\nu^k}{k!}, \qquad {}^{d,k}_{\;\;s}\mathcal{H}^m_{\ell\ell'}(\beta) = \sum_{t=0}^{k}(-1)^{k-t}\binom{k}{t}\, {}^{d-t}_{\;\;s}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta). \]

Since \(\big(\tfrac{\nu'}{\nu}-1\big)^k\) is a polynomial of order \(k\) in \(\gamma\beta\mu'\), a computation to fixed order \(N_\beta\) in \(\beta\) needs only \(k \le N_\beta\) frequency derivatives [CR25, §2.2].

1.2 Symmetries

(B3) Parity, \(m\)-flip, conjugation, transposition verified [CR25, Eqs. 13–15; DC14]
\[ {}^{d}_{s}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = (-1)^{\ell+\ell'}\,{}^{d}_{-s}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\beta), \qquad {}_{-s}\Kk^{-m}_{\ell\ell'} = \big[{}_{s}\Kk^{m}_{\ell\ell'}\big]^* = {}_{s}\Kk^{m}_{\ell\ell'} , \] \[ \textbf{(transposition / weight flip)}\qquad {}^{d}_{s}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) \;=\; {}^{\,2-d}_{\;\;s}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell}(+\beta), \]

and the corresponding boost-operator forms with \(d\to d+\Oh\), e.g. \({}^{d}_{s}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,-\beta) = (-1)^{\ell+\ell'}\, {}^{d}_{-s}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,\beta)\) [CR25, Eq. 14a] and \({}^{d}_{s}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,\beta) = {}^{\,2-d-\Oh}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell}(\beta)\)-type relations [CR25, Eq. 15]. The transposition identity written above is the form that holds in this site’s convention (weight flip and \(\beta\)-flip); it is central to the new results of Section 3.

(B4) Inversion verified [CR25, Eqs. 16–17]
\[ \sum_{\ell'} {}^{d}_{s}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,-\beta)\, {}^{d}_{s}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) = \delta_{\ell\ell''}, \qquad \sum_{\ell'} {}^{d}_{s}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,-\beta)\, {}^{d}_{s}\Bo^m_{\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) = \delta_{\ell\ell''}. \]
(B5) Commutation and rapidity additivity verified (additivity) [CR25, Eqs. 18–19]
\[ {}^{d}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,\beta)\,{}^{d}\Bo^m_{\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta') = {}^{d}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,\beta')\,{}^{d}\Bo^m_{\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) \quad\text{(as \(\Oh\)-functions, they commute)}, \] \[ \sum_{\ell'} {}^{d}_{s}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,\eta_1)\, {}^{d}_{s}\Bo^m_{\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\eta_2) = {}^{d}_{s}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell''}(\nu,\eta_1+\eta_2). \]

Rapidity is additive; two successive collinear boosts compose exactly. Our harness confirms additivity for the kernel family at the \(10^{-24}\) level (04_additivity.wl).

1.3 Recurrence relations (the generators)

(B6) Doppler-weight raising — row-shift form verified [DC14; CR25, Eq. 20; HLC26, Eq. A.7a]
\[ {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\beta) = \gamma\, {}^{d+1}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\beta) - \gamma\beta\Big[ C^m_{\ell+1}\,{}^{d+1}\Kk^m_{\ell+1,\ell'}(\beta) + C^m_{\ell}\,{}^{d+1}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(\beta) \Big] \quad (s=0), \]

with spin-weighted version carrying the extra \(\tfrac{sm}{\ell(\ell+1)}\) mid-term [CR25, Eq. 20a,b]. The boost-operator version follows by \(d \to d+\Oh\), e.g. the weight-\(-1\) reduction used in scattering problems: \({}^{-1}\hat{\mathcal T}^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,-\beta) \equiv {}^{-1}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,-\beta)/\gamma = {}^{0}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'} - \beta\big[C^m_{\ell+1}\,{}^{0}\Bo^m_{\ell+1\,\ell'} + C^m_{\ell}\,{}^{0}\Bo^m_{\ell-1\,\ell'}\big]\) [CR25, Eq. 49].

(B7) Doppler-weight raising — column-shift form verified [DC14; cf. HLC26, Eq. A.7b]
\[ {}^{d+1}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\beta) = \gamma\, {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\beta) + \gamma\beta\Big[ C^m_{\ell'+1}\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell,\ell'+1}(\beta) + C^m_{\ell'}\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell,\ell'-1}(\beta) \Big] \quad (s=0). \]

B6 and B7 use the three-term recurrence of \(\mu\, {}_sY_{\ell m}\) on the primed and unprimed harmonic respectively; together they let one walk the whole Doppler-weight ladder in either index.

(B8) \(k\)-raising for the \(\mathcal{H}\)-kernels literature [CR25, Eqs. 21–22]
\[ {}^{d,k}\mathcal{H}^m_{\ell\ell'} = {}^{d-1,k-1}\mathcal{H}^m_{\ell\ell'} - {}^{d,k-1}\mathcal{H}^m_{\ell\ell'}, \]

plus a closed raising relation in \(k\) built on B6 [CR25, Eq. 22]; no \(k\)-lowering relation is known. Starting from \({}^{d,0}\mathcal{H} \equiv {}^{d}\Kk(-\beta)\), this generates all \({}^{d,k}\mathcal{H}\) needed in B2.

(B9) Rapidity differential equations (generators) verified (s=0) [DC14; CR25, Eqs. 23–31]

With the aberration generator \(\hat Y_z = -i\big[\mu + (\mu^2-1)\partial_\mu\big]\) and its general-weight extension \({}^d\hat Y_z = \hat Y_z - i\mu(d-1)\) [CR25, Eqs. 24–25], the kernel obeys a closed first-order ODE system (written for \(s=0\), in this site’s convention, at argument \(+\beta(\eta)\)):

\[ \partial_\eta\, {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\eta) = (\ell'+d)\,C^m_{\ell'+1}\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell,\ell'+1} - (\ell'+1-d)\,C^m_{\ell'}\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell,\ell'-1}, \qquad {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(0) = \delta_{\ell\ell'} , \]

(general \(s,m\): [CR25, Eq. 28]). Applying transposition (B3) gives the equivalent row-shift form \(\partial_\eta {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'} = -(\ell+2-d)C^m_{\ell+1}{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell+1,\ell'} + (\ell+d-1)C^m_{\ell}{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}\). Promoting \(d \to d+\Oh\) yields the formal boost-operator ODE [CR25, Eq. 31], the workhorse for term-by-term Taylor generation (explicit second-order output: [CR25, Eqs. 33–35, A2]).

(B10) \(\ell\)-raising recursion verified (s=0; m=0,1) [CR26, Eq. B3; RC26, Eq. B3; HLC26, Eq. A.8]
\[ {}^{d}_{s}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = -\frac{1}{{}_sC^m_\ell}\, \frac{\gamma\,{}^{d}_{s}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(-\beta) - {}^{d-1}_{\;\;s}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(-\beta)}{p} \;-\; \frac{1}{{}_sC^m_\ell}\frac{sm}{\ell(\ell-1)}\, {}^{d}_{s}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(-\beta) \;-\; \frac{{}_sC^m_{\ell-1}}{{}_sC^m_\ell}\, {}^{d}_{s}\Kk^m_{\ell-2,\ell'}(-\beta). \]

This is the relation that “allows you to get higher index values of the operator from previous ones”: starting from the seeds (B11) it generates every kernel element — and hence, through B1, every boost-operator element — exactly. Note it couples Doppler weights \(d\) and \(d-1\).

(B11) Seeds / closed forms verified [CR26, Eqs. 9a, B1; RC26, Eqs. B4–B8]
\[ {}^{d}\Kk^0_{00}(-\beta) = \frac{(\gamma+p)^{1-d} - (\gamma-p)^{1-d}}{2(1-d)\,p}, \qquad {}^{d}\Kk^m_{mm}(-\beta) = \frac{1}{\gamma^{m+d}}\, {}_2F_1\!\Big(\tfrac{m+d}{2},\tfrac{m+d+1}{2};\tfrac{3}{2}+m;\beta^2\Big) \quad (s=0), \]

with \(s=\pm2\) seeds expressible through the integrals \(\mathcal{I}_k(p) = (p_+^k - p_-^k)/k\), \(p_\pm = \gamma \pm p\), which obey their own recursion \((k+1)\mathcal{I}_{k+1} = 2\gamma k\,\mathcal{I}_k - (k-1)\mathcal{I}_{k-1}\) [RC26, Eqs. B5–B8].

(B12) Structure facts literature [C11; DC14; CR25, §2.2, §4]

(i) \({}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(0) = \delta_{\ell\ell'}\); (ii) multipoles couple at order \(\beta^{|\ell-\ell'|}\), so at fixed order in \(\beta\) only a finite band of \(\ell\) mixes; (iii) at fixed order in \(\beta\), \({}^{d}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}\) is a polynomial in \(\Oh\); (iv) the kernel-ODE route (B9) is the numerically stable way to compute high-\(\ell\) elements [DC14, C11].

2  The Doppler operator

In any single-scattering computation the boost operator appears twice: once to enter the electron rest frame, once (with Doppler weight lowered to \(d=-1\) by the transformation of the optical depth, and a factor \(1/\gamma\) from the Møller speed) to return to the lab frame, with the Thomson cross-section projecting the intermediate field on rest-frame multipoles \(\ell' \in \{0,2\}\). The round-trip combination is the Doppler operator:

(D0) Definition verified conventions [RC26, Eq. 9; CR26, Eq. 3]
\[ \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) \;\equiv\; \frac{1}{\gamma}\; {}^{-1}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,-\beta)\;\; {}^{0}\Bo^m_{\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) \qquad (s=0;\ \text{spin-weighted version } {}_{ij}\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} \text{ in [RC26, Eq. 9]}). \]

On power laws (Lemma L1) it reduces to a product of scalar-weight kernels, \(\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta)\,\nu^q = \frac{1}{\gamma}\, {}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\beta)\; {}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)\,\nu^q\). The Thomson collision term for a single electron of speed \(\beta \parallel \hat z\) reads \(\frac{\dd n_{\ell m}}{\dd\tau}\big|_{\mathrm T} = \sum_{\ell''}\big\{\Do^m_{\ell 0 \ell''} + \tfrac{1}{10}\Do^m_{\ell 2\ell''} - \delta_{\ell\ell''}(1 + \beta\,[\dots])\big\}\, n_{\ell'' m}(\nu)\) [CR26, Eq. 3; CR25, Eq. 48], and the exact thermal SZ operator is \(\hat{\mathcal S}_{\rm th} = \big\langle \Do^0_{000} + \tfrac{1}{10}\Do^0_{020}\big\rangle_p - 1\) [CR26, Eqs. 4–7].

2.1 What was known

  • Closed forms for the monopole elements: \(\Do^0_{000} = \frac{(\gamma+p)^{3-2\Oh} + (\gamma-p)^{3-2\Oh} - 2\gamma}{4(2-\Oh)(1-\Oh)\,\gamma\, p^2}\) and a companion for \(\Do^0_{020}\) [CR26, Eqs. 10a,b]. verified
  • Sum rule over the intermediate index (from B4 + B6): \(\sum_{\ell'} \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \delta_{\ell\ell''} - \beta\big[C^m_{\ell''+1}\delta_{\ell,\ell''+1} + C^m_{\ell''}\delta_{\ell,\ell''-1}\big]\) [CR25, §4.2; RC26, Eqs. 10–11]. verified
  • Averaged tables \(\langle{}_{ij}\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\rangle\) to \(\mathcal O(p^4)\), obtained by brute-force assembly of boost-operator products [RC26, Eqs. 9a,b (App. B4)], and the exact SZ operator expansion to \(p^8\) [CR26, Eq. 11].
  • An unexplained observation: “all the required operators are linear combinations of powers of the diffusion operator \(\Dv\). In addition, significant structure is visible in the related coefficients, hinting at another way to simplify matters, a problem we will leave to future work.” [RC26, §2.3]

What was missing is what exists for the boost operator itself: recursion relations that generate the Doppler operator’s elements directly — without assembling products of boost operators — and an explanation of the observed \(\Dv\)-structure. Both are supplied below.

3  Core recursion relations and symmetries

The heart of the project: the relations that generate higher Doppler-operator indices from lower ones, and the symmetries that constrain them. Every result links to a complete derivation and was verified numerically at the \(10^{-27}\) level (exact identities) or against finite differences (ODEs); see Section 7. Labels N1–N16 are stable identifiers (order of discovery) and are grouped here by role, not by number.

★ How to generate any element from \(\Do_{000}\) — read this first generation guide verified end-to-end the guide →

Every Doppler operator is a product of two legs, \(\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q) = \tfrac{(-1)^{\ell'+\ell''}}{\gamma}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(q)\Kk_{\ell'\ell''}(q{-}1)\), each leg a single boost-operator column built from the seed by one three-term recurrence. Two practical payoffs: (a) how to raise each index — the outgoing \(\ell\) is the row of leg 1, the incoming \(\ell''\) the column of leg 2, the middle \(\ell'\) both at once (the only hard one); and (b) the easiest closed form of any \(\Do_{hkl}\) — build the small bracket table once, then multiply two entries (e.g. \(\Do_{101} = \tfrac{3}{4\gamma p^4}(\gamma J_{2+q}-J_{3+q})(\gamma J_{1+q}-J_q)\)). For a recurrence that stays entirely among Doppler operators (no kernels) use the pure-physical scheme (P0–P4), verified end-to-end (\(\Do_{303}\) from \(\Do_{000}\)). See the generation guide.

Lemma L1 — power-law reduction foundation proof →

Operator identities among \(\{\Bo, \Do\}\) hold iff the corresponding scalar-weight kernel identities hold for all real weights. Power laws \(\nu^q\) are eigenfunctions of \(\Oh\); at fixed order in \(\beta\) all operators are polynomials in \(\Oh\), so agreement at infinitely many \(q\) implies operator equality. This turns every derivation and every numerical verification below into a statement about ordinary functions.

(N1) Reflection symmetry new verified 7×10⁻²⁸ proof →
\[ \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta)\Big|_{\Oh} \;=\; \Do^m_{\ell''\ell'\ell}(\nu,\beta)\Big|_{\Oh \to 3-\Oh}. \]

Transposing the outer indices of the Doppler operator is equivalent to the substitution \(\Oh \to 3-\Oh\). A two-line consequence of the kernel transposition identity (B3). The fixed point of the reflection is \(\Oh = 3/2\), and the invariant combination is precisely \(\Oh(\Oh-3) = \Dv\).

Corollary N1a. Diagonal elements (\(\ell = \ell''\)) are invariant under \(\Oh\to3-\Oh\); since each Taylor coefficient in \(\beta\) is a polynomial in \(\Oh\), every diagonal element — and every thermal average of one — is a polynomial in the diffusion operator \(\Dv\) alone. This proves the empirical observation of [RC26, §2.3] and explains the “significant structure” in their coefficient tables: e.g. the \(\mathcal O(p^2)\) coefficient of \(\Do_{000}\) is \(\tfrac13\Dv\), of \(\Do_{222}\) is \(\tfrac{1}{21}(11\Dv - 54)\), etc.

(N2) Parity law new verified exactly proof →
\[ \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,-\beta) = (-1)^{\ell+\ell''}\, \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta). \]

The intermediate index drops out of the parity phase. Consequently the Taylor series of \(\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\) contains only powers \(p^k\) with \(k \equiv \ell+\ell'' \pmod 2\) — visible throughout the tables of Section 4.

(N3, N3′) Rapidity ODEs — the Doppler-operator generators new — central result verified (FD, 4×10⁻¹²) proof →
\[ \begin{aligned} \partial_\eta \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} \;=\; &-\big(\ell+3-\Oh\big)\,C^m_{\ell+1}\;\Do^m_{\ell+1,\,\ell',\,\ell''} \;+\;\big(\ell-2+\Oh\big)\,C^m_{\ell}\;\Do^m_{\ell-1,\,\ell',\,\ell''} \\[4pt] &-\big(\ell''+\Oh\big)\,C^m_{\ell''+1}\;\Do^m_{\ell,\,\ell',\,\ell''+1} \;+\;\big(\ell''+1-\Oh\big)\,C^m_{\ell''}\;\Do^m_{\ell,\,\ell',\,\ell''-1} \\[4pt] &-\;\beta\,\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} \end{aligned} \] \[ \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\big|_{\eta=0} = \delta_{\ell\ell'}\delta_{\ell'\ell''},\qquad \beta = \tanh\eta . \]

A closed, exact, first-order system at fixed intermediate index \(\ell'\): only the outer indices shift, no Doppler-weight companions appear, and all coefficients commute (they are functions of \(\Oh\)). This is the direct analog of the boost-operator ODE [CR25, Eq. 31] — but for the physically assembled round-trip operator, so Taylor tables of \(\Do\) are generated term by term without ever multiplying two boost operators. Note the manifest covariance under N1 (swap \(\ell\leftrightarrow\ell''\), \(\Oh\to3-\Oh\): the first pair of terms maps onto the second). It reproduces: the tables of [RC26, Eqs. 9a,b], the closed form [CR26, Eq. 10a] (symbolically, to \(\mathcal O(p^6)\)), and the exact SZ operator [CR26, Eq. 11] through \(\mathcal O(p^6)\) — see Section 4. This is the practical generator: it closes on physical operators and produces every Taylor series by fixing \(\ell'\) through the seed and iterating.

All three indices (N3′). Averaging N3 with its middle-index mirror gives a single flow that moves \(\ell'\) too — at the cost of the off-diagonal companion \(X_{\ell;a,b;\ell''} = \tfrac1\gamma\,{}^{-1}\Bo_{\ell a}(\nu,-\beta)\,{}^{0}\Bo_{b\ell''}(\nu,\beta)\) (the operator with unequal middle slots; \(\Do = X|_{a=b=\ell'}\)):

\[ \begin{aligned} \partial_\eta \Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \tfrac12\Big[ &-(\ell{+}3{-}\Oh)C_{\ell+1}\Do_{\ell+1,\ell',\ell''} + (\ell{-}2{+}\Oh)C_{\ell}\Do_{\ell-1,\ell',\ell''} -(\ell''{+}\Oh)C_{\ell''+1}\Do_{\ell,\ell',\ell''+1} + (\ell''{+}1{-}\Oh)C_{\ell''}\Do_{\ell,\ell',\ell''-1}\Big] \\ +\tfrac12\Big[ &+(\ell'{-}1{+}\Oh)C_{\ell'+1}X_{\ell;\ell'+1,\ell';\ell''} - (\ell'{+}2{-}\Oh)C_{\ell'}X_{\ell;\ell'-1,\ell';\ell''} +(\ell'{+}2{-}\Oh)C_{\ell'+1}X_{\ell;\ell',\ell'+1;\ell''} - (\ell'{-}1{+}\Oh)C_{\ell'}X_{\ell;\ell',\ell'-1;\ell''}\Big] - \beta\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}. \end{aligned} \]

The two brackets are equal (the exact cross-slot balance). N3′ is the form in which no index is a spectator; N3 (top) stays the workhorse because it closes on physical operators. Full derivation and verification: N3′ on the rapidity-ODE page.

(N4) Sum rule (re-derivation) known verified 10⁻¹⁷ proof →
\[ \sum_{\ell'} \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) = \delta_{\ell\ell''} - \beta\Big[C^m_{\ell''+1}\,\delta_{\ell,\ell''+1} + C^m_{\ell''}\,\delta_{\ell,\ell''-1}\Big]. \]

Included for completeness with a two-step proof from B4 + B6 (first given in [CR25, §4.2; RC26, Eqs. 10–11]). Physical content: summed over the scattering multipole, the round trip is the identity up to the \(\mathcal O(\beta)\) optical-depth dipole — photon number is conserved.

(N5, N6) Outer-index raising recursions on the weight lattice new verified 5×10⁻²⁷ proof →

Define the generalized Doppler operators \({}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} \equiv \frac{1}{\gamma}\,{}^{-1-j}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,-\beta)\,{}^{-k}\Bo^m_{\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta)\) (physical case \((j,k)=(0,0)\)). Then, for \(s=0\):

\[ \textbf{(N5)}\quad \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \frac{1}{C^m_{\ell}\,p}\Big[\gamma\,\Do^m_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''} - {}^{(1,0)}\Do^m_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''}\Big] - \frac{C^m_{\ell-1}}{C^m_{\ell}}\,\Do^m_{\ell-2,\ell'\ell''}, \] \[ \textbf{(N6)}\quad \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \frac{1}{C^m_{\ell''}\,p}\Big[\gamma\,\Do^m_{\ell\ell',\ell''-1} - {}^{(0,-1)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell',\ell''-1}\Big] - \frac{C^m_{\ell''-1}}{C^m_{\ell''}}\,\Do^m_{\ell\ell',\ell''-2}. \]

Higher outer indices from lower ones at fixed \(\ell'\) and fixed \(\beta\)-order, at the cost of one weight-shifted companion — mirroring how the boost-operator recursion B10 couples weights \(d\) and \(d-1\).
