N8 — Generating closed forms of higher-index Doppler operators from the \(\Do_{000}\) base case

This page gives every step of two independent derivations of the all-\(\beta\) closed forms of \(\Do_{101}\) and \(\Do_{020}\): Route 1 works at the kernel level (transposition B3 + \(\ell\)-raising B10 applied to the two factors of \(\Do\)); Route 2 works entirely at the Doppler-operator level using the new recursions N5/N6 and never touches a kernel. They agree exactly (harness check F11). Section 5 explains the structural division of labor: which of the new N-results can and cannot produce closed forms, and why. All formulas verified in verification/06_closed_forms.wl (F1–F11, at \(\beta\) up to \(0.7\)).

0  Universal functions and verified ingredients

Function family
\[ J_w \equiv \frac{(\gamma+p)^w - (\gamma-p)^w}{w} = \frac{2\sinh(w\eta)}{w},\qquad E_w \equiv (\gamma+p)^w + (\gamma-p)^w = 2\cosh(w\eta), \]

with \(\gamma\pm p = e^{\pm\eta}\), so \(J_{-w} = J_w\), \(J_0 = 2\eta\), and the product collapse \(J_{a+q}J_{b+q} = \frac{E_{a+b+2q} - E_{a-b}}{(a+q)(b+q)}\) (check F6, exact).

All work is on power laws \(\nu^q\) (Lemma L1: \(\Oh \to -q\); operator identities recovered at the end by \(q \to -\Oh\)), at \(s = 0\), \(m = 0\). The four verified ingredients:

(i) Seed [CR26, Eq. B1] C2
\[ S(d) \equiv {}^{d}\Kk_{00}(-\beta) = {}^{d}\Kk_{00}(+\beta) = \frac{J_{1-d}}{2p} \]

(the two arguments are equal by parity, \((-1)^{0+0} = 1\)).

(ii) \(\ell\)-raising B10 at \(s=0, m=0\) [CR26, Eq. B3] C4
\[ {}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = -\frac{1}{C_\ell\, p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell-1,\ell'}(-\beta) - {}^{d-1}\Kk_{\ell-1,\ell'}(-\beta)\Big] - \frac{C_{\ell-1}}{C_\ell}\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell-2,\ell'}(-\beta), \qquad C_\ell = \frac{\ell}{\sqrt{4\ell^2-1}}, \]

so \(C_1 = \tfrac{1}{\sqrt3}\), \(C_2 = \tfrac{2}{\sqrt{15}}\), \(C_1/C_2 = \tfrac{\sqrt5}{2}\), \(C_0 = 0\).

(iii) Parity C6 (iv) Transposition D2
\[ {}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = (-1)^{\ell+\ell'}\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta), \qquad\qquad {}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = {}^{2-d}\Kk_{\ell'\ell}(+\beta). \]

Target, on \(\nu^q\): \(\;\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q) = \frac{1}{\gamma}\,\underbrace{{}^{-1-q}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)}_{A}\; \underbrace{{}^{-q}\Kk_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)}_{B}\). Base case: \(A = B\)-type seeds give \(\Do_{000} = \frac{1}{\gamma}S(-1-q)S(-q) = \frac{J_{2+q}J_{1+q}}{4\gamma p^2}\) \(= \frac{E_{3+2q}-2\gamma}{4(2+q)(1+q)\gamma p^2}\) — [CR26, Eq. 10a] (checks F5, C11).

1  Route 1: kernel level (B10 + B3), all steps

Step 1.1 — the column element \({}^{d}\Kk_{10}(-\beta)\)

B10 with \((\ell,\ell') = (1,0)\); the \(\ell-2\) term is absent (\(C_0 = 0\)):

\[ {}^{d}\Kk_{10}(-\beta) = -\frac{1}{C_1 p}\big[\gamma S(d) - S(d-1)\big] = -\frac{\sqrt3}{p}\Big[\gamma\,\frac{J_{1-d}}{2p} - \frac{J_{2-d}}{2p}\Big] = -\frac{\sqrt3}{2p^2}\Big(\gamma J_{1-d} - J_{2-d}\Big). \tag{1.1} \]

check F1, 8×10⁻²⁹

Step 1.2 — the column element \({}^{d}\Kk_{20}(-\beta)\)

B10 with \((\ell,\ell') = (2,0)\):

\[ {}^{d}\Kk_{20}(-\beta) = -\frac{\sqrt{15}}{2p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{d}\Kk_{10}(-\beta) - {}^{d-1}\Kk_{10}(-\beta)\Big] - \frac{\sqrt5}{2}\,S(d). \]

Insert (1.1) at weights \(d\) and \(d-1\):

\[ \gamma\,{}^{d}\Kk_{10} - {}^{d-1}\Kk_{10} = -\frac{\sqrt3}{2p^2}\Big[\gamma^2 J_{1-d} - \gamma J_{2-d}\Big] + \frac{\sqrt3}{2p^2}\Big[\gamma J_{2-d} - J_{3-d}\Big] = -\frac{\sqrt3}{2p^2}\Big[\gamma^2 J_{1-d} - 2\gamma J_{2-d} + J_{3-d}\Big], \]

so, using \(S(d) = J_{1-d}/2p\) and \(3\gamma^2 - p^2 = 3(1+p^2) - p^2 = 3+2p^2\),

\[ {}^{d}\Kk_{20}(-\beta) = \frac{3\sqrt5}{4p^3}\Big[\gamma^2 J_{1-d} - 2\gamma J_{2-d} + J_{3-d}\Big] - \frac{\sqrt5}{4p^3}\,p^2 J_{1-d} = \frac{\sqrt5}{4p^3}\Big[(3+2p^2)J_{1-d} - 6\gamma J_{2-d} + 3J_{3-d}\Big]. \tag{1.2} \]

check F2, 2×10⁻²⁸ — equivalent to [CR26, Eq. 9b].

Step 1.3 — assembling \(\Do_{101}\)

Factor \(A = {}^{-1-q}\Kk_{10}(+\beta)\). Parity (iii) with \(\ell+\ell' = 1\) flips the sign of (1.1); at \(d = -1-q\) the indices are \(1-d = 2+q\), \(2-d = 3+q\):

\[ A = -\,{}^{-1-q}\Kk_{10}(-\beta) = +\frac{\sqrt3}{2p^2}\Big(\gamma J_{2+q} - J_{3+q}\Big). \tag{1.3a} \]

Factor \(B = {}^{-q}\Kk_{01}(-\beta)\). Transposition (iv) with \((\ell,\ell') = (0,1)\), \(d = -q\): \(B = {}^{2+q}\Kk_{10}(+\beta)\). Parity flips again: \(B = -\,{}^{2+q}\Kk_{10}(-\beta)\). Now (1.1) at \(d = 2+q\) has \(1-d = -1-q\), \(2-d = -q\), and evenness \(J_{-w} = J_w\) gives:

\[ B = +\frac{\sqrt3}{2p^2}\Big(\gamma J_{-1-q} - J_{-q}\Big) = \frac{\sqrt3}{2p^2}\Big(\gamma J_{1+q} - J_{q}\Big). \tag{1.3b} \]

Product.

\[ \boxed{\;\Do_{101}(q) = \frac{A\,B}{\gamma} = \frac{3}{4\gamma p^4}\, \Big(\gamma J_{2+q} - J_{3+q}\Big)\Big(\gamma J_{1+q} - J_{q}\Big)\;} \tag{1.3} \]

check F3 vs direct quadrature, β ∈ {0.3, 0.7}

Step 1.4 — assembling \(\Do_{020}\)

Factor \(B' = {}^{-q}\Kk_{20}(-\beta)\) is (1.2) at \(d = -q\) directly:

\[ B' = \frac{\sqrt5}{4p^3}\Big[(3+2p^2)J_{1+q} - 6\gamma J_{2+q} + 3J_{3+q}\Big]. \tag{1.4a} \]

Factor \(A' = {}^{-1-q}\Kk_{02}(+\beta)\). Transposition (iv) with \((\ell,\ell') = (2,0)\) reads \({}^{d}\Kk_{20}(-\beta) = {}^{2-d}\Kk_{02}(+\beta)\); matching \(2-d = -1-q\) gives \(d = 3+q\) (no parity step needed: \(\ell+\ell' = 2\) is even). So \(A' = {}^{3+q}\Kk_{20}(-\beta)\), i.e. (1.2) at \(d = 3+q\): indices \(1-d = -2-q\), \(2-d = -1-q\), \(3-d = -q\), and evenness:

\[ A' = \frac{\sqrt5}{4p^3}\Big[(3+2p^2)J_{2+q} - 6\gamma J_{1+q} + 3J_{q}\Big]. \tag{1.4b} \]

Product.

\[ \boxed{\;\Do_{020}(q) = \frac{A'B'}{\gamma} = \frac{5}{16\gamma p^6} \Big[(3+2p^2)J_{2+q} - 6\gamma J_{1+q} + 3J_{q}\Big] \Big[(3+2p^2)J_{1+q} - 6\gamma J_{2+q} + 3J_{3+q}\Big]\;} \tag{1.4} \]

check F4; series limit vs ODE table, F8

2  Route 2: the same forms from the new recursions N5/N6 alone

The outer-index recursions N5/N6 act directly on the Doppler operator, over the weight lattice \({}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \frac1\gamma\,{}^{-1-j}\Bo(\nu,-\beta)\,{}^{-k}\Bo(\nu,\beta)\). No kernel appears below.

Step 2.1 — lattice base cases (seed only)

\[ {}^{(j,k)}\Do_{000}(q) = \frac{1}{\gamma}\,S(-1-j-q)\,S(-k-q) = \frac{J_{2+j+q}\,J_{1+k+q}}{4\gamma p^2}. \tag{2.1} \]

check F9

Step 2.2 — raise the incoming index with N6

N6 at \((\ell,\ell',\ell'') = (0,0,1)\), valid at every lattice point \((j,k)\) (companion shifts \(k \to k-1\)); the \(C_0\) and \(\ell''-2\) terms vanish:

\[ {}^{(j,k)}\Do_{001} = \frac{1}{C_1 p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{000} - {}^{(j,k-1)}\Do_{000}\Big] = \frac{\sqrt3}{4\gamma p^3}\, J_{2+j+q}\Big(\gamma J_{1+k+q} - J_{k+q}\Big). \tag{2.2} \]

check F10

Step 2.3 — raise the outgoing index with N5

N5 at \((\ell,\ell',\ell'') = (1,0,1)\), lattice \((0,0)\) (companion shifts \(j \to j+1\)); the \(\ell-2\) term vanishes:

\[ \Do_{101} = \frac{1}{C_1 p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(0,0)}\Do_{001} - {}^{(1,0)}\Do_{001}\Big] = \frac{\sqrt3}{p}\cdot\frac{\sqrt3}{4\gamma p^3}\Big[\gamma J_{2+q} - J_{3+q}\Big]\Big(\gamma J_{1+q} - J_{q}\Big) = \frac{3}{4\gamma p^4}\Big(\gamma J_{2+q} - J_{3+q}\Big)\Big(\gamma J_{1+q} - J_{q}\Big), \]

identical to (1.3). check F11. This is the fully "novel-results" derivation: seed → (2.1) → N6 → N5, never leaving the Doppler operator.

3  Which N-results can produce closed forms — and why

  • N5/N6: yes, for the outer indices. Route 2 is the demonstration. This is no accident and no independent method: N5/N6 were derived from B10 + B3 (see their proof), so Routes 1 and 2 are the same climb performed at two levels — the kernel factors, or the assembled operator. Route 1 was presented first because it also covers the case N5/N6 cannot (next point). Both terminate after finitely many steps because each application only shifts a \(J\)-index by an integer and multiplies by \((\gamma, p)\)-rational factors — closure in the \(J\)-family is preserved at every step.
  • The intermediate index \(\ell'\): only at the kernel level. \(\ell'\) is a spectator in every \(\Do\)-level relation we have (N3, N5, N6; and the sum rule consumes it entirely). To reach \(\Do_{020}\) one must raise \(\ell'\) from 0 to 2 inside each factor — steps 1.2/1.4 — which is intrinsically a kernel-level move: the two factors share \(\ell'\), and a shift of a shared index cannot be expressed through shifted \(\Do\)-elements (whose two factors would shift together). This is the same obstruction, seen from another side, as the "no closure at \((j,k)=(0,0)\) alone" argument on the N5/N6 page. Physically one rarely needs it: Thomson scattering fixes \(\ell' \in \{0, 2\}\).
  • N3 (rapidity ODE): structurally cannot give closed forms. The ODE couples \((\ell,\ell'')\) to \((\ell\pm1,\ell'')\) and \((\ell,\ell''\pm1)\): the system is an infinite banded hierarchy. Truncating at any finite order in \(\eta\) yields exactly the polynomial Taylor tables (Section 4 of the main page) — polynomials in \(\Oh\) at each order, as you'd expect from a series generator. The closed forms of this page are the resummation of that tower: what N3 builds order by order, (1.3)/(1.4) state in finite terms. The two are complementary — we use N3 to generate/check series (F8) and N5/N6+seeds (or B10+B3) to resum them.
  • N1/N2 (symmetries): constrain, don't construct. Reflection acts on (1.3)/(1.4) by swapping the bracket factors (\(J_{2+q}\leftrightarrow J_{1+q}\), \(J_q \leftrightarrow J_{3+q}\) under \(q\to-3-q\)), which is a nontrivial consistency check (F7, exact) and halves the work (N6 follows from N5, \(A'\) from \(B'\)), but does not generate new elements.

4  Consistency checks and structure theorem

  • Reflection (N1). Under \(q \to -3-q\), evenness gives \(J_{2+q} \to J_{1+q}\) and \(J_{q} \to J_{3+q}\): in both worked forms the two brackets swap, so the diagonal elements are manifestly invariant (check F7, exact zero).
  • Parity (N2). Power counting in \(p\) reproduces \((-1)^{\ell+\ell''}\).
  • Series limit. Expanding \(\Do_{020}\) in \(p\) reproduces the N3-generated table entry \(\tfrac{1}{45}\Oh\big(\Oh^3-6\Oh^2+5\Oh+12\big)\,p^4 + \mathcal O(p^6)\) (check F8).
  • Removable poles. \(J_0 = 2\eta\); all closed forms are entire in \(\Oh\).
Structure theorem (every element is hyperbolic-rational)

For \(m = 0\) and any \((\ell,\ell',\ell'')\):

\[ \Do^0_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) \;=\; \frac{1}{\gamma\, p^{\,\ell+\ell''+2}} \sum_{\text{finite}} c_{ab}(\gamma, p^2)\; \frac{E_{a+b-2\Oh} - E_{a-b}}{(a-\Oh)(b-\Oh)}, \qquad E_{w} = 2\cosh(w\eta), \]

with integer pairs \((a,b)\) windowed by the indices and polynomial \(c_{ab}\) generated by the finite climb (either route). In rapidity variables everything is elementary — \(\cosh/\sinh\) of \((\text{integer}-2\Oh)\eta\) over polynomial denominators. Operator meaning: \((\gamma\pm p)^{-\Oh}n(\nu) = n\big((\gamma\pm p)\nu\big).\)
