N8 — Generating closed forms of higher-index Doppler operators from the \(\Do_{000}\) base case

This page derives the all-\(\beta\) closed forms of \(\Do_{101}\) and \(\Do_{020}\) entirely at the Doppler-operator level, by climbing the site's own index-raising relations from the base case: N6 for the incoming index, N5 for the outgoing index (Route 1), and N10 (M) for the middle index — no kernel is formed or multiplied anywhere in the derivation. An independent kernel-level climb (Route 2, B10 + B3 on the two factors) reproduces the same forms and serves as the cross-check (harness F11). Section 3 explains the structural division of labor: which relations can and cannot produce closed forms, and why. All formulas verified in verification/06_closed_forms.wl (F1–F11, at \(\beta\) up to \(0.7\)).

0  Universal functions and the base case

Function family
\[ J_w \equiv \frac{(\gamma+p)^w - (\gamma-p)^w}{w} = \frac{2\sinh(w\eta)}{w},\qquad E_w \equiv (\gamma+p)^w + (\gamma-p)^w = 2\cosh(w\eta), \]

with \(\gamma\pm p = e^{\pm\eta}\), so \(J_{-w} = J_w\), \(J_0 = 2\eta\), and the product collapse \(J_{a+q}J_{b+q} = \frac{E_{a+b+2q} - E_{a-b}}{(a+q)(b+q)}\) (check F6, exact).

All work is on power laws \(\nu^q\) (Lemma L1: \(\Oh \to -q\); operator identities recovered at the end by \(q \to -\Oh\)), at \(s = 0\), \(m = 0\). The input datum is the base case — the seed integral [CR26, Eq. B1] evaluated on the round trip (checks F5, C11), here on the full weight lattice \({}^{(j,k)}\Do = \frac1\gamma\,{}^{-1-j}\Bo(\nu,-\beta)\,{}^{-k}\Bo(\nu,\beta)\) since N5/N6 step through it:

\[ {}^{(j,k)}\Do_{000}(q) = \frac{J_{2+j+q}\,J_{1+k+q}}{4\gamma p^2}, \qquad\text{physical base case } (j{=}k{=}0):\quad \Do_{000} = \frac{J_{2+q}J_{1+q}}{4\gamma p^2} = \frac{E_{3+2q}-2\gamma}{4(2+q)(1+q)\gamma p^2} \ \ \text{[CR26, Eq. 10a]}. \tag{0.1} \]

check F9

1  Route 1: the Doppler-operator derivation (N6 → N5 → N10)

Step 1.1 — raise the incoming index with N6

N6 at \((\ell,\ell',\ell'') = (0,0,1)\), valid at every lattice point \((j,k)\) (companion shifts \(k \to k-1\)); the \(C_0\) and \(\ell''-2\) terms vanish:

\[ {}^{(j,k)}\Do_{001} = \frac{1}{C_1 p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{000} - {}^{(j,k-1)}\Do_{000}\Big] = \frac{\sqrt3}{4\gamma p^3}\, J_{2+j+q}\Big(\gamma J_{1+k+q} - J_{k+q}\Big), \qquad C_1 = \tfrac{1}{\sqrt3}. \tag{1.1} \]

check F10

Step 1.2 — raise the outgoing index with N5

N5 at \((\ell,\ell',\ell'') = (1,0,1)\), lattice \((0,0)\) (companion shifts \(j \to j+1\)); the \(\ell-2\) term vanishes:

\[ \Do_{101} = \frac{1}{C_1 p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(0,0)}\Do_{001} - {}^{(1,0)}\Do_{001}\Big], \] \[ \boxed{\;\Do_{101}(q) = \frac{3}{4\gamma p^4}\, \Big(\gamma J_{2+q} - J_{3+q}\Big)\Big(\gamma J_{1+q} - J_{q}\Big)\;} \tag{1.2} \]

checks F11 (assembly), F3 (vs direct quadrature, β ∈ {0.3, 0.7}). Seed → N6 → N5, never leaving the Doppler operator.

Step 1.3 — raise the middle index with N10 (M)

The middle index is shared by the two scattering legs, so its linear relation (M) of N10 lives on the extended family with off-diagonal intermediates \(X_{\ell;\ell_1',\ell_2';\ell''}\). In its double-step form (M\('_2\)), applied once from the base case (\(\ell' = 0 \to 2\); every step — W1–W5 — is written out on the middle-index page, which derives this element as its worked example), it produces

\[ \boxed{\;\Do_{020}(q) = \frac{5}{16\gamma p^6} \Big[(3+2p^2)J_{2+q} - 6\gamma J_{1+q} + 3J_{q}\Big] \Big[(3+2p^2)J_{1+q} - 6\gamma J_{2+q} + 3J_{3+q}\Big]\;} \tag{1.3} \]

checks M3 (the N10 climb reproduces this form), F4 (vs quadrature), F8 (series limit vs the N3 table). The companion-free alternative is the nonlinear pure-physical step P3 with the rank-one filling P2 (generation guide), verified end-to-end in harness 22/23.

Worked continuation — \(\Do_{202}\)

The same two outer ladders climbed once more (N6 at \(\ell''{:}\,1\to2\), then N5 at \(\ell{:}\,1\to2\), four lattice steps in all) give the next diagonal element — the harness performs exactly this climb (checks F12–F15):

\[ \Do_{202}(q) = \frac{5}{16\gamma p^6} \Big[(3+2p^2)J_{2+q} - 6\gamma J_{3+q} + 3J_{4+q}\Big] \Big[(3+2p^2)J_{1+q} - 6\gamma J_{q} + 3J_{q-1}\Big]. \tag{1.4} \]

Under \(q\to-3-q\) the two factors swap (\(J_{2+q}\leftrightarrow J_{1+q}\), \(J_{3+q}\leftrightarrow J_{q}\), \(J_{4+q}\leftrightarrow J_{q-1}\)): reflection-invariant, as N1a requires of a diagonal element.

2  Route 2: independent kernel-level cross-check (B10 + B3)

The same forms follow by climbing the aberration-kernel relations on the two factors of the defining product — the literature-level route, kept here as the independent cross-check of Route 1 (they agree exactly, F11). Ingredients (all verified): the seed \(S(d) \equiv {}^{d}\Kk_{00}(\pm\beta) = J_{1-d}/2p\) [CR26, Eq. B1] C2; \(\ell\)-raising B10 [CR26, Eq. B3] C4; parity \({}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = (-1)^{\ell+\ell'}{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta)\) C6; transposition \({}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = {}^{2-d}\Kk_{\ell'\ell}(+\beta)\) D2.

B10 from the seed gives the column elements \({}^{d}\Kk_{10}(-\beta) = -\tfrac{\sqrt3}{2p^2}(\gamma J_{1-d} - J_{2-d})\) (check F1) and \({}^{d}\Kk_{20}(-\beta) = \tfrac{\sqrt5}{4p^3}[(3{+}2p^2)J_{1-d} - 6\gamma J_{2-d} + 3J_{3-d}]\) (check F2; equivalent to [CR26, Eq. 9b]); parity and transposition convert them to the two factors of \(\Do_{101}\) and \(\Do_{020}\) at weights \(-1-q\) and \(-q\), and the products reproduce (1.2) and (1.3) line by line. The full step-by-step algebra of this route is preserved in 06_closed_forms.wl (F1–F4) and, in bracket-table form, in 24_bracket_table.wl / 25_tables_match.wl.

3  Which relations can produce closed forms — and why

  • N5/N6: the outer-index generators. Route 1 is the demonstration. Each application only shifts a \(J\)-index by an integer and multiplies by \((\gamma, p)\)-rational factors, so closure in the \(J\)-family is preserved at every step and the climb terminates. (N5/N6 were themselves derived from B10 + B3 — see their proof — which is why the kernel-level Route 2 is the same climb performed one level down.)
  • The middle index \(\ell'\): N10 (M), or P3. \(\ell'\) is a spectator in N3, N5 and N6, and a shift of the index shared by both scattering legs cannot close on diagonal \(\Do\)-elements alone. It closes on the extended family with off-diagonal intermediates \(X_{\ell;\ell_1',\ell_2';\ell''}\): the relation (M) raises \(\ell'\) at the operator level — Step 1.3 above (check M3). The pure-physical P3 removes the companions at the price of nonlinearity. Physically one rarely needs either: Thomson scattering fixes \(\ell' \in \{0, 2\}\).
  • N3 (rapidity ODE): structurally cannot give closed forms. The ODE couples \((\ell,\ell'')\) to \((\ell\pm1,\ell'')\) and \((\ell,\ell''\pm1)\): the system is an infinite banded hierarchy. Truncating at any finite order in \(\eta\) yields exactly the polynomial Taylor tables (Section 4 of the main page). The closed forms of this page are the resummation of that tower: what N3 builds order by order, (1.2)/(1.3) state in finite terms. The two are complementary — N3 generates/checks series (F8), N5/N6/N10 resum them.
  • N1/N2 (symmetries): constrain, don't construct. Reflection acts on (1.2)/(1.3) by swapping the two factors, a nontrivial consistency check (F7, exact) that also halves the work (N6 follows from N5, the second factor from the first), but does not generate new elements.

4  Consistency checks and structure theorem

  • Reflection (N1). Under \(q \to -3-q\), evenness gives \(J_{2+q} \to J_{1+q}\) and \(J_{q} \to J_{3+q}\): in all worked forms the two factors swap, so the diagonal elements are manifestly invariant (check F7, exact zero).
  • Parity (N2). Power counting in \(p\) reproduces \((-1)^{\ell+\ell''}\).
  • Series limit. Expanding \(\Do_{020}\) in \(p\) reproduces the N3-generated table entry \(\tfrac{1}{45}\Oh\big(\Oh^3-6\Oh^2+5\Oh+12\big)\,p^4 + \mathcal O(p^6)\) (check F8).
  • Removable poles. \(J_0 = 2\eta\); all closed forms are entire in \(\Oh\).
Structure theorem (every element is hyperbolic-rational)

For \(m = 0\) and any \((\ell,\ell',\ell'')\):

\[ \Do^0_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) \;=\; \frac{1}{\gamma\, p^{\,\ell+\ell''+2}} \sum_{\text{finite}} c_{ab}(\gamma, p^2)\; \frac{E_{a+b-2\Oh} - E_{a-b}}{(a-\Oh)(b-\Oh)}, \qquad E_{w} = 2\cosh(w\eta), \]

with integer pairs \((a,b)\) windowed by the indices and polynomial \(c_{ab}\) generated by the finite climb (either route). In rapidity variables everything is elementary — \(\cosh/\sinh\) of \((\text{integer}-2\Oh)\eta\) over polynomial denominators. Operator meaning: \((\gamma\pm p)^{-\Oh}n(\nu) = n\big((\gamma\pm p)\nu\big).\)
