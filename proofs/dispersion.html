N20 — The Compton dispersion relation: the marginal mode identified, exact flatness, and the thermal gap

N18 found that on the spectral line \(q = -\tfrac32 + ik\) the exact single-momentum Thomson matrix \[ T(k)_{\ell\ell''} \;=\; \Do_{\ell 0 \ell''}(q) + \tfrac1{10}\Do_{\ell 2 \ell''}(q) \;-\; \big[\delta_{\ell\ell''} - \beta\,\hat\mu_{\ell\ell''}\big], \qquad q = -\tfrac32 + ik, \] is Hermitian with a real damping spectrum and a persistent zero eigenvalue, and left two questions open: what is the marginal mode? and what does the dispersion relation \(\lambda_j(k)\) look like? Both are answered here, and the second answer is stronger than expected: there is no single-momentum dispersion at all. harness 28_dispersion.wl (D1–D5)

1  Theorem 1: the marginal mode is the boosted comoving equilibrium

Theorem (identification of the zero mode) new D1: 4×10⁻²² per row
\[ T(k)\,v = 0,\qquad v_\ell \;=\; 2\pi\!\int \bar Y_{\ell 0}(\mu)\,\big[\gamma(1-\beta\mu)\big]^{q}\,\dd\mu \quad\text{— the multipoles of } n(\nu,\mu) = (\nu')^{\,q},\ \ \nu' = \gamma\nu(1-\beta\mu). \]

Proof. \((\nu')^q\) is isotropic with the same power law in the electron rest frame. Thomson scattering is elastic there, and for any isotropic rest-frame field the gain integral of the (normalized) phase function equals the loss term frequency by frequency, so the field is exactly invariant; its boosted image is therefore annihilated by the lab-frame evolution operator, which is \(T(k)\) restricted to the Mellin mode. \(\blacksquare\)

Numerically the identification is sharp: per-row residuals \(\sim 4\times10^{-22}\) (quadrature floor) at \(k = 0.7, 1.3\) and \(\beta = 0.3, 0.6\), while the best wrong exponent (e.g. the shadow \(-3-q\) or \(q\pm1\)) fails at \(0.16\) (D1d). The zero eigenvalue persists at every \(k\) — a flat band (D2) — because the comoving equilibrium exists for every \(q\) on the line. Physically: the electron drags its own equilibrium radiation field with it; the marginal direction of Compton scattering is comoving equilibrium, mode by Mellin mode.

2  Theorem 2: the single-momentum dispersion relation is exactly flat

Theorem (isospectrality — unitary equivalence of all \(k\)-blocks) new D5: intertwining verified 10⁻²⁰
\[ T(k) \;=\; W_k\, T(0)\, W_k^{-1}, \qquad W_k = \text{multiplication by } \big[\gamma(1-\beta\mu)\big]^{ik} \quad (\text{unitary: } |[\gamma(1-\beta\mu)]^{ik}| = 1). \]

Proof. Thomson scattering is elastic in the electron rest frame, so the full evolution operator commutes with multiplication by any power of the rest-frame frequency, \((\nu')^{ik} = \nu^{ik}\,[\gamma(1-\beta\mu)]^{ik}\): the factor \((\nu'_{\rm in})^{ik}\) pulls through the gain integral because \(\nu'_{\rm out} = \nu'_{\rm in}\), and the loss term is multiplicative. The \(\nu^{ik}\) factor shifts the Mellin block \(0 \to k\); what remains is the angular unitary \(W_k\). \(\blacksquare\)

Consequences:

  • No dispersion: the damping spectrum \(\{\lambda_j\}\) is exactly \(k\)-independent — it depends only on \(\beta\). (The \(\sim5\times10^{-7}\) drift across \(k \in [0,4]\) in the truncated eigenvalue table is pure \(\ell\)-truncation; the intertwining identity itself holds at \(10^{-20}\), D5.)
  • The flat zero band is the \(W\)-orbit of the comoving equilibrium: \(v(q) = W_k\,v(-\tfrac32)\).
  • Multiple scattering solves once. \(e^{\tau T(k)} = W_k\,e^{\tau T(0)}\,W_k^{-1}\): the entire frequency structure of \(n_\tau = e^{\tau T(k)}n_0\) reduces to the single real symmetric matrix \(T(0)\) at \(q = -\tfrac32\), dressed by a known unitary.

3  Where dispersion really comes from: the thermal gap

Averaging over the relativistic Maxwellian (10 keV) preserves Hermiticity (each \(T\) is Hermitian; D4a at \(10^{-22}\)) but not the zero mode: the comoving equilibrium and the intertwiner \(W_k\) are \(p\)-dependent, so no common invariant survives. The top of the spectrum drops to a strictly negative spectral gap \(g(k)\) — the exact per-mode thermalization rate per unit optical depth, and the only source of \(k\)-dependence in the problem:

Per Mellin mode, multiple scattering through an isothermal cluster exponentiates exactly (independent scatterings): \(n_\tau = e^{\tau\langle T(k)\rangle}\,n_0\). The slow mode \(g(k)\) controls the approach to comptonized equilibrium; the fast angular branches (\(\approx -0.77, -0.85, \dots\)) isotropize the radiation long before its spectrum relaxes — the two-timescale structure of comptonization, here as an exact spectral statement.

4  Remarks

(i) In the N18 dictionary, Theorem 2 says the principal-series blocks of the Thomson operator at fixed \(p\) are all unitarily equivalent — the celestial line carries a single spectral problem, at the self-dual point \(q = -\tfrac32\) (where \(q = \bar q\)-reflection makes \(T(0)\) real symmetric). (ii) The identification theorem gives the kernel of the exact SZ evolution: distortions are orthogonal to the comoving-equilibrium family, and the SZ signal is generated precisely by the mismatch between the cluster-frame and electron-frame equilibria — averaged over \(p\), which is exactly the thermal gap of §3. (iii) Recoil (\(\mathcal O(x')\)) breaks rest-frame elasticity and hence exact flatness; by N14 its corrections move on the weight lattice, so the dispersion it induces is computable in this calculus — the natural next step toward an exact Kompaneets-type equation [HLC26].
