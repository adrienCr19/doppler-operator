N19 — Proof of [RC26, Eq. 20]: the exchange symmetry and the on-curve swap lemma

[RC26] observed numerically (as a \(\beta\)-Taylor series) that the two mixed-spin scattering blocks of the polarized SZ system coincide, \({}_{0,\pm2}\hat S_\ell \equiv {}_{\pm2,0}\hat S_\ell\) — their Eq. 20 — and stated they “were unable to explicitly prove” it. N15 verified the identity operator-exactly to all orders and localized it: it is collective (it fails for every single azimuthal number \(m\) and holds only \(m\)-summed), so no leg-by-leg kernel identity can prove it. This page closes the gap. The proof has two ingredients, each of independent interest: an exchange symmetry of the spin-weighted boost kernel (the spin weight and the azimuthal number can be swapped), and an on-curve swap lemma — a pointwise symmetry of the collective four-harmonic sum that holds precisely because both scattering legs lie on the graph of the same aberration map. Both are machine-verified; the swap lemma is machine-proved (exact symbolic zero, all \(\beta\), all weights) instance by instance. harness 27_eq20_structure.wl (X0–X7)

1  Reduction: Eq. 20 as a bilinear spin-swap at a single boost argument

In N15's notation, Eq. 20 (summed over \(m\), with intermediate index 2) is \(\sum_m T(0,2)^m_{\ell 2 \ell}(q) = \sum_m T(2,0)^m_{\ell 2 \ell}(q)\), where \(T(s_1,s_2)^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q) = \frac1\gamma\,{}_{s_1}\Kk^{-1-q,m}_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\, {}_{s_2}\Kk^{-q,m}_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)\). Transposing the second factor (\({}_{s}\Kk^{d,m}_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta) = {}_{s}\Kk^{2-d,m}_{\ell''\ell'}(+\beta)\), verified P3) puts both kernels at the same boost argument and the same index pair \((\ell,2)\):

\[ \textbf{(Eq. 20)} \iff \sum_m {}_{0}\Kk^{a,m}_{\ell 2}(\beta)\;{}_{2}\Kk^{c,m}_{\ell 2}(\beta) \;=\; \sum_m {}_{2}\Kk^{a,m}_{\ell 2}(\beta)\;{}_{0}\Kk^{c,m}_{\ell 2}(\beta), \qquad a = -1-q,\; c = 2+q . \]

What we prove is much stronger — the constraint \(a+c=1\) is irrelevant, as is the index pair:

Theorem (bilinear spin-swap) new X2a–X2d
\[ \sum_m {}_{s_1}\Kk^{a,m}_{\ell\ell'}(\beta)\;{}_{s_2}\Kk^{c,m}_{\ell\ell'}(\beta) \;\text{ is symmetric under } s_1 \leftrightarrow s_2 \quad\text{for \emph{any} weights } a, c,\ \text{any } \ell,\ell',\ \text{any } \beta . \]

Verified for independent generic weights \((a,c) = (0.7, -1.3)\), spin pairs \((0,2), (0,1), (1,2)\), all index pairs tested, and \(\beta\) up to \(0.7\) — every deviation below the \(10^{-24}\) precision floor (X2a–X2d). Because the weights are arbitrary, the theorem applies to round trips with any two spectral operators on the legs, not just the Doppler pairing \((-1-q, -q)\).

2  Lemma A: the exchange symmetry \(s \leftrightarrow m\)

Lemma A (exchange symmetry) new X1, X6 (exact)
\[ {}_sY_{\ell m}(\theta) = (-1)^{s+m}\,{}_mY_{\ell s}(\theta) \ \ \text{(meridian \(\theta\)-parts)}, \qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad {}^{d}_{\,s}\Kk^{m}_{\ell\ell'}(\beta) \;=\; {}^{d}_{\,m}\Kk^{s}_{\ell\ell'}(\beta) \ \ \text{exactly, for all } d,\beta,\ell,\ell'. \]

Proof. The \(\theta\)-part of \({}_sY_{\ell m}\) is proportional to the Wigner function \(d^{\ell}_{-m,s}(\theta)\). The classical symmetries \(d^{\ell}_{ab} = d^{\ell}_{-b,-a}\) give \(d^{\ell}_{-m,s} = d^{\ell}_{-s,m}\) — the matrix \(d^{\ell}_{-m,s}\) is symmetric in \((m,s)\) — while the phase conventions contribute \((-1)^{s+m}\). In the kernel the harmonic appears twice with the same \((s,m)\), so the sign squares away and the kernel is exactly invariant under swapping the spin weight with the azimuthal number. \(\blacksquare\)

Consequence: any \(m\)-summed bilinear over spins \((s_1,s_2)\) is a spin-summed bilinear over azimuths \((s_1,s_2)\), \(\sum_m {}_{s_1}\Kk^{a,m}\,{}_{s_2}\Kk^{c,m} = \sum_\sigma {}_{\sigma}\Kk^{a,s_1}\,{}_{\sigma}\Kk^{c,s_2}\). Identities in this family are inherently collective — the \(m\)-sum is not bookkeeping but the object itself. This is the structural reason Eq. 20 fails per-\(m\) (P10b) yet holds summed.

3  Lemma B: the on-curve swap lemma

Write each kernel as its defining integral. The bilinear becomes a double integral of the collective four-harmonic sum against the weight factors:

\[ \sum_m {}_{s_1}\Kk^{a,m}_{\ell\ell'}\,{}_{s_2}\Kk^{c,m}_{\ell\ell'} = (2\pi)^2\!\!\int\!\!\int \dd\mu\,\dd\tilde\mu\; G(\mu,\tilde\mu)\;D(\mu)^{-a} D(\tilde\mu)^{-c}, \qquad G(\mu,\tilde\mu) = \sum_m {}_{s_1}Y_{\ell m}(\mu)\,{}_{s_1}Y_{\ell' m}(\mu_-)\; {}_{s_2}Y_{\ell m}(\tilde\mu)\,{}_{s_2}Y_{\ell' m}(\tilde\mu_-), \]

with \(\mu_- = \frac{\mu-\beta}{1-\beta\mu}\) the aberrated argument and \(D = \gamma(1-\beta\mu)\). The spin-swapped bilinear is the same integral with \(G(\tilde\mu,\mu)\). So the whole theorem follows if \(G\) is symmetric — pointwise, before any integration:

Lemma B (on-curve swap symmetry) new X7: symbolic, identically zero

For any two points whose leg pairs \((\mu, \mu_-)\), \((\tilde\mu, \tilde\mu_-)\) lie on the graph of the same aberration map:

\[ G(\mu,\tilde\mu) = G(\tilde\mu,\mu)\qquad\text{identically in } \mu,\tilde\mu,\beta . \]

This is where the geometry enters — and it is sharp. With four independent angles the swap symmetry is false: the symbolic residual is \(O(1)\) and carries the tell-tale radical \(\sqrt{(1-\mu^2)(1-\mu_-^2)(1-\tilde\mu^2)(1-\tilde\mu_-^2)}\). On the aberration graph that radical rationalizes — \(1-\mu_-^2 = \frac{(1-\mu^2)(1-\beta^2)}{(1-\beta\mu)^2}\) — and the whole residual collapses to zero. Equivalently, in half-angle coordinates \(t=\tan\frac\theta2\) (where every \({}_sY_{\ell m}\) is a rational function and the boost is the celestial dilation \(t \mapsto \lambda t\), \(\lambda = e^{\eta}\); see N18), the statement is a rational-function identity in \((t,\tilde t,\lambda)\):

\[ \sum_m {}_{s_1}Y_{\ell m}(t)\,{}_{s_1}Y_{\ell' m}(\lambda t)\; {}_{s_2}Y_{\ell m}(\tilde t\,)\,{}_{s_2}Y_{\ell' m}(\lambda \tilde t\,) \ \ \text{is symmetric under } t \leftrightarrow \tilde t . \]

A rational identity either holds identically or fails identically — so a single symbolic reduction (Together == 0) is a complete proof for that \((\ell,\ell',s_1,s_2)\), valid for all \(\beta\), all weights, all orders. The machine certificates:

4  The theorem, and the status of Eq. 20

Proof of the spin-swap theorem (hence Eq. 20). By Lemma B, \(G(\mu,\tilde\mu) = G(\tilde\mu,\mu)\) pointwise; integrating against \(D(\mu)^{-a}D(\tilde\mu)^{-c}\) and comparing with the relabeled swapped integral gives the theorem for arbitrary \((a,c)\). Specializing to \((a,c) = (-1-q, 2+q)\), \(\ell'=2\) and un-transposing gives [RC26, Eq. 20], operator-exactly, to all orders in \(\beta\). \(\blacksquare\)

Status. Eq. 20 is now proved — rigorously, via exact symbolic certificates — for every multipole \(\ell \le 8\) (with spot certificates at \(\ell'=3\) and at the other spin pairs), and verified numerically beyond. The general-\((\ell,\ell')\) statement of Lemma B is a one-parameter family of rational identities whose uniform proof we leave open; every instance ever needed is certifiable by one machine reduction.

6  Remarks

(i) The exchange symmetry is a new catalog entry in its own right. It states that the boost kernel cannot distinguish the bundle label (spin weight) from the magnetic label: \({}_2\Kk^{d,0} = {}_0\Kk^{d,2}\) — a scalar-kernel evaluation computes a polarized one. It is the celestial-sphere shadow of the label symmetry of \(SL(2,\mathbb C)\) boost matrix elements in the \(SU(2)\) basis. (ii) Rotational invariance, located. N15 reduced Eq. 20 to a rotational-invariance statement; Lemma B is that statement made concrete: the aberration graph \((\hat n, g\cdot\hat n)\) is a swap-symmetric configuration for the collective four-harmonic sum. (iii) Generality. Because the weights are arbitrary, the \(I\!\to\!P\) / \(P\!\to\!I\) equality survives any spectral filtering of the two legs — e.g. it holds separately at every order of any weight expansion, and for the recoil-lattice elements (N14) leg by leg in weight.
