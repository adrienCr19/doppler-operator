E1 (extension) — The exact thermal SZ operator: what is closed, what provably cannot be, and the assembled result

The question. \(\langle\Do_{00}\rangle\) has an exact closed Bessel form — can \(\langle\Do_{02}\rangle\) be put in the same form, giving a fully closed \(\hat{\mathcal S}_{\rm th} = \langle\Do_{00}\rangle + \tfrac1{10}\langle\Do_{02}\rangle - 1\)?

1  What is closed, and what cannot be

Single momentum: everything is closed. \(\Do_{020}(q,p)\) is an elementary hyperbolic-rational function (N8, Eq. 1.4) — on par with \(\Do_{000}\).

Thermal average: the monopole is the only Bessel-closed channel — by theorem, not by lack of effort. The Maxwell–Jüttner measure \(\sinh^2\!\eta\cosh\eta\) exactly cancels the \(\operatorname{csch}^2\) of the monopole element (\(\sigma = \ell + 2\ell' + \ell'' = 0\)), leaving pure \(\cosh(a\eta)\) integrands \(\to K_a(z)\). The quadrupole channel has \(\sigma = 4\): a residual \(\operatorname{csch}^4\eta\) survives, and the companion paper's closure/rigidity theorem — which this project cross-validated symbolically (harness 08) and characterized structurally (N9: \((\partial_z^2-1)^2\) maps it onto a finite Bessel sum; the Kummer collapse of its Mellin kernel would require \(a_j = 0\), but physics fixes \(a_j = \text{integer} - 2\Oh \neq 0\)) — proves \(\langle\Do_{02}\rangle\) is not a finite Bessel combination, nor Meijer-G, nor Fox-H. A \(K\)-ratio form for \(\langle\Do_{02}\rangle\) does not exist.

2  The assembled exact operator

The exact \(\hat{\mathcal S}_{\rm th}\), all orders in \(\theta_{\rm e}\) X1: two independent routes agree at 2×10⁻²¹
\[ \hat{\mathcal S}_{\rm th}(\Oh,\theta_{\rm e}) \;=\; \underbrace{\frac{K_{3-2\Oh}(z) - K_1(z)}{2\theta_{\rm e}\,(2-\Oh)(1-\Oh)\,K_2(z)}}_{\textstyle \langle\Do_{00}\rangle \text{ — closed Bessel}} \;+\; \frac{1}{10}\,\underbrace{\frac{z}{K_2(z)}\sum_{j=1}^{8} c_j(\Oh)\,F(-2, a_j, z)}_{\textstyle \langle\Do_{02}\rangle \text{ — one new transcendent}} \;-\; 1, \qquad z = \tfrac{1}{\theta_{\rm e}}, \]

Before thermal averaging, both channels are in closed form. On \(\nu^q\) (operator form: \(q \to -\Oh\)), with \(J_w = 2\sinh(w\eta)/w\), \(\gamma = \cosh\eta\), \(p = \sinh\eta\):

\[ \Do_{000}(q) = \frac{J_{2+q}\,J_{1+q}}{4\gamma p^2}, \] \[ \Do_{020}(q) \;=\; \frac{5}{16\,\gamma p^6} \Big[3J_{q} - 6\gamma J_{1+q} + (3+2p^2)J_{2+q}\Big] \Big[(3+2p^2)J_{1+q} - 6\gamma J_{2+q} + 3J_{3+q}\Big] \]

(the N8 closed forms: \(\Do_{000}\) is the base case, \(\Do_{020}\) is produced by the middle-index climb N10 (M) from it — see the derivation; their two-factor structure is the rank-one property P2. It is only the Maxwellian average of \(\Do_{020}\) that leaves the Bessel class). The channel data are the cosh-resolution of this same closed form,

\[ \Do_{020}\big|_{\Oh}\;=\; \frac{\sum_{j=1}^{8} c_j(\Oh)\cosh(a_j\eta)}{\sinh^6\!\eta\,\cosh\eta}, \qquad \sum_j c_j = 0 \ \ \text{(exactly)}, \]

with the exact coefficients (harness 08, symbolic; \(D_1 \equiv (\Oh-1)(\Oh-2)\)):

\(a_j\)\(c_j(\Oh)\)
\(1\)\(-\dfrac{5\,(\Oh^4 - 6\Oh^3 + 17\Oh^2 - 24\Oh + 72)}{16\,\Oh\,(\Oh-1)^2(\Oh-2)^2(\Oh-3)}\)
\(3\)\(\dfrac{15\,(\Oh^2 - 3\Oh + 6)}{32\,D_1^2}\)
\(5\)\(-\dfrac{5}{32\,D_1}\)
\(1-2\Oh\)\(-\dfrac{5\,(2\Oh^3 - 12\Oh^2 + 13\Oh + 12)}{16\,\Oh\,D_1^2}\)

\(7-2\Oh\)\(\dfrac{5\,\Oh\,(\Oh+1)}{32\,(\Oh-1)(\Oh-2)^2(\Oh-3)}\)
\(1+2\Oh\)\(\dfrac{5\,(\Oh-3)(\Oh-4)}{32\,\Oh\,(\Oh-1)^2(\Oh-2)}\)

Averaging term by term over the Maxwellian turns each \(\cosh(a_j\eta)\operatorname{csch}^4\!\eta\) into \(F(-2,a_j,z)\) — which is how the \(c_j\) enter \(\hat{\mathcal S}_{\rm th}\) above. The \(F\)-part is then available through three exact, mutually verified representations:

  1. one quadrature (geometric convergence, any \(\theta_{\rm e}\)): \(\sum_j c_j F(-2,a_j,z) = \int_0^\infty e^{-z\cosh\eta}\,\big[\sum_j c_j\cosh(a_j\eta)\big]\operatorname{csch}^4\!\eta\;\dd\eta\) (regular integrand, by \(\sum c_j = 0\));
  2. the N9 ODE: the unique Bessel-rate-decaying solution of \((\partial_z^2-1)^2\big[\tfrac{K_2(z)}{z}\langle\Do_{02}\rangle\big] = \sum_j c_j K_{a_j}(z)\) (verified L3b, 3×10⁻²⁶);
  3. the convergent Bessel–Struve series and, for \(\theta_{\rm e} \lesssim 0.03\), the superasymptotic tower with error bound \(|R_N| \le |{\rm first\ omitted}|\).

Everything else in the F-sector is at hand: the recurrence calculus (N11), the μ-ladder, and the special values. In the precise sense of the rigidity theorem, this is the optimal closed form: elementary + Bessel + exactly one new special function, which cannot be removed.

Two lessons. At 50 keV the tower is visibly asymptotic: going from \(p^4\) to \(p^8\) makes the result worse at \(q = 0.5\) — the superasymptotic divergence in action — while the assembled exact operator is available at machine precision throughout. And the \(q = -4\) rows are exact by a pleasing mechanism: for integer spectral indices the single-momentum operator truncates (\(E\)-collapse: \(\Do_{000}(q{=}{-}4,p) = 1 + \tfrac43 p^2\) exactly), so the tower terminates — a useful anchor point for testing implementations.
