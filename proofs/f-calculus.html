N11 — The F-calculus: a complete recurrence toolkit for the thermal-average class

By N9, every thermally averaged Doppler operator is a finite \(c_j(\Oh)\)-combination of \(F(-\tfrac\sigma2, a_j, z)\). This page equips that function class with the same kind of toolkit that makes Bessel functions usable — ladder, recurrences, derivative relations, special values — so that the non-closed channels can be manipulated symbolically rather than only integrated numerically. The even sector needs an odd partner (exactly as \(K_\nu\) needs the Struve-type companion):

The function pair
\[ F(\mu,a,z) = \int_0^\infty e^{-z\cosh\eta}\cosh(a\eta)\,\sinh^{2\mu}\!\eta\;\dd\eta, \qquad L(\mu,a,z) = \int_0^\infty e^{-z\cosh\eta}\sinh(a\eta)\,\sinh^{2\mu}\!\eta\;\dd\eta, \]

convergent for \(\mu > -\tfrac12\) (all \(a\), \(z>0\)) and, for the physical \(\mu = -\sigma/2 \le -1\), as the \(\sum_j c_j = 0\)-regularized channel combinations. \(F\) is even in \(a\), \(L\) odd; \(L(\mu,0,z)=0\). The physical channels live on integer \(\mu \le 0\) with operator orders \(a_j = \mathrm{integer} - 2\Oh\); the calculus below naturally extends over the half-integer \(\mu\)-lattice.

The five relations

(FC1) μ-ladder FC1a/b
\[ \Big(\frac{\dd^2}{\dd z^2} - 1\Big) F(\mu,a,z) = F(\mu+1,a,z), \qquad \text{same for } L. \]

Proof: \(\partial_z^2\) brings down \(\cosh^2\eta = 1 + \sinh^2\eta\) in the integrand. (This is N9's ladder, now stated for both sectors.)

(FC2) a-recurrence, even step FC2a/b
\[ F(\mu,a+1,z) + F(\mu,a-1,z) = -2\,\partial_z F(\mu,a,z), \qquad \text{same for } L. \]

Proof: \(\cosh((a{+}1)\eta) + \cosh((a{-}1)\eta) = 2\cosh(a\eta)\cosh\eta\), and \(\cosh\eta\) in the integrand is \(-\partial_z\). At \(\mu = 0\) this is the classical \(K_{a+1} + K_{a-1} = -2K_a'\) — FC2 is its \(\mu\)-deformation.

(FC3) a-recurrence, odd step (couples the sectors) FC3a/b (exact)
\[ F(\mu,a+1,z) - F(\mu,a-1,z) = 2\,L(\mu+\tfrac12,a,z), \qquad L(\mu,a+1,z) - L(\mu,a-1,z) = 2\,F(\mu+\tfrac12,a,z). \]

Proof: \(\cosh((a{+}1)\eta) - \cosh((a{-}1)\eta) = 2\sinh(a\eta)\sinh\eta\) and its \(\sinh\)-analog; the extra \(\sinh\eta\) is a half-step in \(\mu\).

(FC4) z-relations (integration by parts) FC4, FC4', FC4b
\[ z\,F(\mu+1,a,z) = a\,L(\mu+\tfrac12,a,z) - (2\mu+1)\,\partial_z F(\mu,a,z) \qquad (\mu > -\tfrac12), \] \[ z\,L(\mu+1,a,z) = a\,F(\mu+\tfrac12,a,z) - (2\mu+1)\,\partial_z L(\mu,a,z) \qquad (\mu \ge -\tfrac12), \]

Proof: integrate \(\frac{\dd}{\dd\eta}\big[e^{-z\cosh\eta}\,{\cosh\atop\sinh}(a\eta)\,\sinh^{2\mu+1}\eta\big]\) over \((0,\infty)\); the three integrand terms give the three members. The boundary at \(\eta = 0\) vanishes for \(\mu > -\tfrac12\) (and always in the odd sector); at the edge case \(\mu = -\tfrac12\) of the even sector it contributes \(e^{-z}\), giving the boundary-corrected relation

\[ z\,F(\tfrac12,a,z) = a\,L(0,a,z) + e^{-z}. \tag{FC4b} \]
(FC5) Special values and the closed upper half FC5a–c, FC4'b (exact)
\[ F(0,a,z) = K_a(z), \qquad L(\tfrac12,a,z) = \frac{a}{z}K_a(z), \qquad F(\tfrac12,a,z) = \frac{a\,L(0,a,z) + e^{-z}}{z}, \] \[ F(1,a,z) = \Big(\frac{\dd^2}{\dd z^2}-1\Big)K_a(z) = \frac{a^2}{z^2}K_a(z) - \frac{K_a'(z)}{z} \quad\text{(Bessel ODE)}, \]

and by iterating FC1/FC4: every \(F(\mu,a,z)\) and \(L(\mu,a,z)\) with \(\mu > 0\) is a finite closed combination of \(K_a(z)\), \(L(0,a,z) \equiv L_a(z)\) and \(e^{-z}\) with rational-in-\((a,1/z)\) coefficients — integer \(\mu\) even sector needs only \(K_a\); half-integer rungs bring in \(L_a\) and \(e^{-z}\). The genuinely transcendental directions are \(L_a\) itself and the downward (\(\mu<0\)) rungs — the physical channels — for which the ladder runs the other way (N9) and closure is excluded by the rigidity theorem.

Channel-regularized form

For physical channels (\(\mu = -\sigma/2 \le -1\)) the relations hold for the regularized combinations. The \(\Do_{101}\) channel data referred to below are the cosh-resolution of the N8 closed form \(\Do_{101}(q) = \tfrac{3}{4\gamma p^4}(\gamma J_{2+q}-J_{3+q})(\gamma J_{1+q}-J_{q})\):

\[ \Do_{101}\big|_{\Oh} = \frac{\sum_{j=1}^{5} c_j(\Oh)\cosh(a_j\eta)}{\sinh^4\!\eta\,\cosh\eta},\qquad \sum_j c_j = 0, \]
\(a_j\)\(c_j(\Oh)\)
\(1\)\(\dfrac{3\,(\Oh^2-3\Oh+6)}{8\,\Oh(\Oh-1)(\Oh-2)(\Oh-3)}\)
\(3\)\(-\dfrac{3}{8\,\Oh(\Oh-3)}\)
\(1-2\Oh\)\(-\dfrac{3}{8\,\Oh(\Oh-1)}\)
\(3-2\Oh\)\(\dfrac{3\,(\Oh^2-3\Oh+1)}{4\,\Oh(\Oh-1)(\Oh-2)(\Oh-3)}\)
\(5-2\Oh\)\(-\dfrac{3}{8\,(\Oh-2)(\Oh-3)}\)

(exact, harness 08; the \(\sigma=4\) \(\langle\Do_{02}\rangle\) table is on the exact-tSZ page). Example (verified, FC6): FC4 at \(\mu = -1\) applied to this channel data:

\[ z\sum_j c_j\,K_{a_j}(z) \;=\; \sum_j c_j\,a_j\,L(-\tfrac12,a_j,z) \;+\; \partial_z \sum_j c_j F(-1,a_j,z), \]

where \(L(-\tfrac12,a,z)\) is individually convergent (\(\sinh(a\eta)/\sinh\eta \to a\)) and \(\sum_j c_j F(-1,a_j,z)\) is the regularized channel integral. Together with FC1 this expresses the \(z\)-derivative of any channel through one lower object and Bessel functions — useful for building ODE systems and for error control of the superasymptotic tower.
