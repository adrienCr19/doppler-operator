N16 — The kinematic (kSZ) dipole channel

The kinematic SZ effect is carried by the dipole scattering channel \(M_{11} = \langle\Do_{101}\rangle + \tfrac1{10}\langle\Do_{121}\rangle\) (companion paper, App. B.3). This page completes its exact ingredients — a new closed form for \(\Do_{121}\) and the all-orders \(M_{11}\) operator — and proves a sharp structural fact about where the physical kSZ signal can and cannot live.

1  Closed form for \(\Do_{121}\) (new)

Derivation — entirely by the site's index relations K1 (form, 4×10⁻²⁷ incl. β = 0.7), K6–K7 (each step)

Every step is an index-raising relation of the Doppler operator itself, starting from the \(\ell'=2\) plane base \(\Do_{020}(q) = \tfrac{5}{16\gamma p^6}A(q)B(q)\) (N10 climb; N8, eq. 1.3), where

\[ A(q) = (3{+}2p^2)J_{2+q} - 6\gamma J_{1+q} + 3J_{q},\qquad B(q) = (3{+}2p^2)J_{1+q} - 6\gamma J_{2+q} + 3J_{3+q}, \qquad B(-3-q) = A(q). \]

Step 1 (N5, outgoing index \(0\to1\)): on the weight lattice, exactly as in the N8 climb, \(\Do_{120} = \tfrac{\sqrt3}{p}\big[\gamma\,{}^{(0,0)}\Do_{020} - {}^{(1,0)}\Do_{020}\big]\), giving

\[ \Do_{120}(q) = \frac{5\sqrt3}{16\gamma p^7}\,W(q)\,B(q),\qquad W(q) \equiv 3\gamma J_{q} - 3(3{+}2p^2)J_{1+q} + \gamma(9{+}2p^2)J_{2+q} - (3{+}2p^2)J_{3+q}. \]

Step 2 (N1, reflection): \(\Do_{021}(q) = \Do_{120}(-3-q)\) — the other border of the plane, for free.

Step 3 (P2, rank-one filling): \(\Do_{121} = \Do_{120}\,\Do_{021}/\Do_{020}\); the \(B\)-factors cancel against \(\Do_{020}\) (\(B(q)B(-3-q) = A B = \tfrac{16\gamma p^6}{5}\Do_{020}\)), leaving

\[ \boxed{\;\Do_{121}(q) \;=\; \frac{15}{16\,\gamma p^8}\;W(q)\,W(-3-q)\;} \]

— manifestly reflection-invariant (the two \(W\)-factors swap under \(q\to-3-q\), as N1a requires), and the last of the four elements of the exact thermal+kinematic SZ system (\(\Do_{000}, \Do_{020}, \Do_{101}, \Do_{121}\)) in closed form. The harness additionally retains the historical kernel-column construction (K1a–c) as an independent cross-check of the same form.

2  The exact dipole-scattering operator \(M_{11}\)

Generated tower and exact evaluation K3 (O(p⁸) residual scaling 16.5), K4b (exact)
\[ \big\langle M_{11}\big\rangle(\theta_{\rm e}) = -\frac{3\,(9\Dv + 14)}{25}\,\theta_{\rm e} \;-\; \frac{3\,(26\Dv^2 - 7\Dv - 38)}{70}\,\theta_{\rm e}^2 \;+\; \frac{-580\Dv^3 + 1856\Dv^2 - 709\Dv + 810}{840}\,\theta_{\rm e}^3 \;+\; \mathcal O(\theta_{\rm e}^4), \]

generated from the N3 ODE tables + exact Maxwell–Jüttner moments; every coefficient is a polynomial in \(\Dv\) alone — verified exactly (K4b), as N1a requires for a sum of diagonal elements. The exact (all-\(\theta_{\rm e}\)) operator is one quadrature of the closed forms (check K3: series vs quadrature at \(\theta_{\rm e} = 0.01\), residual ratio \(16.5 \approx 16\), confirming \(\mathcal O(p^8)\) truncation and the exactness of the quadrature route).

3  A reflection obstruction: where the physical kSZ signal cannot live

Theorem (obstruction) new K2 (symbolic)

The physical first-order kinematic operator (equivalent forms: [CR26, Eq. 29]; the classical \(C_1\) of Nozawa et al. 1998 and SZpack’s \(D[0]\)),

\[ \hat S^{(1)}_{\rm kin} = -\Big[10\,x\partial_x + \tfrac{47}{5}x^2\partial_x^2 + \tfrac75 x^3\partial_x^3\Big] \;\widehat{=}\; -\,q\,\frac{7q^2 + 26q + 17}{5}, \]

is not of the form \(-q\,f(q^2{+}3q)\) (test: the \(q\)-coefficient \(26 \neq 3\times7\)). But by the reflection symmetry N1a, any assembly \(-q\,[\alpha M_{00} - a\Do_{101} - c\Do_{121}]\) of diagonal Doppler elements with an \(\Oh\)-prefactor is of that form. Hence:

\[ \boxed{\;\text{the kSZ signal cannot be assembled from diagonal Doppler elements alone.}\;} \]

The explicit gap of the naive diagonal assembly is \(-\tfrac{1}{25}q\,(q-1)(17q + 43)\) — with a root at \(q = 1\), the fingerprint of the missing ingredient: the kinematic dipole is not an elastic round trip of a weight-0 field, and its frame transformations (optical depth and temperature weights) break the self-adjointness that forces the \(\Dv\)-structure on the diagonal.

Follow-up completed (N21). The frame-exact assembly is done in N21, and it sharpens the diagnosis: the physical signal is carried by the outer off-diagonal monopole columns \(\Do_{\ell\ell'0}\) (weighted by the boosted-Maxwellian dipole moment and the flux factor \(\tau^*\)) — elements exempt from the reflection constraint because N1 maps them to different elements. The weight-lattice hypothesis originally floated here was the right instinct (escape the reflection-diagonal class) but the wrong mechanism: no lattice element is needed, only off-diagonal outer indices. The obstruction theorem itself stands unchanged.
