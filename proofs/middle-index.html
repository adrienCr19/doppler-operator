N10 — Middle-index raising: the \(\ell'\) recursion for the Doppler operator

Provenance. This relation answers the question: since the aberration kernel has recursions in both of its indices, the kernel-level moves that shift the scattering multipole \(\ell'\) must lift to the Doppler operator. They do. The refined statement of the earlier "spectator" discussion (N8, §3) is: no \(\ell'\)-recursion closes on the diagonal Doppler family alone — but on the extended family below it closes completely, exactly as the outer-index recursions N5/N6 close only on the weight lattice.

The \(\ell'\)-raising relation comes in four equivalent forms

All four raise the shared middle index and produce the same operator; pick by what you need:

The rest of this page derives the first two (the linear ladder and its diagonal product (M)); the pure-physical and continuous forms are on the linked pages. In the leg picture (\(\Do = \tfrac1\gamma\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(q)\Kk_{\ell'\ell''}(q{-}1)\)), raising \(\ell'\) means raising the column of leg 1 and the row of leg 2 together — which is why it is the only index that pulls in cross-terms.

0  What is being done, in words

The Doppler operator is a product of two kernels that share the scattering-multipole index:

\[ \Do_{\ell\,\ell'\,\ell''} \;\propto\; \underbrace{{}^{a}\Kk_{\ell\,\boxed{\ell'}}(+\beta)}_{\text{factor 1: } \ell' \text{ is its \emph{column}}}\;\; \underbrace{{}^{b}\Kk_{\boxed{\ell'}\,\ell''}(-\beta)}_{\text{factor 2: } \ell' \text{ is its \emph{row}}}. \]

The plan has three moves:

  1. Find a kernel recursion for each slot separately. The literature \(\ell\)-raising (B10) steps the row of a \(-\beta\) kernel — that is exactly what factor 2 needs (Ingredient B). Factor 1 needs its column stepped at \(+\beta\); no such relation is in the literature, but it follows by conjugating B10 with the transposition identity B3 (Ingredient A, Step 1 below). Each ingredient is a three-term recursion \(\ell' \to \ell'-1, \ell'-2\) that unavoidably drags in one weight-shifted kernel — the same weight companions as in N5/N6.
  2. Step both factors at once. A shift of the shared index must act on both factors simultaneously: solve Ingredient A for factor 1 at index \(\ell'\), Ingredient B for factor 2 at index \(\ell'\), and multiply the two right-hand sides. Each right-hand side has three terms, so the product has nine.
  3. Sort the nine products. Five of them have matched intermediate indices \((\ell'-1,\ell'-1)\) or \((\ell'-2,\ell'-2)\) — these reassemble into genuine Doppler-operator elements (on the weight lattice, because of the companions). The remaining cross products have mismatched indices \((\ell'-1,\ell'-2)\) and \((\ell'-2,\ell'-1)\) — these are the off-diagonal companions \(X\): still exact, still closed-form, but not diagonal. The result is relation (M).
Extended family and statement new verified ≤4×10⁻²⁷

Define (\(s=0\), any \(m\); on \(\nu^q\), with lattice weights \((j,k)\) as in N5/N6):

\[ {}^{(j,k)}X^m_{\ell;\,\ell_1',\ell_2';\,\ell''} \;\equiv\; \frac1\gamma\, {}^{-1-j}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell_1'}(\nu,-\beta)\;{}^{-k}\Bo^m_{\ell_2'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) \;\;\overset{\nu^q}{=}\;\; \frac1\gamma\,{}^{-1-j-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell_1'}(+\beta)\;{}^{-k-q}\Kk^m_{\ell_2'\ell''}(-\beta), \]

with the physical Doppler operator as diagonal slice, \(\Do = X|_{\ell_1'=\ell_2'=\ell'}\). Then (\(C_\ell \equiv C^m_\ell\), all at the same \(\beta\), \(q\) suppressed):

\[ \begin{aligned} {}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell,\ell',\ell''} \;=\; &\;\frac{1}{C_{\ell'}^2\,p^2}\Big[\gamma^2\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do - \gamma\,{}^{(j-1,k)}\Do - \gamma\,{}^{(j,k+1)}\Do + {}^{(j-1,k+1)}\Do\Big]_{\ell,\ell'-1,\ell''} \\[3pt] &+ \frac{C_{\ell'-1}}{C_{\ell'}^2\,p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}X_{\ell;\ell'-1,\ell'-2;\ell''} - {}^{(j-1,k)}X_{\ell;\ell'-1,\ell'-2;\ell''}\Big] \\[3pt] &+ \frac{C_{\ell'-1}}{C_{\ell'}^2\,p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}X_{\ell;\ell'-2,\ell'-1;\ell''} - {}^{(j,k+1)}X_{\ell;\ell'-2,\ell'-1;\ell''}\Big] \;+\; \frac{C_{\ell'-1}^2}{C_{\ell'}^2}\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell,\ell'-2,\ell''}\,. \end{aligned} \tag{M} \]

At the physical point \((j,k) = (0,0)\), for the quadrupole step \(\ell' = 2\) at \(m = 0\) (\(C_2^2 = \tfrac{4}{15}\), so \(\tfrac{1}{C_2^2} = \tfrac{15}{4}\), \(\tfrac{C_1}{C_2^2} = \tfrac{5\sqrt3}{4}\), \(\tfrac{C_1^2}{C_2^2} = \tfrac{5}{4}\)), the relation reads explicitly:

\[ \begin{aligned} \Do_{\ell,2,\ell''} \;=\; &\;\frac{15}{4p^2}\Big[\gamma^2\,{}^{(0,0)}\Do - \gamma\,{}^{(-1,0)}\Do - \gamma\,{}^{(0,1)}\Do + {}^{(-1,1)}\Do\Big]_{\ell,1,\ell''} \\[3pt] &+ \frac{5\sqrt3}{4p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(0,0)}X_{\ell;1,0;\ell''} - {}^{(-1,0)}X_{\ell;1,0;\ell''}\Big] + \frac{5\sqrt3}{4p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(0,0)}X_{\ell;0,1;\ell''} - {}^{(0,1)}X_{\ell;0,1;\ell''}\Big] \;+\; \frac{5}{4}\,\Do_{\ell,0,\ell''}\,. \end{aligned} \tag{M\('_2\)} \]

Higher middle indices from lower ones, at fixed outer indices — at the cost of the two off-diagonal companions and the same weight-lattice shifts as N5/N6.

1  Ingredient A: column recursion of the \(+\beta\) kernel

The verified \(\ell\)-raising B10 recursion acts on the row of a \(-\beta\) kernel. The first factor of \(X\) needs its column \(\ell_1'\) shifted, at argument \(+\beta\). Sandwich B10 in the transposition identity (B3): write \({}^{a}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = {}^{2-a}\Kk_{\ell'\ell}(-\beta)\), apply B10 to the row \(\ell'\) at weight \(d = 2-a\),

\[ {}^{2-a}\Kk_{\ell'\ell}(-\beta) = -\frac{1}{C_{\ell'}p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{2-a}\Kk_{\ell'-1,\ell}(-\beta) - {}^{1-a}\Kk_{\ell'-1,\ell}(-\beta)\Big] - \frac{C_{\ell'-1}}{C_{\ell'}}\,{}^{2-a}\Kk_{\ell'-2,\ell}(-\beta), \]

and transpose every term back (\({}^{2-a}\Kk_{\ell'-1,\ell}(-\beta) = {}^{a}\Kk_{\ell,\ell'-1}(+\beta)\), \({}^{1-a}\Kk_{\ell'-1,\ell}(-\beta) = {}^{a+1}\Kk_{\ell,\ell'-1}(+\beta)\), etc.):

\[ \boxed{\;{}^{a}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = -\frac{1}{C_{\ell'}p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{a}\Kk_{\ell,\ell'-1}(+\beta) - {}^{a+1}\Kk_{\ell,\ell'-1}(+\beta)\Big] - \frac{C_{\ell'-1}}{C_{\ell'}}\,{}^{a}\Kk_{\ell,\ell'-2}(+\beta)\;} \tag{A} \]

Note the weight companion is raised (\(a+1\)), the mirror image of B10's lowered one. check M1, 2×10⁻²⁷ (m = 0, 1)

2  Ingredient B and the one-sided recursions

The second factor \({}^{b}\Kk_{\ell_2'\ell''}(-\beta)\) needs its row shifted: that is B10 verbatim, with weight companion \(b-1\). Because \(X\) factorizes, multiplying (A) by the untouched second factor gives a three-term recursion in \(\ell_1'\) alone (weight companion \(j \to j-1\), since \(a = -1-j-q\)), and multiplying B10 by the untouched first factor gives one in \(\ell_2'\) alone (companion \(k \to k+1\), since \(b = -k-q\)). On the \(X\)-family the middle indices are therefore fully recursive, independently.

3  The diagonal step (M)

With \(F_1(\ell_1') = {}^{-1-j-q}\Kk_{\ell\ell_1'}(+\beta)\), \(F_2(\ell_2') = {}^{-k-q}\Kk_{\ell_2'\ell''}(-\beta)\), and \(F_1^{j-1}, F_2^{k+1}\) the weight companions:

\[ \gamma\,\Do^{(j,k)}_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = F_1(\ell')\,F_2(\ell') = \Big[-\tfrac{1}{C_{\ell'}p}\big(\gamma F_1 - F_1^{j-1}\big)(\ell'-1) - \tfrac{C_{\ell'-1}}{C_{\ell'}}F_1(\ell'-2)\Big] \Big[-\tfrac{1}{C_{\ell'}p}\big(\gamma F_2 - F_2^{k+1}\big)(\ell'-1) - \tfrac{C_{\ell'-1}}{C_{\ell'}}F_2(\ell'-2)\Big]. \]

Expanding the four products: the \((\ell'-1,\ell'-1)\) terms give the first bracket of (M) (four diagonal elements on the weight lattice); the \((\ell'-2,\ell'-2)\) term gives the last; and the two mixed products give the off-diagonal companions \(X_{\ell'-1,\ell'-2}\) and \(X_{\ell'-2,\ell'-1}\). Collecting yields (M). \(\blacksquare\)

4  Worked rederivation of \(\Do_{020}\) from (M\('_2\))

Set \((\ell,\ell'') = (0,0)\) in (M\('_2\)). Shorthand throughout: \(J_n \equiv J_{n+q}\) with \(J_w = \frac{(\gamma+p)^w-(\gamma-p)^w}{w}\), \(J_{-w} = J_w\).

W1 — the input elements in closed form

Only middle-index ≤ 1 elements are needed. Their factors, from the N8 machinery (Eq. 1.1 there, plus transposition):

\[ {}^{-1-j-q}\Kk_{00}(+\beta) = \frac{J_{2+j+q}}{2p},\qquad {}^{-k-q}\Kk_{00}(-\beta) = \frac{J_{1+k+q}}{2p} \qquad\text{(seeds)}, \] \[ {}^{a}\Kk_{01}(+\beta) \overset{\text{B3}}{=} {}^{2-a}\Kk_{10}(-\beta) \overset{(1.1)}{=} -\frac{\sqrt3}{2p^2}\big(\gamma J_{a-1} - J_{a}\big) \;\;\Rightarrow\;\; {}^{-1-j-q}\Kk_{01}(+\beta) = -\frac{\sqrt3}{2p^2}\big(\gamma J_{2+j+q} - J_{1+j+q}\big), \] \[ {}^{-k-q}\Kk_{10}(-\beta) \overset{(1.1)}{=} -\frac{\sqrt3}{2p^2}\big(\gamma J_{1+k+q} - J_{2+k+q}\big), \]

(evenness \(J_{-w}=J_w\) used in the third line). Hence the lattice-diagonal middle-index-1 elements (product of two minus signs):

\[ {}^{(j,k)}\Do_{010} = \frac{3}{4\gamma p^4}\, \big(\gamma J_{2+j+q} - J_{1+j+q}\big)\big(\gamma J_{1+k+q} - J_{2+k+q}\big), \]

and the cross elements:

\[ {}^{(j,k)}X_{0;1,0;0} = -\frac{\sqrt3}{4\gamma p^3}\big(\gamma J_{2+j+q} - J_{1+j+q}\big)\,J_{1+k+q}, \qquad {}^{(j,k)}X_{0;0,1;0} = -\frac{\sqrt3}{4\gamma p^3}\,J_{2+j+q}\,\big(\gamma J_{1+k+q} - J_{2+k+q}\big). \]

W2 — the lattice block factorizes

The four-element bracket of (M\('_2\)) involves \(j \in \{0,-1\}\) (touching only factor 1) and \(k \in \{0,1\}\) (touching only factor 2); it therefore factorizes into two independent combinations:

\[ \Big[\gamma^2\,{}^{(0,0)}\Do - \gamma\,{}^{(-1,0)}\Do - \gamma\,{}^{(0,1)}\Do + {}^{(-1,1)}\Do\Big]_{010} = \frac{3}{4\gamma p^4}\, \underbrace{\Big[\gamma\big(\gamma J_2 - J_1\big) - \big(\gamma J_1 - J_0\big)\Big]}_{\textstyle A}\; \underbrace{\Big[\gamma\big(\gamma J_1 - J_2\big) - \big(\gamma J_2 - J_3\big)\Big]}_{\textstyle B}, \] \[ A = \gamma^2 J_2 - 2\gamma J_1 + J_0, \qquad B = \gamma^2 J_1 - 2\gamma J_2 + J_3 . \]

W3 — the cross combinations produce the same \(A, B\)

\[ \gamma\,{}^{(0,0)}X_{0;1,0;0} - {}^{(-1,0)}X_{0;1,0;0} = -\frac{\sqrt3}{4\gamma p^3}\Big[\gamma\big(\gamma J_2 - J_1\big) - \big(\gamma J_1 - J_0\big)\Big] J_1 = -\frac{\sqrt3}{4\gamma p^3}\,A\,J_1, \] \[ \gamma\,{}^{(0,0)}X_{0;0,1;0} - {}^{(0,1)}X_{0;0,1;0} = -\frac{\sqrt3}{4\gamma p^3}\,J_2\,\Big[\gamma\big(\gamma J_1 - J_2\big) - \big(\gamma J_2 - J_3\big)\Big] = -\frac{\sqrt3}{4\gamma p^3}\,J_2\,B . \]

W4 — assembly

Insert W2, W3 and \(\Do_{000} = J_2 J_1/(4\gamma p^2)\) into (M\('_2\)):

\[ \Do_{020} = \frac{15}{4p^2}\cdot\frac{3AB}{4\gamma p^4} \;+\; \frac{5\sqrt3}{4p}\cdot\Big(\!-\frac{\sqrt3\,A J_1}{4\gamma p^3}\Big) \;+\; \frac{5\sqrt3}{4p}\cdot\Big(\!-\frac{\sqrt3\,J_2 B}{4\gamma p^3}\Big) \;+\; \frac{5}{4}\cdot\frac{J_2 J_1}{4\gamma p^2} \] \[ = \frac{5}{16\gamma p^6}\Big[\,9AB \;-\; 3p^2\big(A J_1 + J_2 B\big) \;+\; p^4 J_1 J_2\,\Big] \;=\; \frac{5}{16\gamma p^6}\,\big(3A - p^2 J_2\big)\big(3B - p^2 J_1\big), \]

where the last step is the factorization of the quadratic form (multiply out to check: \((3A - p^2J_2)(3B - p^2J_1) = 9AB - 3p^2 AJ_1 - 3p^2 J_2 B + p^4 J_1 J_2\)).

W5 — match with the N8 closed form

Finally, using \(3\gamma^2 - p^2 = 3(1+p^2) - p^2 = 3+2p^2\):

\[ 3A - p^2 J_2 = (3\gamma^2 - p^2)J_2 - 6\gamma J_1 + 3J_0 = (3+2p^2)J_2 - 6\gamma J_1 + 3J_0 \;=\; U, \] \[ 3B - p^2 J_1 = (3+2p^2)J_1 - 6\gamma J_2 + 3J_3 \;=\; V, \] \[ \boxed{\;\Do_{020}(q) = \frac{5}{16\gamma p^6}\, \Big[(3+2p^2)J_{2+q} - 6\gamma J_{1+q} + 3J_{q}\Big] \Big[(3+2p^2)J_{1+q} - 6\gamma J_{2+q} + 3J_{3+q}\Big]\;} \]

— identically the closed form obtained by Route 1 in N8, Eq. (1.4). \(\blacksquare\)

check M3: the full numeric assembly of (M) at \(\ell'=2\) equals the N8 form, deviation 0.0   check M5: W4–W5 verified as an exact polynomial identity in free symbols \(J_0,\dots,J_3\) modulo \(\gamma^2 = 1+p^2\)

Note how the pieces conspire: the lattice block alone gives \(9AB\), which is not the answer; the two off-diagonal cross terms and the \(\ell'-2\) term supply exactly \(-3p^2(AJ_1 + J_2B) + p^4J_1J_2\), completing the product \((3A - p^2J_2)(3B - p^2J_1)\). Dropping the \(X\)-companions would therefore give a wrong result — they are not a technical artifact but half of the answer.

5  Why the diagonal family alone cannot close

The cross terms are unavoidable: a shift of the shared index acts on both factors, and the product of two three-term recursions inevitably contains mixed \((\ell'-1,\ell'-2)\) pairings. One can also see it by counting: the diagonal family is a codimension-one slice of the \(X\)-family, and one-sided shifts do not preserve the slice. This is the precise content of the earlier "spectator" remark — \(\ell'\) is a spectator of N3/N5/N6, and its own recursion (M) lives on the extended family.
