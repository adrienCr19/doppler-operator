N10 — Middle-index raising: the \(\ell'\) recursion for the Doppler operator

Provenance. This relation answers the question: since the aberration kernel has recursions in both of its indices, the kernel-level moves that shift the scattering multipole \(\ell'\) must lift to the Doppler operator. They do. The refined statement of the earlier "spectator" discussion (N8, §3) is: no \(\ell'\)-recursion closes on the diagonal Doppler family alone — but on the extended family below it closes completely, exactly as the outer-index recursions N5/N6 close only on the weight lattice.

Extended family and statement new verified ≤4×10⁻²⁷

Define (\(s=0\), any \(m\); on \(\nu^q\), with lattice weights \((j,k)\) as in N5/N6):

\[ {}^{(j,k)}X^m_{\ell;\,\ell_1',\ell_2';\,\ell''} \;\equiv\; \frac1\gamma\, {}^{-1-j}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell_1'}(\nu,-\beta)\;{}^{-k}\Bo^m_{\ell_2'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) \;\;\overset{\nu^q}{=}\;\; \frac1\gamma\,{}^{-1-j-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell_1'}(+\beta)\;{}^{-k-q}\Kk^m_{\ell_2'\ell''}(-\beta), \]

with the physical Doppler operator as diagonal slice, \(\Do = X|_{\ell_1'=\ell_2'=\ell'}\). Then (\(C_\ell \equiv C^m_\ell\), all at the same \(\beta\), \(q\) suppressed):

\[ \begin{aligned} {}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell,\ell',\ell''} \;=\; &\;\frac{1}{C_{\ell'}^2\,p^2}\Big[\gamma^2\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do - \gamma\,{}^{(j-1,k)}\Do - \gamma\,{}^{(j,k+1)}\Do + {}^{(j-1,k+1)}\Do\Big]_{\ell,\ell'-1,\ell''} \\[3pt] &+ \frac{C_{\ell'-1}}{C_{\ell'}^2\,p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}X_{\ell;\ell'-1,\ell'-2;\ell''} - {}^{(j-1,k)}X_{\ell;\ell'-1,\ell'-2;\ell''}\Big] \\[3pt] &+ \frac{C_{\ell'-1}}{C_{\ell'}^2\,p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}X_{\ell;\ell'-2,\ell'-1;\ell''} - {}^{(j,k+1)}X_{\ell;\ell'-2,\ell'-1;\ell''}\Big] \;+\; \frac{C_{\ell'-1}^2}{C_{\ell'}^2}\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell,\ell'-2,\ell''}\,. \end{aligned} \tag{M} \]

Higher middle indices from lower ones, at fixed outer indices — at the cost of the two off-diagonal companions \(X_{\ell'-1,\ell'-2}\), \(X_{\ell'-2,\ell'-1}\) and the same weight-lattice shifts as N5/N6.

Step 1 — Ingredient A: column recursion of the \(+\beta\) kernel

The verified \(\ell\)-raising B10 recursion acts on the row of a \(-\beta\) kernel. The first factor of \(X\) needs its column \(\ell_1'\) shifted, at argument \(+\beta\). Sandwich B10 in the transposition identity (B3): write \({}^{a}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = {}^{2-a}\Kk_{\ell'\ell}(-\beta)\), apply B10 to the row \(\ell'\) at weight \(d = 2-a\),

\[ {}^{2-a}\Kk_{\ell'\ell}(-\beta) = -\frac{1}{C_{\ell'}p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{2-a}\Kk_{\ell'-1,\ell}(-\beta) - {}^{1-a}\Kk_{\ell'-1,\ell}(-\beta)\Big] - \frac{C_{\ell'-1}}{C_{\ell'}}\,{}^{2-a}\Kk_{\ell'-2,\ell}(-\beta), \]

and transpose every term back (\({}^{2-a}\Kk_{\ell'-1,\ell}(-\beta) = {}^{a}\Kk_{\ell,\ell'-1}(+\beta)\), \({}^{1-a}\Kk_{\ell'-1,\ell}(-\beta) = {}^{a+1}\Kk_{\ell,\ell'-1}(+\beta)\), etc.):

\[ \boxed{\;{}^{a}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = -\frac{1}{C_{\ell'}p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{a}\Kk_{\ell,\ell'-1}(+\beta) - {}^{a+1}\Kk_{\ell,\ell'-1}(+\beta)\Big] - \frac{C_{\ell'-1}}{C_{\ell'}}\,{}^{a}\Kk_{\ell,\ell'-2}(+\beta)\;} \tag{A} \]

Note the weight companion is raised (\(a+1\)), the mirror image of B10's lowered one. check M1, 2×10⁻²⁷ (m = 0, 1)

Step 2 — Ingredient B and the one-sided recursions

The second factor \({}^{b}\Kk_{\ell_2'\ell''}(-\beta)\) needs its row shifted: that is B10 verbatim, with weight companion \(b-1\). Because \(X\) factorizes, multiplying (A) by the untouched second factor gives a three-term recursion in \(\ell_1'\) alone (weight companion \(j \to j-1\), since \(a = -1-j-q\)), and multiplying B10 by the untouched first factor gives one in \(\ell_2'\) alone (companion \(k \to k+1\), since \(b = -k-q\)). On the \(X\)-family the middle indices are therefore fully recursive, independently — your intuition, made precise.

Step 3 — the diagonal step (M)

To move the physical (diagonal) index \(\ell' \to \ell'\) from \(\ell'-1, \ell'-2\), apply (A) to factor 1 and B10 to factor 2 simultaneously and multiply. With \(F_1(\ell_1') = {}^{-1-j-q}\Kk_{\ell\ell_1'}(+\beta)\), \(F_2(\ell_2') = {}^{-k-q}\Kk_{\ell_2'\ell''}(-\beta)\):

\[ \gamma\,\Do^{(j,k)}_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = F_1(\ell')\,F_2(\ell') = \Big[-\tfrac{1}{C_{\ell'}p}\big(\gamma F_1 - F_1^{j-1}\big)(\ell'-1) - \tfrac{C_{\ell'-1}}{C_{\ell'}}F_1(\ell'-2)\Big] \Big[-\tfrac{1}{C_{\ell'}p}\big(\gamma F_2 - F_2^{k+1}\big)(\ell'-1) - \tfrac{C_{\ell'-1}}{C_{\ell'}}F_2(\ell'-2)\Big]. \]

Expanding the four products: the \((\ell'-1,\ell'-1)\) terms give the first bracket of (M) (four diagonal elements on the weight lattice); the \((\ell'-2,\ell'-2)\) term gives the last; and the two mixed products give the off-diagonal companions \(X_{\ell'-1,\ell'-2}\) and \(X_{\ell'-2,\ell'-1}\). Collecting yields (M). \(\blacksquare\)

check M2: (M) verified for \(\ell' \le 3\), \(\ell,\ell'' \le 2\), \(m \in \{0,1\}\), \(q \in \{0.5, -0.7\}\) at 4×10⁻²⁷; M4: at shifted lattice points \((j,k)\)

Step 4 — closed-form demonstration: \(\Do_{020}\) by middle-index climbing

Every element of the extended family is a product of two kernel columns, hence \(J\)-closed (N8); (M) is therefore a closed-form-preserving generator in \(\ell'\). Applying (M) at \((\ell,\ell',\ell'') = (0,2,0)\) — inputs: \(\Do_{010}\)-family on the weight lattice, \(\Do_{000}\), and the four cross elements \(X_{0;1,0;0}, X_{0;0,1;0}\) at shifted weights, all elementary \(J\)-products — reproduces the \(\Do_{020}\) closed form of N8 exactly (check M3, deviation 0.0). So \(\Do_{020}\) can be reached from the \(\Do_{000}\) base case without ever descending to a bare kernel: N6/N5 climb the outer indices, (M) climbs the middle one.

Why the diagonal family alone cannot close

The cross terms are unavoidable: a shift of the shared index acts on both factors, and the product of two three-term recursions inevitably contains mixed \((\ell'-1,\ell'-2)\) pairings. One can also see it by counting: the diagonal family is a codimension-one slice of the \(X\)-family, and one-sided shifts do not preserve the slice. This is the precise content of the earlier "spectator" remark — \(\ell'\) is a spectator of N3/N5/N6, and its own recursion (M) lives on the extended family.
