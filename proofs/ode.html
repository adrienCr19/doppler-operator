N3 — The rapidity ODE: a closed generator for the Doppler operator

Statement

With \(\beta = \tanh\eta\), \(C^m_\ell = \sqrt{\smash{(\ell^2-m^2)}/(4\ell^2-1)}\), and all coefficients understood as commuting functions of \(\Oh\) (\(s=0\)):

\[ \partial_\eta \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = -(\ell+3-\Oh)\,C^m_{\ell+1}\,\Do^m_{\ell+1,\ell'\ell''} +(\ell-2+\Oh)\,C^m_{\ell}\,\Do^m_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''} -(\ell''+\Oh)\,C^m_{\ell''+1}\,\Do^m_{\ell,\ell',\ell''+1} +(\ell''+1-\Oh)\,C^m_{\ell''}\,\Do^m_{\ell,\ell',\ell''-1} -\beta\,\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}, \] \[ \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\big|_{\eta=0} = \delta_{\ell\ell'}\delta_{\ell'\ell''}. \]

The system is closed: only the outer indices \(\ell,\ell''\) shift, the intermediate (scattering) index \(\ell'\) is a spectator, and no Doppler-weight companions appear. It is the Doppler-operator analog of the boost-operator ODE [CR25, Eq. 31].

Ingredient 1 — kernel rapidity ODE, column form

In this site’s convention (established by harness check C9 against finite differences; cf. [DC14; CR25, Eq. 28]), the kernel at argument \(+\beta(\eta)\) obeys

\[ \partial_\eta\, {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = (\ell'+d)\,C^m_{\ell'+1}\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell,\ell'+1}(+\beta) - (\ell'+1-d)\,C^m_{\ell'}\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell,\ell'-1}(+\beta), \]

and consequently the kernel at \(-\beta(\eta)\) obeys the same relation with an overall minus sign (chain rule under \(\eta \to -\eta\)). No Doppler weight is shifted — this is what ultimately keeps the Doppler-operator system weight-closed.

Ingredient 2 — row form via transposition

The derivative of the first factor of \(\Do\) must shift its row index (the column is the spectator \(\ell'\)). Apply the transposition identity (B3, proved here) \({}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = {}^{2-d}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell}(-\beta)\), differentiate with Ingredient 1 (at \(-\beta\), weight \(2-d\), column index now \(\ell\)), and transpose each term back:

\[ \partial_\eta\, {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = -(\ell+2-d)\,C^m_{\ell+1}\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell+1,\ell'}(+\beta) + (\ell+d-1)\,C^m_{\ell}\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(+\beta). \]

Assembly

On power laws (Lemma L1), \(\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q,\eta) = \frac{1}{\gamma}\,F_1 F_2\) with \(F_1 = {}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta(\eta))\), \(F_2 = {}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta(\eta))\). Product rule with three pieces:

(a) \(\partial_\eta F_1\) by the row form with \(d = -1-q\): \(\;-(\ell+3+q)C^m_{\ell+1} F_1[\ell{+}1] + (\ell-2-q)C^m_{\ell} F_1[\ell{-}1]\).

(b) \(\partial_\eta F_2\) by the column form at \(-\beta\) with \(d = -q\): \(\;-(\ell''-q)C^m_{\ell''+1} F_2[\ell''{+}1] + (\ell''+1+q)C^m_{\ell''} F_2[\ell''{-}1]\).

(c) \(\partial_\eta \gamma^{-1} = -\sinh\eta/\cosh^2\eta = -\beta\,\gamma^{-1}\).

Each term in (a) multiplies the untouched \(F_2\) (and vice versa), so every product is again a \(\Do\)-element with one outer index shifted and the same \(\ell'\), \(q\), and weights. Collecting and replacing \(q \to -\Oh\) (Lemma L1(ii)) gives the boxed ODE. \(\blacksquare\)

Structural checks

  • Reflection covariance (N1). Under \(\ell \leftrightarrow \ell''\), \(\Oh\to3-\Oh\): \(-(\ell+3-\Oh) \to -(\ell''+\Oh)\) and \((\ell-2+\Oh) \to (\ell''+1-\Oh)\) — the first pair of terms maps exactly onto the second pair. The ODE is manifestly N1-covariant.
  • Parity (N2). Each application of the generator shifts \(\ell+\ell''\) by one and adds one power of \(\eta\); the \(-\beta\Do\) term adds even powers. Hence \(p^k\) appears only with \(k \equiv \ell+\ell'' \pmod 2\), re-deriving N2 order by order.
  • Sum rule (N4). Summing the boxed ODE over \(\ell'\) at fixed \((\ell,\ell'')\) and using the initial condition reproduces the \(\eta\)-derivative of the sum rule.
  • Weight-lattice generalization. For the generalized operators \({}^{(j,k)}\Do\) (see N5/N6) the identical derivation gives the same ODE with \((\ell+3-\Oh)\to(\ell+3+j-\Oh)\), \((\ell-2+\Oh)\to(\ell-2-j+\Oh)\), \((\ell''+\Oh)\to(\ell''+k+\Oh)\), \((\ell''+1-\Oh)\to(\ell''+1-k-\Oh)\): the whole lattice is generated by one formula.

Taylor-generation algorithm

c[0][l,l''] = δ(l,l') δ(l',l'')                      # seed at η = 0
c[k][l,l''] = (1/k) * (
    -(l+3+q)  C(l+1)  c[k-1][l+1,l'']
    +(l-2-q)  C(l)    c[k-1][l-1,l'']
    -(l''-q)  C(l''+1) c[k-1][l,l''+1]
    +(l''+1+q) C(l'')  c[k-1][l,l''-1]
    - Σ_{j≥1} tanh_j · c[k-1-j][l,l''] )              # tanh η = Σ_j tanh_j η^j
then  D(η) = Σ_k c[k] η^k ,   p-series via η = arcsinh(p),  q = -Ô.

Each order costs one sweep over the (finitely banded) \((\ell,\ell'')\) grid — no boost-operator products, no angular or momentum-space integrals. This is how all tables in Section 4 were produced (05_export_tables.wl).

Verification record

  • Direct FD check (N3, 02_doppler.wl): the boxed right-hand side matches the central finite difference \(\partial_\eta \Do\) of the quadrature-evaluated operator at \(\beta = 0.3\) to \(4\times10^{-12}\) (FD-limited), for \(q\in\{0.5,-0.7\}\), \(m\in\{0,1\}\), \(\ell,\ell',\ell''\le2\).
  • Series vs. quadrature (T1, T3, 03_tables.wl): order-4 series agree with direct integration at \(\beta=0.05\) to truncation accuracy; the truncation error scales as \(\eta^6\) for parity-even elements (ratio 64 under \(\beta\)-halving), as predicted by N2.
  • Against closed forms (T2): the generated \(\Do_{000}\) series equals the exact operator closed form [CR26, Eq. 10a] symbolically through \(\mathcal O(p^6)\).
  • Against the literature tables: reproduces the \(m\)-resolved entries of [RC26, Eq. 9a] (e.g. \(\Do_{000}, \Do_{101}, \Do_{020}\)) exactly, and the exact SZ operator [CR26, Eq. 11] through \(\mathcal O(p^6)\) (T4; \(p^2,p^4\) as symbolic identities, \(p^6\) symbolically generated and numerically cross-checked by Richardson extrapolation).
