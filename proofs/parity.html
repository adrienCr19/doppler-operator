N2 — Parity law \(\;\Do(-\beta) = (-1)^{\ell+\ell''}\Do(\beta)\)

Statement
\[ \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,-\beta) \;=\; (-1)^{\ell+\ell''}\; \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) \qquad (s=0). \]

Corollary. The expansion of \(\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\) in \(p=\gamma\beta\) contains only powers \(p^k\) with \(k \equiv \ell + \ell'' \pmod 2\). The intermediate index \(\ell'\) plays no role in the parity.

Step 1 — kernel parity

Claim ([CR25, Eq. 13b] in this site’s convention; harness check C6):

\[ {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = (-1)^{\ell+\ell'}\; {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta). \]

Proof. In the defining integral substitute \(\hat y' \to -\hat y'\) (i.e. \(\mu' \to -\mu'\), \(\phi' \to \phi'+\pi\)). Then \(1+\beta\mu' \to 1-\beta\mu'\) (the weight of the \(+\beta\) kernel), and the aberration map satisfies \(\mu_+[-\mu';\beta] = -\mu_-[\mu';\beta] = -\mu_+[\mu';-\beta]\), so the second harmonic’s argument also flips. Under the antipodal map \({}_sY_{\ell m}(-\hat y) = (-1)^{\ell}\,{}_{-s}Y_{\ell m}(\hat y)\); for \(s = 0\) each harmonic contributes \((-1)^{\ell}\), giving the phase \((-1)^{\ell+\ell'}\) (for general spin one picks up the \(s\to-s\) flip of [CR25, Eq. 13a/14a]). \(\blacksquare\)

Step 2 — assembly

On power laws, \(\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q,\beta) = \frac1\gamma\,{}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\,{}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)\). Sending \(\beta\to-\beta\) (note \(\gamma\) is even):

\[ \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q,-\beta) = \frac1\gamma\,\underbrace{{}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta)}_{(-1)^{\ell+\ell'}\,{}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)} \;\underbrace{{}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(+\beta)}_{(-1)^{\ell'+\ell''}\,{}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)} = (-1)^{\ell+2\ell'+\ell''}\,\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q,\beta), \]

and \((-1)^{2\ell'} = 1\): the intermediate index cancels. Lemma L1(ii) lifts the statement to operators. \(\blacksquare\)

Physical reading and verification

Reversing the electron’s velocity is a spatial reflection of the scattering geometry; the round trip picks up the product of the parities of the incoming (\(\ell''\)) and outgoing (\(\ell\)) multipoles only — the rest-frame projection multipole \(\ell'\) is traversed twice and its parity cancels. Practically: elements with \(\ell+\ell''\) even are even series in \(p\) (e.g. \(\Do_{000}, \Do_{101}, \Do_{222}\)), those with \(\ell+\ell''\) odd start at \(\mathcal O(p)\) (e.g. \(\Do_{122}, \Do_{001}\)) — exactly as seen in the tables of Section 4 and in [RC26, Eqs. 9a,b].

Numerics: check N2 of 02_doppler.wl — deviation exactly \(0.0\) (the quadrature evaluates identical integrands after the substitution). An indirect dynamical check: the truncation error of the order-4 ODE series for \(\Do_{101}\) scales as \(\eta^6\), not \(\eta^5\) (check T1 ratio \(64\), not \(32\)), because the \(\eta^5\) term is forbidden by this parity law.
