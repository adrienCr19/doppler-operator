N24 — The polarized ladders: spin-weighted N5/N6, and the ladder derivation of the polarized catalog

This page gives the polarized sector the same generation provenance as the scalar one: outer-index ladders acting on the mixed-spin Doppler operator itself, climbing from seed elements — so that every polarized closed form of N22 is derived, not merely evaluated. 1  Derivation

Exactly the scalar N5/N6 argument with the verified spin-weighted ingredients. For N5\(_s\): apply the spin-weighted kernel raising (RC26 Eq. B3 with our \(sY\)-convention \(sm\)-sign — verified as P5, harness 15) to leg 1 at \(+\beta\); the \(\beta\to-\beta\) bookkeeping flips the sign of the \(1/p\) bracket while the \(sm\)-term (no \(p\)) rides along unchanged, and the weight shift \(d\to d-1\) is the lattice move \(j\to j+1\). For N6\(_s\): transposition (P3, \({}_s\Kk^{d,m}_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta) = {}_s\Kk^{2-d,m}_{\ell''\ell'}(+\beta)\)) converts the incoming-column move of leg 2 into a row move at weight \(2+k+q\), whose shift is \(k\to k-1\) — the mirror ladder, again with the \(sm\)-term unchanged (Y0: \(\sigma_6 = +1\), verified across the grid, Y1b exact to precision). At \(m=0\) or \(s=0\) the \(sm\)-terms vanish and \({}_sC^m_\ell \to C_\ell\): the scalar N5/N6 are recovered as the special case.

2  Ladder provenance of the polarized catalog

Base cases: the seeds \(T(s_1,s_2)^m_{222}(q)\) — one seed-integral evaluation per spin/m combination (the polarized analog of \(\Do_{000}\); their \(J\)-forms are the N22 evaluations of the seed elements \({}_s\Kk^{d,m}_{22}\)). Climb: N6\(_s\) raises the incoming index (the \(\ell''{-}2\) tail vanishes for spin-2 legs, exactly as \(C_0 = 0\) does in the scalar climb), then N5\(_s\) raises the outgoing index through the lattice companions. Verified end-to-end:

  • Y2: seeds \(T^m_{222}\) → N6\(_s\) → N5\(_s\) → \(T^m_{323}\) equals direct quadrature (to precision floor) for \((s_1,s_2) = (2,2), (0,2)\), \(m = 0,1,2\) — the polarized analog of the scalar \(\Do_{303}\)-from-\(\Do_{000}\) climb;
  • Y3: the climbed values equal the N22 rationalization-lemma closed forms exactly — the two routes (ladder generation vs. direct \(J\)-reduction) cross-validate, the polarized analog of the scalar F11 check.

So the polarized sector now runs exactly like the scalar one: seeds → ladders → closed forms → exact thermal averages, with every closed form derived by index relations of the (mixed-spin) Doppler operator itself and the direct \(J\)-reduction demoted to a cross-check.

3  Remarks

(i) The physical polarized SZ system lives at fixed middle index \(\ell' = 2\) (Thomson), so the two outer ladders suffice for the whole catalog; a polarized middle-index relation would follow the scalar N10 pattern on the mixed-spin off-diagonal family, with \({}_sC^m\)-coefficients. (ii) By the exchange symmetry (N19, \({}_s\Kk^{d,m} = {}_m\Kk^{d,s}\)), N5\(_s\) at azimuthal number \(m\) is simultaneously a general-\(m\) ladder at spin \(m\) — one relation covers both generalizations of N23. (iii) Reflection (polarized N1/P7–P8, harness 15) maps the two ladders into each other, exactly as in the scalar case.
