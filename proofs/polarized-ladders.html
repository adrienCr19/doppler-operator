N24 — The polarized ladders: spin-weighted N5/N6, and the ladder derivation of the polarized catalog

This page gives the polarized sector the same generation provenance as the scalar one: outer-index ladders acting on the mixed-spin Doppler operator itself, climbing from seed elements — so that every polarized closed form of N22 is derived, not merely evaluated. On the weight lattice, \({}^{(j,k)}T(s_1,s_2)^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q) = \tfrac1\gamma\,{}_{s_1}\Kk^{-1-j-q,m}_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\, {}_{s_2}\Kk^{-k-q,m}_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)\), and with the spin-weighted coefficients \({}_s C^m_\ell = \sqrt{(\ell^2-m^2)(\ell^2-s^2)/(4\ell^2-1)}/\ell\): harness 31_polarized_ladders.wl (Y0–Y3)

[N5\(_s\)] outgoing ladder  ·  [N6\(_s\)] incoming ladder new Y1: ≤4×10⁻²³ over all spin pairs, m = 0–2
\[ {}^{(j,k)}T_{\ell} \;=\; \frac{1}{{}_{s_1}C^m_\ell\,p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}T_{\ell-1} - {}^{(j+1,k)}T_{\ell-1}\Big] \;+\; \frac{s_1 m}{\ell(\ell-1)\,{}_{s_1}C^m_\ell}\,{}^{(j,k)}T_{\ell-1} \;-\; \frac{{}_{s_1}C^m_{\ell-1}}{{}_{s_1}C^m_\ell}\,{}^{(j,k)}T_{\ell-2}, \] \[ {}^{(j,k)}T_{\ell''} \;=\; \frac{1}{{}_{s_2}C^m_{\ell''}\,p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}T_{\ell''-1} - {}^{(j,k-1)}T_{\ell''-1}\Big] \;+\; \frac{s_2 m}{\ell''(\ell''-1)\,{}_{s_2}C^m_{\ell''}}\,{}^{(j,k)}T_{\ell''-1} \;-\; \frac{{}_{s_2}C^m_{\ell''-1}}{{}_{s_2}C^m_{\ell''}}\,{}^{(j,k)}T_{\ell''-2} \]

(indices not shown are spectators; each ladder touches only its own leg's spin and the \(sm\)-term signs are both positive in this site's conventions — pinned numerically, Y0).

1  Derivation

Exactly the scalar N5/N6 argument with the verified spin-weighted ingredients. For N5\(_s\): apply the spin-weighted kernel raising (RC26 Eq. B3 with our \(sY\)-convention \(sm\)-sign — verified as P5, harness 15) to leg 1 at \(+\beta\); the \(\beta\to-\beta\) bookkeeping flips the sign of the \(1/p\) bracket while the \(sm\)-term (no \(p\)) rides along unchanged, and the weight shift \(d\to d-1\) is the lattice move \(j\to j+1\). For N6\(_s\): transposition (P3, \({}_s\Kk^{d,m}_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta) = {}_s\Kk^{2-d,m}_{\ell''\ell'}(+\beta)\)) converts the incoming-column move of leg 2 into a row move at weight \(2+k+q\), whose shift is \(k\to k-1\) — the mirror ladder, again with the \(sm\)-term unchanged (Y0: \(\sigma_6 = +1\), verified across the grid, Y1b exact to precision). At \(m=0\) or \(s=0\) the \(sm\)-terms vanish and \({}_sC^m_\ell \to C_\ell\): the scalar N5/N6 are recovered as the special case.

2  Ladder provenance of the polarized catalog

Base cases: the seeds \(T(s_1,s_2)^m_{222}(q)\) — one seed-integral evaluation per spin/m combination (the polarized analog of \(\Do_{000}\); their \(J\)-forms are the N22 evaluations of the seed elements \({}_s\Kk^{d,m}_{22}\)). Climb: N6\(_s\) raises the incoming index (the \(\ell''{-}2\) tail vanishes for spin-2 legs, exactly as \(C_0 = 0\) does in the scalar climb), then N5\(_s\) raises the outgoing index through the lattice companions. Verified end-to-end:

  • Y2: seeds \(T^m_{222}\) → N6\(_s\) → N5\(_s\) → \(T^m_{323}\) equals direct quadrature (to precision floor) for \((s_1,s_2) = (2,2), (0,2)\), \(m = 0,1,2\) — the polarized analog of the scalar \(\Do_{303}\)-from-\(\Do_{000}\) climb;
  • Y3: the climbed values equal the N22 rationalization-lemma closed forms exactly — the two routes (ladder generation vs. direct \(J\)-reduction) cross-validate, the polarized analog of the scalar F11 check.

So the polarized sector now runs exactly like the scalar one: seeds → ladders → closed forms → exact thermal averages, with every closed form derived by index relations of the (mixed-spin) Doppler operator itself and the direct \(J\)-reduction demoted to a cross-check.

3  The polarized middle-index relation (M\(_s\))

The remaining index. On the mixed-spin off-diagonal family \({}^{(j,k)}X(s_1,s_2)^m_{\ell;\,\ell_1',\ell_2';\,\ell''} = \tfrac1\gamma\, {}_{s_1}\Kk^{-1-j-q,m}_{\ell\ell_1'}(+\beta)\,{}_{s_2}\Kk^{-k-q,m}_{\ell_2'\ell''}(-\beta)\) (physical operator = diagonal slice), the two slot ingredients are the spin-weighted analogs of N10's: the leg-1 column recursion at \(+\beta\) (spin-weighted B10 conjugated with transposition P3; weight companion raised, \(j\to j-1\)) and the leg-2 row recursion at \(-\beta\) (P5 directly; weight companion lowered, \(k\to k+1\)) — each acquiring the \(sm\)-term of its own leg, verified separately (V1/V2, signs again both positive). Solving each for the \(\ell'\)-element and multiplying gives, with \(C_i \equiv {}_{s_i}C^m_{\ell'}\), \(C_i' \equiv {}_{s_i}C^m_{\ell'-1}\) and the \(sm\)-dressed \(\Gamma_i \equiv \gamma - \dfrac{s_i m\, p}{\ell'(\ell'-1)}\):

[M\(_s\)] polarized middle-index relation new V3: exact to precision, all spin pairs, m = 0–2, β up to 0.7
\[ \begin{aligned} {}^{(j,k)}T^m_{\ell,\ell',\ell''} \;=\; &\;\frac{1}{C_1 C_2\,p^2}\Big[\Gamma_1\Gamma_2\,{}^{(j,k)}T - \Gamma_1\,{}^{(j,k+1)}T - \Gamma_2\,{}^{(j-1,k)}T + {}^{(j-1,k+1)}T\Big]_{\ell,\ell'-1,\ell''} \\[3pt] &+ \frac{C_2'}{C_1 C_2\,p}\Big[\Gamma_1\,{}^{(j,k)}X - {}^{(j-1,k)}X\Big]_{\ell;\,\ell'-1,\ell'-2;\,\ell''} + \frac{C_1'}{C_1 C_2\,p}\Big[\Gamma_2\,{}^{(j,k)}X - {}^{(j,k+1)}X\Big]_{\ell;\,\ell'-2,\ell'-1;\,\ell''} \\[3pt] &+ \frac{C_1' C_2'}{C_1 C_2}\,{}^{(j,k)}T_{\ell,\ell'-2,\ell''}\,. \end{aligned} \]

At \(s_1 = s_2 = 0\): \(\Gamma_i \to \gamma\), \(C_i \to C^m_{\ell'}\), and the scalar (M) of N10 is recovered term for term. And a structural bonus: for spin-2 legs the coefficient \({}_2C^m_{\ell'-1}\) vanishes at \(\ell'-1 = 2\) (\(\ell^2 - s^2 = 0\) — the spin-weighted analog of \(C_0 = 0\)), so the first polarized middle step \(\ell' = 2 \to 3\) collapses: all three tail terms drop and \(T(2,2)^m_{\ell,3,\ell''}\) is generated from the lattice \(T^m_{\ell,2,\ell''}\)-family alone, no off-diagonal companions at all (V4). Off-diagonal \(X\)'s first enter at \(\ell' = 3 \to 4\), exactly as \(C_0 = 0\) delays them in the scalar climb.

4  Remarks

(i) The physical polarized SZ system lives at fixed middle index \(\ell' = 2\) (Thomson), so the two outer ladders suffice for its catalog; (M\(_s\)) completes the relation set for any phase function — every index of the mixed-spin operator now has its own verified raising relation. (ii) By the exchange symmetry (N19, \({}_s\Kk^{d,m} = {}_m\Kk^{d,s}\)), N5\(_s\) at azimuthal number \(m\) is simultaneously a general-\(m\) ladder at spin \(m\) — one relation covers both generalizations of N23. (iii) Reflection (polarized N1/P7–P8, harness 15) maps the two ladders into each other, exactly as in the scalar case.
