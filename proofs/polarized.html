N15 — The polarized sector: spin-weighted reflections, and the status of [RC26, Eq. 20]

Polarization promotes the photon field to spin-weighted components (\(s = 0, \pm2\)) and the Doppler operator to mixed-spin round trips: the return leg carries the spin of the observed component, the ingoing leg the spin of the source component. This page extends the reflection framework to that sector and settles — at the operator level, to all orders in \(\beta\) — the identity that [RC26] stated they “were unable to explicitly prove” (their Eq. 20).

Update (July 2026): the missing proof is now supplied N19

The rotational-invariance lemma this page reduces Eq. 20 to has been proved: the kernel exchange symmetry \({}_s\Kk^{d,m} = {}_m\Kk^{d,s}\) shows the identity is inherently collective in \(m\), and the on-curve swap lemma (a pointwise symmetry of the four-harmonic sum on the aberration graph, machine-proved symbolically) yields Eq. 20 for arbitrary leg weights, all orders in \(\beta\), certified for every \(\ell \le 8\) (numerically beyond). See N19 — proof of Eq. 20.

0  Setting and conventions

Spin-weighted kernels in this site’s convention (harmonics \({}_sY_{\ell m}\) in the standard phase, measured recurrence \(\mu\,{}_sY_{\ell m} = {}_sC^m_{\ell+1}\,{}_sY_{\ell+1,m} - \tfrac{sm}{\ell(\ell+1)}{}_sY_{\ell m} + {}_sC^m_{\ell}\,{}_sY_{\ell-1,m}\)):

\[ {}_s\Kk^{d,m}_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = \int \dd^2\hat y'\, \frac{{}_sY^*_{\ell m}(\hat y')\;{}_sY_{\ell' m}(\hat y_+[\hat y'])}{[\gamma(1+\beta\mu')]^d}, \qquad T(s_1,s_2)^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q) \equiv \frac1\gamma\,{}_{s_1}\Kk^{-1-q,m}_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\; {}_{s_2}\Kk^{-q,m}_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta), \]

the mixed-spin Doppler operator on \(\nu^q\) (physical polarized case: \(s_i \in \{0,\pm2\}\), intermediate \(\ell' = 2\); [RC26, Eqs. 8–9, 18]). foundations P1–P6, P12: orthonormality, \(\delta\)-limit, transposition (same \(s\), same form as B3), parity (now flipping \(s \to -s\)), the \(\ell\)-raising B10 and the kernel rapidity ODE with their \(sm\)-terms, and the RC26 B5 seed integral, all verified at \(s = 2\), \(m \in \{0,1,2\}\).

Convention dictionary. The papers build kernels from \({}_{-s}Y\); ours use \({}_sY\). Consequently every explicit \(sm\)-term flips sign relative to the printed [RC26, Eq. B3] and [CR25, Eq. 28] (their own footnote flags the same flip). Parity (P4) converts between the two exactly: \({}_s\Kk^{d}(-\beta) = (-1)^{\ell+\ell'}\,{}_{-s}\Kk^{d}(+\beta)\). All identities below are convention-robust (diagonals and \(\pm s\)-summed objects are invariant under the rephasing).

1  The three polarized reflection theorems

(N15a) Polarized N1 — same spin P7 (exact)
\[ {}_s\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\Oh) = {}_s\Do^m_{\ell''\ell'\ell}(3-\Oh) \]

Proof: identical to N1 — the transposition identity is spin-diagonal (P3), so the two-line factor-wise proof goes through verbatim. Corollary: same-spin diagonal elements are polynomials in \(\Dv\) — this proves the \(\Dv\)-structure of the polarization-to-polarization tables [RC26, Eq. 21a] (e.g. their \({}_{\pm\pm}\hat S_2\) coefficient \(\tfrac13 + \tfrac{\Oh}{10} - \tfrac{\Oh^2}{30}\) contains \(\tfrac{3\Oh - \Oh^2}{30} = -\Dv/30\)), exactly as N1a proved it for the intensity tables.

(N15b) Mixed-spin reflection new P8 (exact)
\[ T(s_1,s_2)^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\Oh) \;=\; T(s_2,s_1)^m_{\ell''\ell'\ell}(3-\Oh): \]

under the phase-space reflection the spins ride along with the legs — transposing the round trip swaps which leg carries the polarization. Proof: apply the spin-diagonal transposition to each factor; the weight bookkeeping is unchanged from N1. Via N12 this says the intensity→polarization and polarization→intensity blocks of the scattering operator are mutual adjoints on polarized photon phase space.

(N15c) Polarized parity P9 (exact)
\[ T(s_1,s_2)^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\beta) = (-1)^{\ell+\ell''}\,T(-s_1,-s_2)^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(-\beta). \]

Reversing the boost flips both spins (a spatial reflection swaps the two circular/spin components), with the same outer-index phase as N2.

2  The status of [RC26, Eq. 20]: \({}_{0,\pm2}\hat S_\ell \equiv {}_{\pm2,0}\hat S_\ell\)

[RC26] confirmed this identity term by term in a \(p\)-expansion but could not prove it. Three results:

(i) Operator-exact verification, all orders P10: ℓ = 2, 3, 4; q ∈ {0.5, −0.7}; β ∈ {0.3, 0.7}
\[ \sum_{m} T(0,2)^m_{\ell,2,\ell}(q,\beta) \;=\; \sum_{m} T(2,0)^m_{\ell,2,\ell}(q,\beta) \]

holds to \(10^{-24}\) (quadrature-limited) at generic non-integer \(q\) and \(\beta\) up to \(0.7\) — by Lemma L1 this certifies the operator identity order by order in \(\beta\), upgrading the paper’s series check to all orders in both expansion parameters.

(ii) The identity is collective in \(m\) new structural fact P10b

Per-\(m\), the identity fails badly (differences of order \(0.07\)–\(0.8\) at \(\ell=2\), \(\beta=0.3\)): \(T(0,2)^m \neq T(2,0)^m\). Only the \(m\)-sum — the rotational average over boost directions that defines \({}_{ij}\hat S_\ell\) — restores the equality. Any attempted proof that works \(m\)-by-\(m\) must therefore fail; this explains why the boost-engine route of [RC26] could not reach it.

(iii) Equivalence theorem: Eq. 20 ⟺ mixed reflection invariance P11 (exact)

By N15b at \(\ell = \ell''\), \(m\)-summed: \(\sum_m T(0,2)_{\ell2\ell}(3-\Oh) = \sum_m T(2,0)_{\ell2\ell}(\Oh)\). Hence

\[ \text{[RC26, Eq. 20]} \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \sum_m T(0,2)_{\ell 2 \ell}(\Oh) = \sum_m T(0,2)_{\ell 2 \ell}(3-\Oh), \]

i.e. the unproven identity is exactly the statement that the \(m\)-summed mixed diagonal is reflection-invariant — equivalently (N12 language) that the direction-averaged \(I\!\to\!P\) coupling is self-adjoint on photon phase space, i.e. scattering reciprocity between intensity and polarization. Both sides of the equivalence are verified exactly (P10, P11); a full analytic proof now reduces to a single rotational-invariance lemma for the \(m\)-summed round trip — sharply localized, and manifestly a collective-\(m\) statement, consistent with (ii). Corollary of the equivalence: the mixed \({}_{0\pm}\hat S_\ell\) operators are also polynomials in \(\Dv\), as their printed tables show.
