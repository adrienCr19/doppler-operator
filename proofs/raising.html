N5, N6 — Outer-index raising recursions on the weight lattice

Setting

Define the generalized Doppler operators (\(s=0\); physical case \((j,k) = (0,0)\)):

\[ {}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) \;\equiv\; \frac{1}{\gamma}\; {}^{-1-j}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,-\beta)\;\; {}^{-k}\Bo^m_{\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) \;\;\overset{\text{on }\nu^q}{=}\;\; \frac{1}{\gamma}\, {}^{-1-j-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\;{}^{-k-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)\,. \]
Statement
\[ \textbf{(N5)}\qquad \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \frac{1}{C^m_{\ell}\,p}\Big[\gamma\,\Do^m_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''} - {}^{(1,0)}\Do^m_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''}\Big] - \frac{C^m_{\ell-1}}{C^m_{\ell}}\,\Do^m_{\ell-2,\ell'\ell''}\,, \] \[ \textbf{(N6)}\qquad \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \frac{1}{C^m_{\ell''}\,p}\Big[\gamma\,\Do^m_{\ell\ell',\ell''-1} - {}^{(0,-1)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell',\ell''-1}\Big] - \frac{C^m_{\ell''-1}}{C^m_{\ell''}}\,\Do^m_{\ell\ell',\ell''-2}\,, \]

with the same relations holding at every lattice point \((j,k)\) (shift \(j\) or \(k\) accordingly). Together with the seeds (N7) these generate all elements at arbitrary outer indices, at fixed intermediate \(\ell'\).

Ingredient — the kernel \(\ell\)-raising recursion at \(+\beta\)

The verified literature recursion (B10; [CR26, Eq. B3], [RC26, Eq. B3]; harness check C4) reads, at argument \(-\beta\) and \(s=0\):

\[ {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = -\frac{1}{C^m_\ell}\, \frac{\gamma\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(-\beta) - {}^{d-1}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(-\beta)}{p} - \frac{C^m_{\ell-1}}{C^m_\ell}\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell-2,\ell'}(-\beta). \]

Applying the parity identity \({}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\pm\beta) = (-1)^{\ell+\ell'}{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\mp\beta)\) (B3) to every term — which flips the sign of the \((\ell-1)\)-terms relative to the \(\ell\)- and \((\ell-2)\)-terms — gives the \(+\beta\) version:

\[ {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = +\frac{1}{C^m_\ell}\, \frac{\gamma\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(+\beta) - {}^{d-1}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(+\beta)}{p} - \frac{C^m_{\ell-1}}{C^m_\ell}\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell-2,\ell'}(+\beta). \]

Proof of N5

On power laws the first factor of \(\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\) is \({}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\); its row index \(\ell\) is the outgoing index and its column \(\ell'\) is the spectator. Apply the \(+\beta\) recursion with \(d = -1-q\) and multiply through by the untouched second factor \(\tfrac1\gamma {}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)\). The \(d\)-term products reassemble into \(\Do^m_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''}\) and \(\Do^m_{\ell-2,\ell'\ell''}\); the \((d-1)\)-term has first weight \(-2-q = -1-(1)-q\), i.e. the lattice point \((j,k)=(1,0)\). \(\blacksquare\)

Proof of N6

The second factor \({}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)\) needs a recursion in its column \(\ell''\). Transpose it (B3): \({}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta) = {}^{2+q}\Kk^m_{\ell''\ell'}(+\beta)\), apply the \(+\beta\) row recursion above with \(d = 2+q\) and row index \(\ell''\), then transpose each term back: \({}^{2+q}\Kk^m_{\ell''-1,\ell'}(+\beta) = {}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell',\ell''-1}(-\beta)\) and \({}^{1+q}\Kk^m_{\ell''-1,\ell'}(+\beta) = {}^{1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell',\ell''-1}(-\beta)\). The latter has second weight \(-k-q\) with \(k = -1\): the lattice point \((0,-1)\). Multiplying by the untouched first factor completes the proof. \(\blacksquare\)

Reflection duality

Under the reflection N1 (\(\ell \leftrightarrow \ell''\), \(\Oh \to 3-\Oh\), which on the lattice acts as \((j,k) \to (-k,-j)\)), relation N5 maps exactly onto relation N6 — deriving either one yields the other for free, and any implementation can generate one outer index and obtain the other by symmetry.

Why no recursion closes at \((j,k)=(0,0)\) alone

One might hope to eliminate the companion \({}^{(1,0)}\Do\) using the weight-raising relation (B6), \({}^{-2-q}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(\beta) = \gamma\,{}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(\beta) - \gamma\beta\big[C^m_{\ell}{}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell,\ell'}(\beta) + C^m_{\ell-1}{}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell-2,\ell'}(\beta)\big]\). Substituting it into N5 collapses the relation to the tautology \(\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\): the \(\ell\)-raising recursion and the weight-raising relation are not independent, so the weight companion carries genuinely new information. Closure without companions is achieved only by the rapidity ODE (N3), which trades weight shifts for a derivative in \(\eta\). This mirrors the situation for the boost operator itself, where the \(\ell\)-raising recursion (B10) inevitably couples Doppler weights \(d\) and \(d-1\).

Verification

Checks N5 and N6 of 02_doppler.wl: max deviations \(4.6\times10^{-27}\) and \(2.1\times10^{-27}\) at \(\beta = 0.3\), for \(q \in \{0.5, -0.7\}\), \(m \in \{0,1\}\), raised index up to 3, spectator and other index up to 2. The lattice companions were evaluated from their defining kernel products.
