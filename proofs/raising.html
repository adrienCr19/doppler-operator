N5, N6 — Outer-index raising recursions on the weight lattice

0  What is being done, in words

The Doppler operator is a product of two kernels, and its two outer indices are each private to one factor:

\[ \Do_{\boxed{\ell}\,\ell'\,\boxed{\ell''}} \;\propto\; \underbrace{{}^{a}\Kk_{\boxed{\ell}\,\ell'}(+\beta)}_{\ell \text{ is factor 1's \emph{row}}}\;\; \underbrace{{}^{b}\Kk_{\ell'\,\boxed{\ell''}}(-\beta)}_{\ell'' \text{ is factor 2's \emph{column}}}. \]

This makes raising them structurally easier than the shared middle index (N10): a one-sided kernel recursion applied to one factor, with the other factor untouched, immediately reassembles into Doppler elements — no off-diagonal cross terms can appear. The plan:

  1. For \(\ell\) (outgoing, N5): factor 1 needs its row stepped at argument \(+\beta\). The literature \(\ell\)-raising B10 is stated at \(-\beta\); the parity identity (sign flip \((-1)^{\ell+\ell'}\) per element) converts it. B10 unavoidably couples the Doppler weight \(d\) to \(d-1\); on the first factor, whose weight is \(a = -1-j-q\), this shifts the lattice label \(j \to j+1\). That weight-shifted element is the companion.
  2. For \(\ell''\) (incoming, N6): factor 2 needs its column stepped at \(-\beta\). Transpose it (B3), apply the \(+\beta\) row recursion, transpose back. The weight companion now acts on \(b = -k-q\), shifting \(k \to k-1\).
  3. Multiply by the spectator factor. Every term of the kernel recursion carries the same untouched second (resp. first) factor, so each term is itself a Doppler element on the weight lattice \((j,k)\). The recursion closes.
Setting and statement

Generalized Doppler operators on the weight lattice (\(s=0\); physical case \((j,k) = (0,0)\)):

\[ {}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) \;\equiv\; \frac{1}{\gamma}\; {}^{-1-j}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,-\beta)\;\; {}^{-k}\Bo^m_{\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) \;\;\overset{\text{on }\nu^q}{=}\;\; \frac{1}{\gamma}\, {}^{-1-j-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\;{}^{-k-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)\,. \]

Then, at every lattice point \((j,k)\), for \(s=0\):

\[ \textbf{(N5)}\qquad {}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \frac{1}{C^m_{\ell}\,p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''} - {}^{(j+1,k)}\Do^m_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''}\Big] - \frac{C^m_{\ell-1}}{C^m_{\ell}}\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell-2,\ell'\ell''}\,, \] \[ \textbf{(N6)}\qquad {}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \frac{1}{C^m_{\ell''}\,p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell',\ell''-1} - {}^{(j,k-1)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell',\ell''-1}\Big] - \frac{C^m_{\ell''-1}}{C^m_{\ell''}}\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell',\ell''-2}\,. \]

Together with the seeds (N7) these generate all elements at arbitrary outer indices, at fixed intermediate \(\ell'\).

At \(m=0\), the coefficients for the first two steps of each ladder are explicit numbers (\(C_1 = \tfrac1{\sqrt3}\), \(C_2 = \tfrac2{\sqrt{15}}\), \(C_1/C_2 = \tfrac{\sqrt5}2\), \(C_0 = 0\)):

\[ \textbf{(N6, } \ell''\!: 0\to1\textbf{)}\quad {}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell'1} = \frac{\sqrt3}{p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell'0} - {}^{(j,k-1)}\Do_{\ell\ell'0}\Big], \] \[ \textbf{(N6, } \ell''\!: 1\to2\textbf{)}\quad {}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell'2} = \frac{\sqrt{15}}{2p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell'1} - {}^{(j,k-1)}\Do_{\ell\ell'1}\Big] - \frac{\sqrt5}{2}\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell'0}, \]

and identically for N5 with \(\ell\)-steps and companions \((j+1,k)\).

1  Ingredient — the kernel \(\ell\)-raising recursion at \(+\beta\)

The verified literature recursion (B10; [CR26, Eq. B3], [RC26, Eq. B3]; harness check C4) reads, at argument \(-\beta\) and \(s=0\):

\[ {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = -\frac{1}{C^m_\ell}\, \frac{\gamma\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(-\beta) - {}^{d-1}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(-\beta)}{p} - \frac{C^m_{\ell-1}}{C^m_\ell}\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell-2,\ell'}(-\beta). \]

Applying the parity identity \({}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\pm\beta) = (-1)^{\ell+\ell'}{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\mp\beta)\) (B3) to every term — which flips the sign of the \((\ell-1)\)-terms relative to the \(\ell\)- and \((\ell-2)\)-terms — gives the \(+\beta\) version:

\[ {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = +\frac{1}{C^m_\ell}\, \frac{\gamma\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(+\beta) - {}^{d-1}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(+\beta)}{p} - \frac{C^m_{\ell-1}}{C^m_\ell}\,{}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell-2,\ell'}(+\beta). \]

2  Proof of N5

On power laws the first factor of \({}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\) is \({}^{-1-j-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\); its row index \(\ell\) is the outgoing index and its column \(\ell'\) is the spectator. Apply the \(+\beta\) recursion with \(d = -1-j-q\) and multiply through by the untouched second factor \(\tfrac1\gamma {}^{-k-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)\). The \(d\)-term products reassemble into \({}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''}\) and \({}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell-2,\ell'\ell''}\); the \((d-1)\)-term has first weight \(-2-j-q = -1-(j{+}1)-q\), i.e. the lattice point \((j+1,k)\). \(\blacksquare\)

3  Proof of N6

The second factor \({}^{-k-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)\) needs a recursion in its column \(\ell''\). Transpose it (B3): \({}^{-k-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta) = {}^{2+k+q}\Kk^m_{\ell''\ell'}(+\beta)\), apply the \(+\beta\) row recursion above with \(d = 2+k+q\) and row index \(\ell''\), then transpose each term back: \({}^{2+k+q}\Kk^m_{\ell''-1,\ell'}(+\beta) = {}^{-k-q}\Kk^m_{\ell',\ell''-1}(-\beta)\) and \({}^{1+k+q}\Kk^m_{\ell''-1,\ell'}(+\beta) = {}^{1-k-q}\Kk^m_{\ell',\ell''-1}(-\beta)\). The latter has second weight \(-(k{-}1)-q\): the lattice point \((j,k-1)\). Multiplying by the untouched first factor completes the proof. \(\blacksquare\)

4  Reflection duality

Under the reflection N1 (\(\ell \leftrightarrow \ell''\), \(\Oh \to 3-\Oh\), which on the lattice acts as \((j,k) \to (-k,-j)\)), relation N5 maps exactly onto relation N6 — deriving either one yields the other for free, and any implementation can generate one outer index and obtain the other by symmetry.

5  Worked rederivation: \(\Do_{202}\) from the \(\Do_{000}\) base case, four steps

The four-step climb \( (0,\!0,\!0) \xrightarrow{\text{N6}} (0,\!0,\!1) \xrightarrow{\text{N6}} (0,\!0,\!2) \xrightarrow{\text{N5}} (1,\!0,\!2) \xrightarrow{\text{N5}} (2,\!0,\!2)\) exercises everything: both ladders, the weight companions at every step, and (in steps W2, W4) the \(C_{\ell-1}\) three-term tail. Shorthand: \(J_n \equiv J_{n+q}\), \(J_w = \frac{(\gamma+p)^w - (\gamma-p)^w}{w}\), \(J_{-w} = J_w\). It produces a new closed form not derived elsewhere on this site.

W0 — lattice base cases (seed only)

\[ {}^{(j,k)}\Do_{000} = \frac{J_{2+j}\,J_{1+k}}{4\gamma p^2} \qquad\text{(N8, Eq. 2.1; check F9)}. \]

W1 — N6 step \(\ell''\!: 0 \to 1\)

The companion difference touches only factor 2 (\(k\)-label):

\[ \gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{000} - {}^{(j,k-1)}\Do_{000} = \frac{J_{2+j}}{4\gamma p^2}\big(\gamma J_{1+k} - J_{k}\big) \;\;\Rightarrow\;\; {}^{(j,k)}\Do_{001} = \frac{\sqrt3}{4\gamma p^3}\, J_{2+j}\big(\gamma J_{1+k} - J_{k}\big). \tag{W1} \]

check F10

W2 — N6 step \(\ell''\!: 1 \to 2\) (first appearance of the three-term tail)

\[ \gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{001} - {}^{(j,k-1)}\Do_{001} = \frac{\sqrt3}{4\gamma p^3}\,J_{2+j}\Big[\gamma\big(\gamma J_{1+k} - J_k\big) - \big(\gamma J_k - J_{k-1}\big)\Big] = \frac{\sqrt3}{4\gamma p^3}\,J_{2+j}\Big[\gamma^2 J_{1+k} - 2\gamma J_k + J_{k-1}\Big], \] \[ {}^{(j,k)}\Do_{002} = \frac{\sqrt{15}}{2p}\cdot\frac{\sqrt3\,J_{2+j}}{4\gamma p^3}\Big[\gamma^2 J_{1+k} - 2\gamma J_k + J_{k-1}\Big] - \frac{\sqrt5}{2}\cdot\frac{J_{2+j} J_{1+k}}{4\gamma p^2} = \frac{\sqrt5\,J_{2+j}}{8\gamma p^4}\Big[3\gamma^2 J_{1+k} - 6\gamma J_k + 3J_{k-1} - p^2 J_{1+k}\Big], \]

and with \(3\gamma^2 - p^2 = 3+2p^2\) (the same collapse as everywhere in N8/N10):

\[ {}^{(j,k)}\Do_{002} = \frac{\sqrt5}{8\gamma p^4}\, J_{2+j}\; V_k, \qquad V_k \equiv (3+2p^2)\,J_{1+k} - 6\gamma\,J_{k} + 3\,J_{k-1}. \tag{W2} \]

check F12 (exact). Note \(V_0\) is precisely the second bracket of the \(\Do_{020}\) closed form — the same universal quadrupole bracket, now produced by the \(\ell''\)-ladder.

W3 — N5 step \(\ell\!: 0 \to 1\)

Now the companion difference touches only factor 1 (\(j\)-label), with \(V_k\) a spectator:

\[ \gamma\,{}^{(0,k)}\Do_{002} - {}^{(1,k)}\Do_{002} = \frac{\sqrt5}{8\gamma p^4}\big(\gamma J_{2} - J_{3}\big)V_k \;\;\Rightarrow\;\; {}^{(0,k)}\Do_{102} = \frac{\sqrt{15}}{8\gamma p^5}\big(\gamma J_{2} - J_{3}\big) V_k . \tag{W3} \]

check F13, 6×10⁻³²  (here we set \(j=0\); the general-\(j\) form has \(J_{2+j}, J_{3+j}\)).

W4 — N5 step \(\ell\!: 1 \to 2\) and the final form

\[ \gamma\,{}^{(0,0)}\Do_{102} - {}^{(1,0)}\Do_{102} = \frac{\sqrt{15}}{8\gamma p^5}\Big[\gamma\big(\gamma J_2 - J_3\big) - \big(\gamma J_3 - J_4\big)\Big]V_0 = \frac{\sqrt{15}}{8\gamma p^5}\Big[\gamma^2 J_2 - 2\gamma J_3 + J_4\Big]V_0, \] \[ \Do_{202} = \frac{\sqrt{15}}{2p}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{15}}{8\gamma p^5}\Big[\gamma^2 J_2 - 2\gamma J_3 + J_4\Big]V_0 - \frac{\sqrt5}{2}\cdot\frac{\sqrt5\,J_2\,V_0}{8\gamma p^4} = \frac{5\,V_0}{16\gamma p^6}\Big[3\gamma^2 J_2 - 6\gamma J_3 + 3J_4 - p^2 J_2\Big], \]

and the \(3\gamma^2 - p^2 = 3+2p^2\) collapse once more:

\[ \boxed{\;\Do_{202}(q) = \frac{5}{16\gamma p^6} \Big[(3+2p^2)J_{2+q} - 6\gamma J_{3+q} + 3J_{4+q}\Big] \Big[(3+2p^2)J_{1+q} - 6\gamma J_{q} + 3J_{q-1}\Big]\;} \tag{W4} \]

check F14 vs direct quadrature, β ∈ {0.3, 0.7} — a new closed form

Consistency. \(\Do_{202}\) is diagonal (\(\ell = \ell''\)), so N1a requires invariance under \(q \to -3-q\). Indeed, evenness maps \(J_{2+q}\!\to\!J_{1+q}\), \(J_{3+q}\!\to\!J_{q}\), \(J_{4+q}\!\to\!J_{q-1}\): the two brackets swap, exactly as in \(\Do_{101}\) and \(\Do_{020}\) (check F15, exact). Its \(p\)-series \(\tfrac{1}{45}\Oh(\Oh^3-6\Oh^2+11\Oh-6)p^4 + \dots\) starts at \(p^4\), consistent with the Section-4 table entry \(\Do_{2,0,2}\). The simpler two-step example (\(\Do_{101}\), no three-term tails) is worked as Route 1 on the N8 page (checks F9–F11).

6  Why no recursion closes at \((j,k)=(0,0)\) alone

One might hope to eliminate the companion \({}^{(j+1,k)}\Do\) using the weight-raising relation (B6), \({}^{-2-q}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(\beta) = \gamma\,{}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(\beta) - \gamma\beta\big[C^m_{\ell}{}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell,\ell'}(\beta) + C^m_{\ell-1}{}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell-2,\ell'}(\beta)\big]\). Substituting it into N5 collapses the relation to the tautology \(\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\): the \(\ell\)-raising recursion and the weight-raising relation are not independent, so the weight companion carries genuinely new information. Closure without companions is achieved only by the rapidity ODE (N3), which trades weight shifts for a derivative in \(\eta\). This mirrors the situation for the boost operator itself, where the \(\ell\)-raising recursion (B10) inevitably couples Doppler weights \(d\) and \(d-1\) — and the middle index continues the pattern with one more companion type (N10).
