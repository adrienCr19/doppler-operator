N14 — Finite recoil: the recoil ladder is the weight lattice, and every recursion extends verbatim

Everything on this site so far is the Thomson (no-recoil) limit. At finite photon energy \(x = h\nu/m_ec^2\), the rest-frame cross-section and energy shift acquire corrections in powers of the rest-frame photon energy \(x' \), and the operator algebra becomes noncommutative: \([\Oh, x] = -x\). The companion rSZ working paper organizes this into a normal-ordered recoil ladder \(\sum_s x^s f_s(\Oh)\) whose rung functions \(f_s\) are assembled from round trips with rest-frame energy insertions. This page establishes the structural result that makes the whole N-toolkit apply at finite recoil: the rest-frame insertions land exactly on the weight lattice \({}^{(j,k)}\Do\) that the recursions N5/N6/N10 already required — the lattice, introduced as a formal closure device, is the recoil ladder.

(i) Commutation dictionary R1 (exact)
\[ [\Oh, x] = -x \qquad\Longleftrightarrow\qquad f(\Oh)\,x^{s} = x^{s} f(\Oh - s) \quad\text{for analytic } f . \]

Proof: on dilations, \(e^{a\Oh}\big[x^s g(x)\big] = (e^{-a}x)^s g(e^{-a}x) = x^s e^{-as}\,[e^{a\Oh}g](x)\), i.e. \(e^{a\Oh}x^s = x^s e^{a(\Oh-s)}\); expand in \(a\). Moving explicit energy powers through any \(\Oh\)-function — including every closed form on this site — costs only the shift \(\Oh \to \Oh - s\).

(ii) Rest-frame insertion = lattice shift R2 (exact)

A recoil correction inserts powers of the rest-frame energy \(x'\) between the two boosts. On a power law \(\nu^q\), the round trip with an \(x'^s\) insertion evaluates to

\[ \frac1\gamma\,\Bo(-\beta)\Big[\,x'^s\cdot\big(\Bo(\beta)\,\nu^q\big)\Big] \;=\; x^{s}\;\Big[\tfrac1\gamma\,{}^{-1-s-q}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\,{}^{-q}\Kk_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)\Big]\,\nu^{q} \;=\; x^{s}\;{}^{(s,0)}\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q), \]

because the returned spectrum \(\nu^{q+s}\) presents the first boost with weight \(-1-(q+s)\): precisely the lattice point \((j,k) = (s,0)\). Insertions on the incoming leg populate \(k\), and the Klein–Nishina angular structure only reweights the \(\ell'\)-channels (which N13 handles for arbitrary phase functions). So the \(s\)-th recoil rung of the exact single-scattering operator is

\[ \text{rung } s: \qquad x^{s} \sum_{\ell'} w^{(s)}_{\ell'}(\Oh)\; {}^{(j,k)\,\in\,\text{level } s}\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}, \]

a finite combination of level-\(s\) lattice elements with rational rest-frame coefficients — the operator form of the companion paper's normal-ordered ladder \(\sum_s x^s f_s(\Oh)\) (whose static limits give \(R_1 = 4-\Oh\), \(R_2 = \tfrac{7}{10}(\Oh^2 - 5\Oh)\), …).

(iii) All recursions extend verbatim to every rung R3; M4; N5/N6 at lattice points

The N-relations were derived — and verified — at general lattice point \((j,k)\), so they apply to each recoil rung unchanged. In particular the lattice-generalized rapidity ODE,

\[ \begin{aligned} \partial_\eta\, {}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} \;=\; &-(\ell+3+j-\Oh)\,C^m_{\ell+1}\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell+1,\ell'\ell''} +(\ell-2-j+\Oh)\,C^m_{\ell}\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''} \\[2pt] &-(\ell''+k+\Oh)\,C^m_{\ell''+1}\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell,\ell',\ell''+1} +(\ell''+1-k-\Oh)\,C^m_{\ell''}\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell,\ell',\ell''-1} \;-\;\beta\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}, \end{aligned} \]

was verified by finite differences at the recoil-relevant rungs \((j,k) \in \{1,2,0\}\times\{0,-1\}\) (R3, 6×10⁻¹²); the raising recursions N5/N6 and the middle-index relation N10 hold at shifted lattice points by their proofs (checks N5/N6, M4). The closed-form machinery (N8) applies too: lattice elements are the same \(J\)-products with shifted indices, e.g. \({}^{(s,0)}\Do_{000}(q) = J_{2+s+q}J_{1+q}/(4\gamma p^2)\).

What does not extend, and why

Two Thomson-limit features fail at finite recoil, both for identified reasons:

  • Reflection/self-adjointness per rung. N1 maps \((j,k) \to (-k,-j)\) (see N5/N6): individual rungs with \(j \neq -k\) are not self-conjugate — only appropriately paired insertions on the two legs are. This is the operator-level trace of energy exchange: recoil breaks the elastic reciprocity that made the Thomson round trip self-adjoint on photon phase space (N12); the full recoil operator instead satisfies detailed balance with the Planck weight, not symmetry with the vacuum weight.
  • Bessel closure of thermal averages. The companion paper proves the recoil ladder's thermal average is only asymptotic in general (the Maxwellian tail crosses the Klein–Nishina branch point on \(\Oh\)-eigenmodes; superasymptotic ambiguity \(\sim e^{-\cosh\eta_*/\theta_{\rm e}}\)). The lattice structure is consistent with this: rung \(s\) shifts \(a_j \to a_j \pm s\) inside the same \(F(\mu,a,z)\) class (N9/N11), so each rung individually remains in the class, while the \(s\)-resummation is where convergence is lost.

Practical upshot

To compute recoil-corrected SZ signals in this framework: (1) expand the rest-frame cross-section and energy shift to the desired order in \(x'\) — this fixes the channel weights \(w^{(s)}_{\ell'}(\Oh)\) (Klein–Nishina angular terms enter as Funk–Hecke weights via N13); (2) evaluate the required lattice elements \({}^{(j,k)}\Do\) by the N3 ODE (series) or N8 closed forms (all \(\beta\)); (3) thermally average with N9/N11, respecting the superasymptotic truncation \(N_* \sim 1/\theta_{\rm e}\). Every ingredient is already verified; what remains application-specific is only the rest-frame expansion in step (1) — the route by which [HLC26] obtain the Kompaneets equation and the companion paper obtains \(R_1, R_2, R_3\).
