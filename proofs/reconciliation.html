Reconciliation with “The Doppler Operator Generated from Its Seed”

An independent document — The Doppler Operator Generated from Its Seed: Explicit Theorems and Closed Forms (9 July 2026; companion script doppler_generator.wl), the third of a series that also derives the kernel-level inputs — restates the raising machinery as a closed, self-contained calculus on the Doppler operator itself. We re-derived and independently verified its claims against this project’s results (verification/20_reconcile_generation.wl, all checks by high-precision quadrature on \(\nu^q\)). Verdict: the two developments agree everywhere they overlap; the paper contributes several genuinely new results; and its rapidity ODE surfaced a transcription error on our N14 page, now corrected.

2  What the paper adds (verified here)

  • The gauge identity (G2c): \({}^{(j+1,k+1)}X(q) = {}^{(j,k)}X(q+1)\) R1, exact. A genuinely useful new observation: a diagonal move on the weight lattice is a unit shift of the power-law exponent, so the 2D lattice collapses to the single label \(j-k\) (plus the \(q\)-exponent). This is the engine behind their pure-physical relations — it lets a lattice companion be rewritten as a physical element at shifted \(q\).
  • Pure-physical relations P0–P4 — every term a physical \(\Do\), no auxiliary family:
    • P1 outer-pair relation R3, \(10^{-27}\): \(C_{\ell+1}\Do_{\ell+1,\ell'\ell''}(q) + C_\ell\Do_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''}(q) - C_{\ell''+1}\Do_{\ell,\ell',\ell''+1}(q{+}1) - C_{\ell''}\Do_{\ell,\ell',\ell''-1}(q{+}1) = \tfrac\gamma p[\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q) - \Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q{+}1)]\). This is new relative to the site — our N5/N6 always carried a lattice companion; here the two outer ladders are combined and the companion cancels via the gauge identity.
    • P0 axis ladders R4/R4b, exact: an explicit one-index raising seeded by \(\Do_{000}\) alone, e.g. \(C_1\Do_{100}(q) = \tfrac\gamma p\Do_{000}(q) - \tfrac1p\tfrac{J_{1+q}}{J_{2+q}}\Do_{000}(q{+}1)\).
    • P2 rank-one R5, exact: at fixed \(\ell', q\) the array \(\Do_{h\ell'l}\) is rank one, \(\Do_{h\ell'l} = \Do_{h\ell'0}\Do_{0\ell'l}/\Do_{0\ell'0}\) — a clean corollary of the factorization, and a compression: one row and column determine everything.
    • P3 pure-physical middle step (nonlinear; a ratio of physical elements). Consistent with our N10 finding that a linear middle recursion needs the off-diagonal companions — they show the companion-free version exists but must be nonlinear, exactly because the diagonal slice is codimension one.
  • The mirror rapidity flow (G5b) R6, \(10^{-12}\): a second, complementary rapidity ODE in which only the middle indices move (outer indices spectate) — the mirror of our N3, in which the middle index spectates. New; verified on off-diagonal elements.
  • The two middle ladders kept separate. Where our N10 combines the two middle-index steps into one diagonal relation (M) with off-diagonal companions, the paper keeps G1-\(\ell_1'\), G1-\(\ell_2'\) separate on the full off-diagonal family — cleaner and strictly more general (M is their diagonal-restricted product). We had noted the independent recursiveness (N10 §2); they make it the primary object. A genuine improvement in framing.

3  The correction the paper surfaced

N14 lattice ODE: \(k\)-sign typo, now fixed R2 decisive

Our N14 page displayed the lattice-generalized rapidity ODE with the \(\ell''\)-term signs of \(k\) flipped. Finite differences of the quadrature-defined operator at \(k = \pm1\) match the paper’s G5a (\(5\times10^{-12}\)) and reject our displayed form (deviation \(1.7\)):

\[ \text{correct (paper G5a, our harness):}\quad -(\ell''-k+\Oh)\,C_{\ell''+1}\Do_{\dots,\ell''+1} + (\ell''+1+k-\Oh)\,C_{\ell''}\Do_{\dots,\ell''-1}, \] \[ \text{our earlier N14 display (wrong):}\quad -(\ell''+k+\Oh)\,C_{\ell''+1}\Do_{\dots,\ell''+1} + (\ell''+1-k-\Oh)\,C_{\ell''}\Do_{\dots,\ell''-1}. \]

Crucially, our verification harness 14_recoil_lattice.wl always used the correct signs (which is why its check R3 legitimately passed at \(6\times10^{-12}\)) — the error was confined to the HTML transcription and affected no downstream result. The physical N3 (\(k=0\)) is unaffected, since the two forms coincide at \(k=0\). The page is corrected and carries a dated note. This is exactly the value of an independent re-derivation: a display typo that the internal checks could not catch (they tested the code, not the prose) was caught by cross-referencing a second formulation.

4  Verdict

Do they agree? Yes — completely, on every shared object and identity (R1, R3–R9 all pass; the one apparent disagreement, R2, was our transcription error, not a substantive one). Is the work useful? Yes. Beyond confirming the framework independently, it delivers: the gauge identity reducing the lattice to one label; the pure-physical relation set P0–P4 (removing the auxiliary family from the outer-index recursions entirely, and characterizing why the middle index cannot be linearized); the mirror rapidity flow; the \(F\)-bracket consolidation; and the rank-one structure — plus the catch of our N14 display error. Its framing (a generation calculus that never references kernels, boosts, integrals, or derivatives) is a cleaner packaging of the N8 structure theorem, and its “keep the middle ladders separate” stance is a modest but real improvement over our (M). We recommend adopting the gauge identity and P1/P0 into the core toolkit, and the mirror flow G5b as a companion to N3.
