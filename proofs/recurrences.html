The exact recurrence relations of the Doppler operator

Complete proofs, carried to conclusion, from the aberration kernel. This page is the core of the project. It states the exact index-raising relations of the Doppler operator and proves each one by descending to the object the Doppler operator is built from — the aberration kernel of the boost operator — establishing the kernel's own recurrences directly from its integral definition and the Legendre relation, and then doing every line of algebra needed to lift them back to the Doppler operator. Nothing is imported as a "known" fact: the seed, parity, transposition and the master \(\ell\)-raising recurrence are all derived here. Every identity is verified to machine zero in 36_kernel_foundations.wl.

The four exact recurrences (summary) central result all verified, dev = 0

On power laws \(\nu^q\) the Doppler operator factorizes (§1). Writing \(C_\ell=\ell/\sqrt{4\ell^2-1}\), \(\gamma=\cosh\eta\), \(p=\sinh\eta\), and using the weight-lattice family \({}^{(j,k)}\Do\) (§1), the exact relations are:

\[ \textbf{(N5) outgoing }\ell:\quad {}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \tfrac{1}{C_\ell p}\big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''} - {}^{(j+1,k)}\Do_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''}\big] - \tfrac{C_{\ell-1}}{C_\ell}\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell-2,\ell'\ell''}, \] \[ \textbf{(N6) incoming }\ell'':\quad {}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \tfrac{1}{C_{\ell''} p}\big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell',\ell''-1} - {}^{(j,k-1)}\Do_{\ell\ell',\ell''-1}\big] - \tfrac{C_{\ell''-1}}{C_{\ell''}}\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell',\ell''-2}, \] \[ \textbf{(N10) middle }\ell':\quad\text{a shared-index step — closes on the extended family (linear, §4.2) or purely on }\Do\text{ (nonlinear, §4.3)}, \] \[ \textbf{(N25) pure-}\Do:\quad\text{the axis ladder (P0), middle step (P3), reflection (N1) and rank-one fill (P2) — no companions (§5).} \]

N5 and N6 raise the two private outer indices; each pays one weight-lattice companion. The shared middle index \(\ell'\) is the hard one and is treated in full in §4. The roadmap: §1 the two objects and the reduction; §2 the four kernel identities, each proved from the integral; §3 the outer recurrences; §4 the middle index; §5 the pure-\(\Do\) closure.

1  The two objects and the reduction

The aberration kernel of the boost operator, at scalar weight \(d\) and \(s=0\), is the matrix element (\(\bar Y_\ell(\mu)=\sqrt{(2\ell+1)/4\pi}\,P_\ell(\mu)\)):

\[ {}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) \;=\; 2\pi\!\int_{-1}^{1} \bar Y_\ell(\mu)\,\bar Y_{\ell'}(\mu')\,\big[\gamma(1+\beta\mu)\big]^{-d}\,\dd\mu, \qquad \mu' = \frac{\mu+\beta}{1+\beta\mu}, \tag{1.1} \]

and \({}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\) is the same with \(\beta\to-\beta\) (aberration \((\mu-\beta)/(1-\beta\mu)\), weight \([\gamma(1-\beta\mu)]^{-d}\)). The Doppler operator is the physically assembled round trip \(\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}=\gamma^{-1}\,{}^{-1}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,-\beta)\,{}^{0}\Bo^m_{\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta)\); on \(\nu^q\) the boost operators reduce to kernels (Lemma L1: \(\Oh\to-q\)), giving the product that drives everything below — together with the weight lattice \((j,k)\) that the recurrences move on:

\[ {}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q) \;=\; \frac{1}{\gamma}\;{}^{-1-j-q}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\;{}^{-k-q}\Kk_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta), \qquad \Do = {}^{(0,0)}\Do . \tag{1.2} \]

The outgoing index \(\ell\) is the row of factor 1; the incoming index \(\ell''\) is the column of factor 2; the scattering index \(\ell'\) is shared — the column of factor 1 and the row of factor 2. That structure is the whole story: private indices give clean one-sided recursions (§3), the shared index does not (§4).

2  The four kernel identities, proved from the integral

Two elementary aberration facts, used repeatedly. From \(\mu'=(\mu+\beta)/(1+\beta\mu)\),

\[ 1-\beta\mu' = \frac{1-\beta^2}{1+\beta\mu} = \frac{1}{\gamma^2(1+\beta\mu)} \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad 1+\beta\mu = \frac{1}{\gamma^2(1-\beta\mu')}, \qquad \frac{\dd\mu}{\dd\mu'} = \frac{1-\beta^2}{(1-\beta\mu')^2}. \tag{2.0} \]

2.1  Seed (the one integral)

With \(\bar Y_0=1/\sqrt{4\pi}\), the monopole element is elementary:

\[ {}^{d}\Kk_{00}(-\beta) = \frac{2\pi}{4\pi}\,\gamma^{-d}\!\int_{-1}^1(1+\beta\mu)^{-d}\dd\mu = \frac{\gamma^{-d}}{2\beta}\,\frac{(1+\beta)^{1-d}-(1-\beta)^{1-d}}{1-d}. \]

Now \(\gamma^{-d}(1\pm\beta)^{1-d}=\gamma^{-1}[\gamma(1\pm\beta)]^{1-d}=\gamma^{-1}(\gamma\pm p)^{1-d}\) (since \(\gamma(1\pm\beta)=\gamma\pm p=e^{\pm\eta}\)), and \(\gamma^{-1}/\beta=1/p\). Hence, with \(J_w=\frac{(\gamma+p)^w-(\gamma-p)^w}{w}=\frac{2\sinh(w\eta)}{w}\),

\[ \boxed{\;{}^{d}\Kk_{00}(\pm\beta) = \frac{J_{1-d}}{2p}\;} \tag{2.1} \]

(sign-independent: swapping \(\gamma+p\leftrightarrow\gamma-p\) flips both the bracket and the \(1/2p\)). I1/I1′, dev 0

2.2  Parity

Substitute \(\mu\to-\mu\) in (1.1). Then \(\bar Y_\ell(-\mu)=(-1)^\ell\bar Y_\ell(\mu)\), the weight \([\gamma(1+\beta\mu)]^{-d}\to[\gamma(1-\beta\mu)]^{-d}\) becomes the \(+\beta\) weight, and the aberrated argument \(\frac{-\mu+\beta}{1-\beta\mu}=-\frac{\mu-\beta}{1-\beta\mu}\) is minus the \(+\beta\) aberration, so \(\bar Y_{\ell'}\!\to(-1)^{\ell'}\bar Y_{\ell'}\) evaluated at the \(+\beta\) map. Collecting the two signs:

\[ \boxed{\;{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = (-1)^{\ell+\ell'}\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\;} \tag{2.2} \]

I2, dev 0

2.3  Transposition

Change the integration variable in (1.1) to \(\mu'\), i.e. \(\mu=\frac{\mu'-\beta}{1-\beta\mu'}\). By (2.0) the weight is \([\gamma(1+\beta\mu)]^{-d}=[\gamma^{-1}(1-\beta\mu')^{-1}]^{-d}=\gamma^{d}(1-\beta\mu')^{d}\) and the Jacobian is \(\gamma^{-2}(1-\beta\mu')^{-2}\), so their product is \([\gamma(1-\beta\mu')]^{d-2}\). The roles of the two harmonics swap and the map \(\mu(\mu')=\frac{\mu'-\beta}{1-\beta\mu'}\) is exactly the \(+\beta\) aberration of \(\mu'\):

\[ {}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = 2\pi\!\int_{-1}^1 \bar Y_{\ell'}(\mu')\,\bar Y_{\ell}\!\Big(\tfrac{\mu'-\beta}{1-\beta\mu'}\Big)\,\big[\gamma(1-\beta\mu')\big]^{-(2-d)}\dd\mu' = {}^{2-d}\Kk_{\ell'\ell}(+\beta). \] \[ \boxed{\;{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = {}^{2-d}\Kk_{\ell'\ell}(+\beta)\;} \tag{2.3} \]

I3, dev 0

2.4  The master \(\ell\)-raising recurrence

This is the engine. Two ingredients. First, the Legendre three-term relation in normalized form — the matrix element of \(\mu\):

\[ \mu\,\bar Y_\ell(\mu) = C_{\ell+1}\,\bar Y_{\ell+1}(\mu) + C_\ell\,\bar Y_{\ell-1}(\mu), \qquad C_\ell=\frac{\ell}{\sqrt{4\ell^2-1}}, \tag{2.4a} \]

(directly from \((2\ell+1)\mu P_\ell=(\ell+1)P_{\ell+1}+\ell P_{\ell-1}\); I0, dev 0). Second, the \(\mu\)-to-weight identity: since \(\mu=\frac1\beta[(1+\beta\mu)-1]\) and \((1+\beta\mu)\,[\gamma(1+\beta\mu)]^{-d}=\gamma^{-1}[\gamma(1+\beta\mu)]^{-(d-1)}\),

\[ \mu\,\big[\gamma(1+\beta\mu)\big]^{-d} = \frac1\beta\Big[\gamma^{-1}\big[\gamma(1+\beta\mu)\big]^{-(d-1)} - \big[\gamma(1+\beta\mu)\big]^{-d}\Big] \tag{2.4b} \]

(I4a, dev 0). Insert (2.4a) under the integral (1.1). The extra factor of \(\mu\) multiplies the weight; apply (2.4b) and read off two kernels of adjacent weight:

\[ 2\pi\!\int \big(\mu\,\bar Y_\ell\big)\bar Y_{\ell'}(\mu')\,[\gamma(1+\beta\mu)]^{-d}\dd\mu = \frac{1}{\beta}\Big[\gamma^{-1}\,{}^{d-1}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) - {}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta)\Big]. \]

But by (2.4a) that same integral equals \(C_{\ell+1}\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell+1,\ell'}(-\beta) + C_\ell\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell-1,\ell'}(-\beta)\). Equate, use \(1/\beta\gamma=1/p\) and \(1/\beta=\gamma/p\), and shift \(\ell+1\to\ell\):

\[ \boxed{\;C_\ell\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = \frac1p\,{}^{d-1}\Kk_{\ell-1,\ell'}(-\beta) - \frac{\gamma}{p}\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell-1,\ell'}(-\beta) - C_{\ell-1}\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell-2,\ell'}(-\beta)\;} \tag{KR} \]

I4, dev 0. This is the aberration-kernel \(\ell\)-raising recurrence — the "B10" of the literature, here derived outright from Legendre + the weight identity. The \(+\beta\) form follows by multiplying (KR) through by \((-1)^{\ell+\ell'}\) and applying parity (2.2) term by term; the \((\ell-1)\)-terms carry an extra sign, so

\[ {}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = \frac{1}{C_\ell p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell-1,\ell'}(+\beta) - {}^{d-1}\Kk_{\ell-1,\ell'}(+\beta)\Big] - \frac{C_{\ell-1}}{C_\ell}\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell-2,\ell'}(+\beta). \tag{KR$^+$} \]

I4+, dev 0

3  The outer recurrences — full reassembly

3.1  N5 (raise the outgoing index \(\ell\))

In (1.2) the first factor is \({}^{-1-j-q}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\); its row is \(\ell\), its column \(\ell'\) is a spectator. Apply (KR\(^+\)) with weight \(d=-1-j-q\):

\[ {}^{-1-j-q}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = \frac{1}{C_\ell p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{-1-j-q}\Kk_{\ell-1,\ell'}(+\beta) - {}^{-2-j-q}\Kk_{\ell-1,\ell'}(+\beta)\Big] - \frac{C_{\ell-1}}{C_\ell}\,{}^{-1-j-q}\Kk_{\ell-2,\ell'}(+\beta). \]

Multiply every term by the untouched second factor \(\tfrac1\gamma\,{}^{-k-q}\Kk_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)\). By the definition (1.2), the \(\gamma\)- and \(C_{\ell-1}\)-terms reassemble as \({}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''}\) and \({}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell-2,\ell'\ell''}\); the companion \({}^{-2-j-q}\Kk = {}^{-1-(j+1)-q}\Kk\) carries the first weight one step down the lattice, giving \({}^{(j+1,k)}\Do_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''}\). Therefore

\[ \boxed{\;{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \frac{1}{C_\ell p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''} - {}^{(j+1,k)}\Do_{\ell-1,\ell'\ell''}\Big] - \frac{C_{\ell-1}}{C_\ell}\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell-2,\ell'\ell''}\;} \tag{N5} \]

Every term is a Doppler operator on the weight lattice; the spectator second factor never moved. R-N5, dev 0 \(\blacksquare\)

3.2  N6 (raise the incoming index \(\ell''\))

The second factor \({}^{-k-q}\Kk_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)\) needs a recursion in its column \(\ell''\). Transpose it (2.3) to put \(\ell''\) in the row: \({}^{-k-q}\Kk_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)={}^{2+k+q}\Kk_{\ell''\ell'}(+\beta)\). Apply (KR\(^+\)) at weight \(d=2+k+q\), row \(\ell''\):

\[ {}^{2+k+q}\Kk_{\ell''\ell'}(+\beta) = \frac{1}{C_{\ell''}p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{2+k+q}\Kk_{\ell''-1,\ell'}(+\beta) - {}^{1+k+q}\Kk_{\ell''-1,\ell'}(+\beta)\Big] - \frac{C_{\ell''-1}}{C_{\ell''}}\,{}^{2+k+q}\Kk_{\ell''-2,\ell'}(+\beta). \]

Transpose each term back with (2.3) in the form \({}^{a}\Kk_{ab}(+\beta)={}^{2-a}\Kk_{ba}(-\beta)\): \({}^{2+k+q}\Kk_{\ell''-1,\ell'}(+\beta)={}^{-k-q}\Kk_{\ell',\ell''-1}(-\beta)\), and the companion \({}^{1+k+q}\Kk_{\ell''-1,\ell'}(+\beta)={}^{1-k-q}\Kk_{\ell',\ell''-1}(-\beta)={}^{-(k-1)-q}\Kk\), i.e. lattice \(k\to k-1\). Multiplying by the untouched first factor \(\tfrac1\gamma\,{}^{-1-j-q}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\):

\[ \boxed{\;{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \frac{1}{C_{\ell''} p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell',\ell''-1} - {}^{(j,k-1)}\Do_{\ell\ell',\ell''-1}\Big] - \frac{C_{\ell''-1}}{C_{\ell''}}\,{}^{(j,k)}\Do_{\ell\ell',\ell''-2}\;} \tag{N6} \]

R-N6, dev 0 \(\blacksquare\)   N5 and N6 exchange under the reflection N1 (\(\ell\leftrightarrow\ell''\), \((j,k)\to(-k,-j)\)) — a cross-check of the whole system. The four-step climb from \(\Do_{000}\) to the new closed form \(\Do_{202}\), with every weight companion worked out, is on the N5/N6 page.

4  The middle index \(\ell'\)

4.1  Ingredient: the column recursion of the \(+\beta\) kernel

The shared index is the column of factor 1. To step it, transpose factor 1 to expose \(\ell'\) as a row, apply (KR) at \(-\beta\), and transpose back. With \({}^{a}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)={}^{2-a}\Kk_{\ell'\ell}(-\beta)\), (KR) on the row \(\ell'\) at weight \(2-a\), and transposing every term:

\[ \boxed{\;{}^{a}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = -\frac{1}{C_{\ell'} p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{a}\Kk_{\ell,\ell'-1}(+\beta) - {}^{a+1}\Kk_{\ell,\ell'-1}(+\beta)\Big] - \frac{C_{\ell'-1}}{C_{\ell'}}\,{}^{a}\Kk_{\ell,\ell'-2}(+\beta)\;} \tag{A} \]

Note the weight companion is raised (\(a+1\)), the mirror of (KR\(^+\))'s lowered one. R-A, dev 0

4.2  The linear middle step (N10)

A shift of the shared index acts on both factors of (1.2) at once. Solving (A) for factor 1 at column \(\ell'\) and the transpose of (A) for factor 2 at row \(\ell'\), and multiplying the two three-term right-hand sides, gives nine products. Five have matched intermediate indices and reassemble into Doppler operators on the weight lattice; the other four have mismatched intermediate indices \((\ell'-1,\ell'-2)\), \((\ell'-2,\ell'-1)\) — genuine off-diagonal elements \(X_{\ell;\ell_1'\ell_2';\ell''}\). The resulting linear relation (M), and the proof that no linear diagonal relation can avoid these companions, are on the middle-index page (N10); applied once at \(\ell'{=}2\) it reproduces the \(\Do_{020}\) closed form exactly.

4.3  The pure-\(\Do\) middle step (N25/P3)

The companions can be removed entirely — at the price of a ratio — by turning each kernel factor back into a physical \(\Do\). Parity (2.2) gives \({}^{-q}\Kk_{b0}(-\beta)=(-1)^b\,{}^{-q}\Kk_{b0}(+\beta)\), and the seed value (2.1) gives \({}^{-q}\Kk_{00}(-\beta)=J_{1+q}/2p\); together with (1.2) they yield the dictionary

\[ {}^{-1-q}\Kk_{ab}(+\beta) = (-1)^b\,\frac{J_q}{2p}\,\frac{\Do_{ab0}(q)}{\Do_{b00}(q-1)}. \tag{D} \]

Substituting (D) into the column recursion (A) (at column \(\ell'{+}1\)) and cancelling the common factor \((-1)^{\ell'}J_q/2p\) collapses everything to physical Doppler operators — the explicit, companion-free middle-index recurrence

\[ \boxed{ \begin{aligned} C_{\ell'+1}\,\Do_{h,\ell'+1,0}(q) =\;& \frac{\Do_{\ell'+1,00}(q{-}1)}{p} \Big[\gamma\,\tfrac{\Do_{h\ell'0}(q)}{\Do_{\ell'00}(q{-}1)} - \tfrac{J_{q-1}}{J_q}\,\tfrac{\Do_{h\ell'0}(q{-}1)}{\Do_{\ell'00}(q{-}2)}\Big] \\ &- C_{\ell'}\,\tfrac{\Do_{\ell'+1,00}(q{-}1)}{\Do_{\ell'-1,00}(q{-}1)}\,\Do_{h,\ell'-1,0}(q). \end{aligned}} \tag{P3} \]

The full derivation, the dictionary proof, and the end-to-end generation are on the N25 page, which also rederives the \(\Do_{020}\) and \(\Do_{121}\) closed forms from these relations as exact symbolic identities.

5  The pure-\(\Do\) closure

With (P3) supplying the \(\ell''{=}0\) border, the remaining moves are companion-free too:

  • (P0) axis ladder — N5 on the axis (\(\ell'{=}\ell''{=}0\)), with the weight companion absorbed into a physical \(\Do\) at shifted \(q\) via the seed value (2.1): \(C_a\Do_{a00}(q)=\tfrac\gamma p\Do_{a-1,00}(q)-\tfrac1p\tfrac{J_{1+q}}{J_{2+q}}\Do_{a-1,00}(q{+}1)-C_{a-1}\Do_{a-2,00}(q)\).
  • (N1) reflection — \(\Do_{h\ell'l}(q)=\Do_{l\ell'h}(-3-q)\), the operator form of the transposition (2.3); supplies the opposite border. Proof on the reflection page.
  • (P2) rank-one fill — because (1.2) factorizes, \(\Do_{h\ell'l}=\Do_{h\ell'0}\Do_{0\ell'l}/\Do_{0\ell'0}\) fills any fixed-\(\ell'\) plane from its two borders.

These four (P0, P3, N1, P2) generate every \(\Do_{h\ell'l}(q)\) from the single seed \(\Do_{000}\) with no object outside the physical family — the subject of N25.
