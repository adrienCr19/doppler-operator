The exact recurrence relations of the Doppler operator

Complete proofs, carried to conclusion, from the aberration kernel. This page is the core of the project. Every central result below is a relation among the physical Doppler operators \(\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q)\) of the literature — no extended weight lattice, no off-diagonal family. The aberration kernel of the boost operator appears only inside the proofs, as the object \(\Do\) is defined from: we establish the kernel's own identities directly from its integral definition and then do every line of algebra to lift them back onto the Doppler operator, cancelling any auxiliary object before the result is stated. Everything is verified to machine zero in 36, 34 and 35.

The recurrence relations (physical Doppler operator only) central result verified, dev = 0

With \(C_\ell=\ell/\sqrt{4\ell^2-1}\), \(\gamma=\cosh\eta\), \(p=\sinh\eta\), \(J_w=\frac{(\gamma+p)^w-(\gamma-p)^w}{w}=\frac{2\sinh(w\eta)}{w}\), and the seed \(\Do_{000}(q)=\frac{J_{2+q}J_{1+q}}{4\gamma p^2}\), the whole catalogue is generated from \(\Do_{000}\) by these four relations — each term a physical \(\Do\):

\[ \textbf{(P0) raise an outer index (on the axis):}\quad C_a\,\Do_{a00}(q) = \frac\gamma p\Do_{a-1,00}(q) - \frac1p\,\frac{J_{1+q}}{J_{2+q}}\,\Do_{a-1,00}(q{+}1) - C_{a-1}\Do_{a-2,00}(q), \] \[ \textbf{(P3) raise the middle index:}\quad C_{\ell'+1}\Do_{h,\ell'+1,0}(q) = \frac{\Do_{\ell'+1,00}(q{-}1)}{p}\Big[\gamma\tfrac{\Do_{h\ell'0}(q)}{\Do_{\ell'00}(q{-}1)} - \tfrac{J_{q-1}}{J_q}\tfrac{\Do_{h\ell'0}(q{-}1)}{\Do_{\ell'00}(q{-}2)}\Big] - C_{\ell'}\tfrac{\Do_{\ell'+1,00}(q{-}1)}{\Do_{\ell'-1,00}(q{-}1)}\Do_{h,\ell'-1,0}(q), \] \[ \textbf{(N1) reflection:}\quad \Do_{h\ell'l}(q)=\Do_{l\ell'h}(-3-q), \qquad \textbf{(P2) rank-one fill:}\quad \Do_{h\ell'l}(q)=\frac{\Do_{h\ell'0}(q)\,\Do_{0\ell'l}(q)}{\Do_{0\ell'0}(q)}. \]

Recipe. P0 climbs the two outer axes from \(\Do_{000}\); P3 climbs the middle index up each \(\ell''{=}0\) column to the border \(\Do_{h\ell'0}\); N1 turns those into the \(\ell{=}0\) border; P2 fills the interior. Every intermediate is a physical Doppler operator. For the \(p\)-series there is also the rapidity ODE (N3), which likewise closes on physical operators at fixed \(\ell'\).

The rest of this page proves each of these from the aberration kernel: §1 the reduction; §2 the four kernel identities; §3 the derivation of P0; §4 of P3; §5 of N1 and P2.

1  The two objects and the reduction

The aberration kernel of the boost operator, at scalar weight \(d\) and \(s=0\), is the matrix element (\(\bar Y_\ell(\mu)=\sqrt{(2\ell+1)/4\pi}\,P_\ell(\mu)\)):

\[ {}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) \;=\; 2\pi\!\int_{-1}^{1} \bar Y_\ell(\mu)\,\bar Y_{\ell'}(\mu')\,\big[\gamma(1+\beta\mu)\big]^{-d}\,\dd\mu, \qquad \mu' = \frac{\mu+\beta}{1+\beta\mu}, \tag{1.1} \]

and \({}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\) is the same with \(\beta\to-\beta\). The Doppler operator is the physically assembled round trip \(\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}=\gamma^{-1}\,{}^{-1}\Bo^m_{\ell\ell'}(\nu,-\beta)\,{}^{0}\Bo^m_{\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta)\); on \(\nu^q\) the boost operators reduce to kernels (Lemma L1: \(\Oh\to-q\)), giving the product that everything below rests on:

\[ \Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q) \;=\; \frac{1}{\gamma}\;{}^{-1-q}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\;{}^{-q}\Kk_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta). \tag{1.2} \]

The outgoing index \(\ell\) is the row of factor 1; the incoming index \(\ell''\) is the column of factor 2; the scattering index \(\ell'\) is shared — the column of factor 1 and the row of factor 2. Factor 2 on the axis is the seed alone, \({}^{-q}\Kk_{00}(-\beta)=J_{1+q}/2p\) (§2.1), which is exactly what lets the outer ladder close on physical operators (§3). The single spectral shift \(q\to q\pm1\) that appears in P0/P3 keeps every object a physical Doppler operator.

2  The four kernel identities, proved from the integral

Two elementary aberration facts, used repeatedly. From \(\mu'=(\mu+\beta)/(1+\beta\mu)\),

\[ 1+\beta\mu = \frac{1}{\gamma^2(1-\beta\mu')}, \qquad \frac{\dd\mu}{\dd\mu'} = \frac{1-\beta^2}{(1-\beta\mu')^2}, \qquad \gamma(1\pm\beta)=\gamma\pm p=e^{\pm\eta}. \tag{2.0} \]

2.1  Seed (the one integral)

With \(\bar Y_0=1/\sqrt{4\pi}\),

\[ {}^{d}\Kk_{00}(-\beta) = \frac{\gamma^{-d}}{2\beta}\,\frac{(1+\beta)^{1-d}-(1-\beta)^{1-d}}{1-d} = \frac{1}{2p(1-d)}\big[(\gamma+p)^{1-d}-(\gamma-p)^{1-d}\big], \]

using \(\gamma^{-d}(1\pm\beta)^{1-d}=\gamma^{-1}(\gamma\pm p)^{1-d}\) and \(\gamma^{-1}/\beta=1/p\). Hence

\[ \boxed{\;{}^{d}\Kk_{00}(\pm\beta) = \frac{J_{1-d}}{2p}\;} \tag{2.1} \]

I1, dev 0

2.2  Parity

Substituting \(\mu\to-\mu\) in (1.1) sends \(\bar Y_\ell(-\mu)=(-1)^\ell\bar Y_\ell\), turns the weight into the \(+\beta\) weight, and the aberrated argument into minus the \(+\beta\) aberration (a further \((-1)^{\ell'}\)):

\[ \boxed{\;{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = (-1)^{\ell+\ell'}\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\;} \tag{2.2} \]

I2, dev 0

2.3  Transposition

Change the integration variable in (1.1) to \(\mu'\). By (2.0) the weight times the Jacobian is \([\gamma(1-\beta\mu')]^{d-2}\), the two harmonics swap roles, and \(\mu(\mu')\) is the \(+\beta\) aberration of \(\mu'\):

\[ \boxed{\;{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = {}^{2-d}\Kk_{\ell'\ell}(+\beta)\;} \tag{2.3} \]

I3, dev 0

2.4  The master \(\ell\)-raising recurrence

Two ingredients. The Legendre relation (matrix element of \(\mu\)):

\[ \mu\,\bar Y_\ell = C_{\ell+1}\,\bar Y_{\ell+1} + C_\ell\,\bar Y_{\ell-1}, \qquad C_\ell=\frac{\ell}{\sqrt{4\ell^2-1}}, \tag{2.4a} \]

(I0, dev 0), and the \(\mu\)-to-weight identity, from \(\mu=\frac1\beta[(1+\beta\mu)-1]\) and \((1+\beta\mu)[\gamma(1+\beta\mu)]^{-d}=\gamma^{-1}[\gamma(1+\beta\mu)]^{-(d-1)}\):

\[ \mu\,\big[\gamma(1+\beta\mu)\big]^{-d} = \frac1\beta\Big[\gamma^{-1}\big[\gamma(1+\beta\mu)\big]^{-(d-1)} - \big[\gamma(1+\beta\mu)\big]^{-d}\Big] \tag{2.4b} \]

(I4a, dev 0). Insert (2.4a) under the integral; the extra \(\mu\) multiplies the weight, and (2.4b) turns it into two kernels of adjacent weight. Equating the two evaluations of \(2\pi\!\int(\mu\bar Y_\ell)\bar Y_{\ell'}(\mu')\,[\gamma(1+\beta\mu)]^{-d}\dd\mu\) and shifting \(\ell+1\to\ell\):

\[ \boxed{\;C_\ell\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = \frac1p\,{}^{d-1}\Kk_{\ell-1,\ell'}(-\beta) - \frac{\gamma}{p}\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell-1,\ell'}(-\beta) - C_{\ell-1}\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell-2,\ell'}(-\beta)\;} \tag{KR} \]

I4, dev 0. The \(+\beta\) form follows by multiplying through by \((-1)^{\ell+\ell'}\) and applying parity (2.2) term by term (the \((\ell-1)\)-terms flip sign):

\[ {}^{d}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = \frac{1}{C_\ell p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell-1,\ell'}(+\beta) - {}^{d-1}\Kk_{\ell-1,\ell'}(+\beta)\Big] - \frac{C_{\ell-1}}{C_\ell}\,{}^{d}\Kk_{\ell-2,\ell'}(+\beta). \tag{KR$^+$} \]

I4+, dev 0. These four identities — seed, parity, transposition, master recurrence — are everything the Doppler recurrences are built from.

3  Raising an outer index — P0, in physical \(\Do\)

Take the axis element \(\Do_{a00}(q)=\gamma^{-1}\,{}^{-1-q}\Kk_{a0}(+\beta)\,{}^{-q}\Kk_{00}(-\beta)\). Its first factor has row \(a\); apply (KR\(^+\)) at weight \(-1-q\) to raise it:

\[ {}^{-1-q}\Kk_{a0}(+\beta) = \frac{1}{C_a p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{-1-q}\Kk_{a-1,0}(+\beta) - {}^{-2-q}\Kk_{a-1,0}(+\beta)\Big] - \frac{C_{a-1}}{C_a}\,{}^{-1-q}\Kk_{a-2,0}(+\beta). \]

Multiply by \(\tfrac1\gamma\,{}^{-q}\Kk_{00}(-\beta)\). The \(\gamma\)- and \(C_{a-1}\)-terms are already physical: \(\gamma^{-1}{}^{-1-q}\Kk_{a-1,0}(+\beta)\,{}^{-q}\Kk_{00}(-\beta)=\Do_{a-1,00}(q)\), likewise \(\Do_{a-2,00}(q)\). The one weight-shifted companion \({}^{-2-q}\Kk_{a-1,0}(+\beta)\) is where a naïve treatment would leave the physical family — but on the axis it does not, because factor 2 is the seed. Using (2.1), \({}^{-q}\Kk_{00}(-\beta)=J_{1+q}/2p\) while \({}^{-(q+1)}\Kk_{00}(-\beta)=J_{2+q}/2p\), so

\[ \frac{1}{\gamma}\,{}^{-2-q}\Kk_{a-1,0}(+\beta)\,{}^{-q}\Kk_{00}(-\beta) = \frac{1}{\gamma}\,{}^{-1-(q+1)}\Kk_{a-1,0}(+\beta)\,{}^{-(q+1)}\Kk_{00}(-\beta)\cdot\frac{J_{1+q}/2p}{J_{2+q}/2p} = \frac{J_{1+q}}{J_{2+q}}\,\Do_{a-1,00}(q{+}1). \]

The companion has become a physical Doppler operator at spectral index \(q+1\) (verified, dev 0). Substituting:

\[ \boxed{\;C_a\,\Do_{a00}(q) = \frac{\gamma}{p}\,\Do_{a-1,00}(q) - \frac{1}{p}\,\frac{J_{1+q}}{J_{2+q}}\,\Do_{a-1,00}(q{+}1) - C_{a-1}\,\Do_{a-2,00}(q)\;} \tag{P0} \]

— a three-term ladder in physical Doppler operators, the mirror form for \(\Do_{00b}\) following identically with \(J_{2+q}/J_{1+q}\) and \(q\to q-1\). harness 34, P0a/P0b, dev 0 \(\blacksquare\)

Off the axis the same kernel step leaves a companion that is not a single physical element (factor 2 is no longer the seed); the honest options are then either the pure rank-one route (P2, §5, which fills any plane from its two borders and needs no outer ladder at all) or, at the kernel level, the weight-shifted companion of the raising relations N5/N6 — a scaffolding device, not a Doppler operator, kept off the central results and confined to the N5/N6 worked climb.

4  Raising the middle index — P3, in physical \(\Do\)

The shared index is the hard one: a first-order move touches both factors of (1.2). The kernel step that raises the column \(\ell'\) of factor 1 is (KR) transposed (2.3),

\[ {}^{a}\Kk_{h,\ell'+1}(+\beta) = -\frac{1}{C_{\ell'+1} p}\Big[\gamma\,{}^{a}\Kk_{h,\ell'}(+\beta) - {}^{a+1}\Kk_{h,\ell'}(+\beta)\Big] - \frac{C_{\ell'}}{C_{\ell'+1}}\,{}^{a}\Kk_{h,\ell'-1}(+\beta), \tag{A} \]

(R-A, dev 0). The remaining kernel factors are converted to physical operators by a dictionary built from parity (2.2) and the seed (2.1): since \({}^{-q}\Kk_{b0}(-\beta)=(-1)^b\,{}^{-q}\Kk_{b0}(+\beta)\) and \(\Do_{b00}(q-1)=\gamma^{-1}{}^{-q}\Kk_{b0}(+\beta)\cdot J_q/2p\),

\[ {}^{-1-q}\Kk_{ab}(+\beta) = (-1)^b\,\frac{J_q}{2p}\,\frac{\Do_{ab0}(q)}{\Do_{b00}(q-1)}. \tag{D} \]

Substituting (D) into (A) at column \(\ell'{+}1\) and cancelling the common factor \((-1)^{\ell'}J_q/2p\) leaves a relation among physical Doppler operators only — no off-diagonal element ever appears:

\[ \boxed{ \begin{aligned} C_{\ell'+1}\,\Do_{h,\ell'+1,0}(q) =\;& \frac{\Do_{\ell'+1,00}(q{-}1)}{p} \Big[\gamma\,\tfrac{\Do_{h\ell'0}(q)}{\Do_{\ell'00}(q{-}1)} - \tfrac{J_{q-1}}{J_q}\,\tfrac{\Do_{h\ell'0}(q{-}1)}{\Do_{\ell'00}(q{-}2)}\Big] \\ &- C_{\ell'}\,\tfrac{\Do_{\ell'+1,00}(q{-}1)}{\Do_{\ell'-1,00}(q{-}1)}\,\Do_{h,\ell'-1,0}(q). \end{aligned}} \tag{P3} \]

harness 34, P3, dev 1.3×10⁻³⁷. The price of staying in the physical family is that the relation is nonlinear (ratios); this is unavoidable, and the ratios are precisely the reabsorbed companions. The full derivation and the end-to-end generation are on the N25 page. \(\blacksquare\)

A purely linear \(\ell'\)-relation cannot exist among diagonal Doppler operators; forcing linearity introduces an off-diagonal companion family \(X\) — a scaffolding device kept off the central results and confined to the N10 page. P3 is the price-free physical statement.

5  Reflection and rank-one fill — N1, P2

Reflection (N1). The transposition (2.3), applied to both factors of (1.2) with the weight bookkeeping \(-1-q\leftrightarrow -q\) under \(q\to-3-q\), exchanges the outer indices:

\[ \boxed{\;\Do_{h\ell'l}(q)=\Do_{l\ell'h}(-3-q)\;} \]

(verified, dev 0; operator form \(\Do(\Oh)=\Do^{\mathsf T}(3-\Oh)\), full proof on the reflection page). It supplies the \(\ell{=}0\) border from the \(\ell''{=}0\) one.

Rank-one fill (P2). Because (1.2) is a product of an \(h\)-vector (factor 1) and an \(l\)-vector (factor 2) sharing \(\ell'\), the ratios \(\Do_{h\ell'l}/\Do_{0\ell'l}\) and \(\Do_{h\ell'l}/\Do_{h\ell'0}\) are independent of \(l\) and \(h\) respectively; hence

\[ \boxed{\;\Do_{h\ell'l}(q)=\frac{\Do_{h\ell'0}(q)\,\Do_{0\ell'l}(q)}{\Do_{0\ell'0}(q)}\;} \]

fills any fixed-\(\ell'\) plane from its two borders. harness 34, P2, exact

Together P0, P3, N1, P2 generate every \(\Do_{h\ell'l}(q)\) from the seed \(\Do_{000}\), with the physical Doppler operator the only object at every step. As a check, this scheme rederives the \(\Do_{020}\) and \(\Do_{121}\) closed forms exactly, matching the published expressions as symbolic identities (harness 35).
