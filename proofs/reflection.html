N1 — Reflection symmetry \(\;\Do(\Oh) = \Do^{\mathsf T}(3-\Oh)\)

Statement
\[ \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta)\Big|_{\Oh} \;=\; \Do^m_{\ell''\ell'\ell}(\nu,\beta)\Big|_{\Oh\to 3-\Oh} \qquad (s = 0,\ \text{any } m,\ \text{same } \beta). \]

Corollary N1a. Diagonal elements \(\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell}\) are invariant under \(\Oh \to 3-\Oh\); every coefficient of their expansion in \(p = \gamma\beta\) is a polynomial in the diffusion operator \(\Dv = \Oh^2 - 3\Oh = -\Oh(3-\Oh)\).

Corollary N1b. The same holds for all \(m\)-summed and thermally averaged diagonal operators — proving the empirical observation of [RC26, §2.3] that “all the required operators are linear combinations of powers of the diffusion operator \(\Dv\)”.

Step 1 — the kernel transposition identity

Claim (B3, transposition form of this site; equivalent to [CR25, Eq. 13a] after convention translation):

\[ {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) \;=\; {}^{2-d}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell}(+\beta). \]

Proof. Start from the definition (\(s=0\); the azimuthal integration is trivial and the \(\mu\)-integrands below are real),

\[ {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = \int \dd^2\hat y'\; \frac{Y^*_{\ell m}(\mu')\, Y_{\ell' m}(\mu_+[\mu'])}{D_+(\mu')^{\,d}}, \qquad D_\pm(\mu) \equiv \gamma(1\pm\beta\mu),\quad \mu_\pm[\mu] = \frac{\mu\pm\beta}{1\pm\beta\mu}. \]

Change variables to the boosted direction \(\mu_2 = \mu_+[\mu']\), i.e. \(\mu' = \mu_-[\mu_2]\). Two standard identities of the Lorentz angle map:

\[ \frac{\dd\mu'}{\dd\mu_2} = \frac{1-\beta^2}{(1-\beta\mu_2)^2} = \frac{1}{D_-(\mu_2)^2}, \qquad D_+(\mu') = \gamma\big(1+\beta\,\mu_-[\mu_2]\big) = \frac{\gamma(1-\beta^2)}{1-\beta\mu_2} = \frac{1}{D_-(\mu_2)} . \]

Substituting,

\[ {}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = \int \dd^2\hat y_2\; Y^*_{\ell' m}(\mu_2)\, Y_{\ell m}(\mu_-[\mu_2])\; D_-(\mu_2)^{\,d}\, D_-(\mu_2)^{-2} = \int \dd^2\hat y_2\; \frac{Y^*_{\ell' m}(\mu_2)\, Y_{\ell m}(\mu_-[\mu_2])}{D_-(\mu_2)^{\,2-d}} , \]

which is precisely \({}^{2-d}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell}(+\beta)\) (the definition with \(\beta\to-\beta\) has the \(\mu_-\) map and the \(D_-\) weight). Complex conjugation is immaterial since the \(\mu\)-integrands are real for every \(m\). \(\blacksquare\)

Verified numerically as check D2 of 02_doppler.wl (max deviation \(6\times10^{-28}\) over \(d \in \{-1,0,\tfrac12,1,2\}\), \(m\in\{0,1\}\), \(\ell,\ell'\le3\)). Note the identity flips both the weight \(d\to2-d\) and the sign of \(\beta\) — in the convention-checking run, the same-\(\beta\) version failed (harness check C5), which is how the correct form was pinned down.

Step 2 — the two-line proof of N1

Work on power laws (Lemma L1), \(\Oh \to -q\):

\[ \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q) = \frac{1}{\gamma}\, {}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\; {}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta). \]

Apply Step 1 to each factor (once in each direction):

\[ {}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta) = {}^{3+q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell}(-\beta), \qquad {}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta) = {}^{2+q}\Kk^m_{\ell''\ell'}(+\beta). \]

Therefore

\[ \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q) = \frac{1}{\gamma}\,{}^{2+q}\Kk^m_{\ell''\ell'}(+\beta)\;{}^{3+q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell}(-\beta) = \Do^m_{\ell''\ell'\ell}(q')\Big|_{q' = -3-q}, \]

where the last equality is read off by matching against the power-law form with \(-1-q' = 2+q\) and \(-q' = 3+q\), both giving \(q' = -3-q\) consistently. In eigenvalue language \(q' = -3-q\) is exactly \(\Oh \to 3-\Oh\) (since \(\Oh\nu^q = -q\nu^q\)). By Lemma L1(ii) this lifts to the operator statement. \(\blacksquare\)

Step 3 — Corollaries

N1a. Setting \(\ell'' = \ell\): \(\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell}(\Oh) = \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell}(3-\Oh)\). Each Taylor coefficient in \(p\) is a polynomial in \(\Oh\) (Lemma L1(ii)); a polynomial invariant under the reflection \(x \to 3-x\) is a polynomial in the invariant \((x-\tfrac32)^2\), equivalently in \(x(x-3) = (x-\tfrac32)^2 - \tfrac94\). Since \(\Oh(\Oh-3) = \Dv\), every coefficient is a polynomial in \(\Dv\). \(\blacksquare\)

N1b. The reflection acts only on the frequency variable; summation over \(m\) and thermal averaging over the electron momentum distribution \(f(p)\) act on other variables and commute with it. Physical content: the fixed point \(\Oh = 3/2\) separates the photon-number-like and energy-like weights (\(\nu^{3/2}\) is the self-dual spectrum); the invariant combination \(\Dv\) is the unique second-order operator that preserves photon number, which is why only it can appear on the diagonal of a scattering round trip.

Consistency and verification

  • Numerical: check N1 of 02_doppler.wl, max deviation \(7.2\times10^{-28}\) over \(q \in \{-0.7, 0.5, 1.3\}\), \(m \in \{0,1\}\), all \(\ell,\ell',\ell'' \le 3\).
  • Symbolic: check N9 — all diagonal elements generated by the ODE (N3) to \(\mathcal O(p^4)\) are exactly invariant under \(q \to -3-q\).
  • Visible in the tables: e.g. \(\Do_{023} \propto (\Oh-4)^2(\Oh-3)\) versus \(\Do_{320} \propto \Oh(\Oh+1)^2\), and indeed \((3-\Oh)-4 = -(\Oh+1)\), \((3-\Oh)-3 = -\Oh\).
  • The rapidity ODE (N3) is manifestly covariant under N1, an independent structural check.
