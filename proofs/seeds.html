N7 — Seeds and a finite exact-generation algorithm

Statement

Every element \({}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta)\) — in particular every physical Doppler-operator element — is computable exactly and in finitely many algebraic steps from two closed-form kernel seeds. As a by-product, the corner elements admit the new closed form (\(s=0\), any \(m \ge 0\)):

\[ \Do^m_{mmm}(\nu,\beta) \;=\; \gamma^{-2(m+\Oh)}\; {}_2F_1\!\Big(\tfrac{m-1+\Oh}{2},\tfrac{m+\Oh}{2};\tfrac32+m;\beta^2\Big)\; {}_2F_1\!\Big(\tfrac{m+\Oh}{2},\tfrac{m+\Oh+1}{2};\tfrac32+m;\beta^2\Big), \]

which for \(m=0\) reduces to the elementary closed form of [CR26, Eq. 10a].

The two seeds (verified)

Seed 1[CR26, Eqs. 9a/B1] verified 9×10⁻³⁰
\[ {}^{d}\Kk^0_{00}(-\beta) = \frac{(\gamma+p)^{1-d} - (\gamma-p)^{1-d}}{2(1-d)\,p} \]
Seed 2[RC26, Eq. B4] verified 9×10⁻²⁹
\[ {}^{d}\Kk^m_{mm}(-\beta) = \frac{1}{\gamma^{m+d}}\; {}_2F_1\!\Big(\tfrac{m+d}{2},\tfrac{m+d+1}{2};\tfrac32+m;\beta^2\Big) \qquad (s=0,\ m \ge 0). \]

Seed 1 is the \(m=0\) case of Seed 2 via the quadratic hypergeometric identity \({}_2F_1(a, a+\tfrac12; \tfrac32; z^2) = \frac{(1+z)^{1-2a} - (1-z)^{1-2a}}{2z(1-2a)}\) with \(a = d/2\), \(z=\beta\) (and \(\gamma^{-d}(1\pm\beta)^{1-d}\gamma^{d-1} = (\gamma\pm p)^{1-d}/\gamma^{\,0}\)-type rearrangement). When transcribing [RC26, Eq. B4], note that the printed prefactor belongs to the intermediate integral representation; the final \({}_2F_1\) form carries only \(\gamma^{-(m+d)}\) — this was pinned down numerically (harness checks C3 → D1).

The algorithm

To obtain \({}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\) exactly at scalar weight \(q\) (hence, by Lemma L1, as an operator to any order):

  1. Seed: evaluate \({}^{d}\Kk^m_{mm}(-\beta)\) for the finitely many weights \(d \in \{-1-j-q - t\} \cup \{-k-q - t\}\), \(t = 0,1,\dots\) required below (Seed 2; each a single \({}_2F_1\)).
  2. Raise the row index: apply the \(\ell\)-raising recursion (B10) — which couples weights \(d\) and \(d-1\), whence the finite list of \(t\)-shifted seeds — to build the kernel columns \({}^{-1-j-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\,\ell'}(\pm\beta)\) and \({}^{2+k+q}\Kk^m_{\ell''\ell'}(\pm\beta)\) up to the required \(\ell,\ell''\). (Parity (B3) converts between \(\pm\beta\) versions; transposition converts row- to column-generation, exactly as in the proofs of N5/N6.)
  3. Assemble: \({}^{(j,k)}\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q) = \tfrac1\gamma\, {}^{-1-j-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(+\beta)\; {}^{-k-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta)\).

Total cost: \(\mathcal O(\ell + \ell'')\) recursion steps and two seed evaluations per element — no integrals. For series tables in \(p\) the rapidity ODE (N3) is even leaner (pure polynomial sweeps); the algorithm above is the exact/closed-form route, valid at all \(\beta\).

Derivation of the corner closed form

For \(\ell = \ell' = \ell'' = m\) no raising is needed. On \(\nu^q\):

\[ \Do^m_{mmm}(q) = \frac1\gamma\,{}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{mm}(+\beta)\; {}^{-q}\Kk^m_{mm}(-\beta) = \frac1\gamma\,{}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{mm}(-\beta)\; {}^{-q}\Kk^m_{mm}(-\beta), \]

using parity (\((-1)^{m+m}=1\)) on the first factor. Inserting Seed 2 twice:

\[ \Do^m_{mmm}(q) = \frac{\gamma^{-(m-1-q)}\,\gamma^{-(m-q)}}{\gamma}\; {}_2F_1\!\Big(\tfrac{m-1-q}{2},\tfrac{m-q}{2};\tfrac32+m;\beta^2\Big)\, {}_2F_1\!\Big(\tfrac{m-q}{2},\tfrac{m-q+1}{2};\tfrac32+m;\beta^2\Big) = \gamma^{2(q-m)}\,{}_2F_1(\cdot)\,{}_2F_1(\cdot), \]

and \(q \to -\Oh\) gives the boxed operator form. For \(m=0\) one can instead insert Seed 1 twice (weights \(d = -1-q\) and \(d = -q\), i.e. exponents \(1-d = 2+q\) and \(1+q\)):

\[ \Do^0_{000}(q) = \frac{\big[(\gamma+p)^{2+q}-(\gamma-p)^{2+q}\big]\big[(\gamma+p)^{1+q}-(\gamma-p)^{1+q}\big]} {4(2+q)(1+q)\,\gamma\,p^2}. \]

Expanding the numerator and using \((\gamma+p)(\gamma-p) = 1\), the cross terms collapse: \((\gamma+p)^{2+q}(\gamma-p)^{1+q} = (\gamma+p)\) and \((\gamma+p)^{1+q}(\gamma-p)^{2+q} = (\gamma-p)\), so the numerator equals \((\gamma+p)^{3+2q} + (\gamma-p)^{3+2q} - 2\gamma\) — exactly the compact form of [CR26, Eq. 10a], \(\Do^0_{000} = \frac{(\gamma+p)^{3+2q} + (\gamma-p)^{3+2q} - 2\gamma}{4(2+q)(1+q)\gamma p^2}\). Both forms were verified against direct quadrature (harness C11, \(2\times10^{-29}\)).
