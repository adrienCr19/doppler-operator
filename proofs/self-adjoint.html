N12 — The origin of the reflection symmetry: self-adjointness on photon phase space

N1 was discovered algebraically (\(\Do(\Oh) = \Do^{\mathsf T}(3-\Oh)\)) and proved from the kernel transposition identity. This page identifies what it is: the statement that the single-scattering Doppler operator is self-adjoint on the photon phase-space Hilbert space

\[ \mathcal H \;=\; L^2\big(\mathbb R_+,\, \nu^2\dd\nu\big) \otimes L^2(S^2), \]

whose measure \(\nu^2\dd\nu\,\dd\hat y\) is the photon-mode density. The two "mysterious" numbers of the symmetry are dimensions: the frequency shadow \(\Oh \to 3-\Oh\) carries the \(3\) of momentum space, the sphere transposition \(d \to 2-d\) carries the \(2\) of the celestial sphere.

Lemma 1 — the frequency shadow: \(\Oh^\dagger = 3-\Oh\)

On \(L^2(\nu^2\dd\nu)\), integrate by parts:

\[ \langle f, \Oh g\rangle = -\!\int_0^\infty\! f\,(\nu\partial_\nu g)\;\nu^2\dd\nu = -\!\int_0^\infty\! \nu^3 f\,\partial_\nu g\;\dd\nu = \int_0^\infty\! g\,\partial_\nu\big(\nu^3 f\big)\,\dd\nu = \int_0^\infty\! g\,\big(3f + \nu\partial_\nu f\big)\,\nu^2\dd\nu = \langle (3-\Oh)f,\, g\rangle . \]

Equivalently, for dilations: \(\langle f, e^{a\Oh}g\rangle = \int f(\nu)g(e^{-a}\nu)\nu^2\dd\nu = e^{3a}\!\int f(e^{a}\mu)g(\mu)\mu^2\dd\mu = \langle e^{a(3-\Oh)}f, g\rangle\) (substitution \(\mu = e^{-a}\nu\); the \(e^{3a}\) is the Jacobian of the phase-space measure). Hence \(f(\Oh)^\dagger = f(3-\Oh)\) for real-coefficient operator functions. check S1 (exact). The self-dual spectra are \(\nu^{q}\) with \(q = -3/2\) (eigenvalue at the fixed point \(\Oh = 3/2\)): precisely the spectra for which \(|f|^2\nu^2\dd\nu = \dd\nu/\nu\) is scale-invariant.

Lemma 2 — the sphere shadow: transposition = conformal adjoint

A boost acts on the celestial sphere as a conformal (Möbius) transformation \(\hat y \mapsto \hat y_+[\hat y]\) whose conformal factor is the Doppler factor: \(\dd\hat y_+/\dd\hat y = D_+(\mu)^{-2}\) (the aberration Jacobian used throughout, e.g. in the N1 proof). A Doppler-weight-\(d\) field is a conformal density of weight \(d\), and the \(L^2(S^2)\) pairing of a weight-\(d\) with a weight-\((2-d)\) field is boost-invariant — the Jacobian exponent \(2\) is saturated. The matrix expression of this adjointness in the orthonormal harmonic basis is exactly the verified transposition identity \({}^{d}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(-\beta) = {}^{2-d}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell}(+\beta)\) (B3): the transpose of a boost at weight \(d\) is the inverse boost at the shadow weight \(2-d\). This is the same weight reflection \(\Delta \to 2-\Delta\) familiar as the shadow transform of conformal field theory on \(S^2\).

Theorem — \(\Do^\dagger = \Do\), and it is N1

Take the adjoint of \(\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta)\) on \(\mathcal H\): the frequency part maps \(\Oh \to 3-\Oh\) (Lemma 1), the sphere part transposes the multipole matrix, \((\ell,\ell'')\to(\ell'',\ell)\) (orthonormal basis; Lemma 2 supplies the weight bookkeeping that makes the round trip transpose consistently). Hence

\[ \big[\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta)\big]^\dagger \;=\; \Do^m_{\ell''\ell'\ell}(\nu,\beta)\Big|_{\Oh\to3-\Oh} \;\overset{\text{N1}}{=}\; \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta). \]

So N1 \(\iff\) self-adjointness of the scattering round trip on photon phase space. \(\blacksquare\)

Direct verification on non-power-law spectra S2/S3 (exact)

Self-adjointness was checked without any power-law reduction, using a new integral representation that makes the closed forms act on arbitrary spectra:

\[ J_{w-\Oh}\,g(\nu) \;=\; \int_{-\eta}^{\eta} e^{w u}\, g\big(e^{u}\nu\big)\,\dd u \qquad\Big(\text{from } J_w = \int_{-\eta}^{\eta}e^{wu}\dd u \text{ and } e^{-u\Oh}g(\nu) = g(e^u\nu)\Big), \]

so e.g. \(\Do_{101}g(\nu) = \frac{3}{4\gamma p^4}\iint_{[-\eta,\eta]^2} (\gamma e^{2u} - e^{3u})(\gamma e^{v} - 1)\,g(e^{u+v}\nu)\,\dd u\,\dd v\). With \(f = e^{-\nu}\), \(g = e^{-2\nu}\): \(\langle f, \Do_{101}g\rangle = \langle \Do_{101}f, g\rangle\) (S2) and the off-diagonal adjoint pair \(\langle f, \Do_{001}g\rangle = \langle \Do_{100}f, g\rangle\) (S3), both to full quadrature precision. Analytically, the substitution \(\mu = e^{u+v}\nu\) maps each weight to \(\widetilde W(u) = e^{3u}W(-u)\), which sends \(\gamma e^{2u}-e^{3u} \leftrightarrow \gamma e^{u}-1\): the adjoint swaps the two bracket factors — the same swap that appears in every reflection check of the closed forms (F7, F15).

Corollaries and physical reading

  • Why \(\Dv\) and only \(\Dv\) (N1a, explained). \(\Dv = \Oh(\Oh-3) = -(\Oh-\tfrac32)^2 + \tfrac94\) is the canonical self-adjoint quadratic in \(\Oh\) — the Casimir of the frequency dilation sector. Diagonal (hence symmetric) blocks of a self-adjoint operator that are functions of \(\Oh\) alone must be functions of the invariant combination: the empirical "\(\Dv\)-structure" of the SZ tables is representation theory, not accident.
  • Kernel symmetry. Self-adjointness on \(\nu^2\dd\nu\) is the operator form of the classical symmetry of the Compton scattering kernel in logarithmic frequency (cf. the symmetrized kernels of Sazonov & Sunyaev 2000): reciprocity of the Thomson round trip. It guarantees a real spectral decomposition of the scattering operator — the natural starting point for multiple-scattering expansions.
  • Duality of spectra. The reflection pairs \(\nu^{q}\) with \(\nu^{-3-q}\); the product is \(\nu^{-3}\), the scale-invariant phase-space density. In particular it exchanges "occupation-like" and "intensity-like" power laws (\(I_\nu \propto \nu^3 n\)), tying the shadow to the \(d=0\) vs \(d=3\) Doppler weights of \(n\) and \(I_\nu\).
  • Two shadows, one identity. The sphere shadow \(d \to 2-d\) (transposition B3) and the frequency shadow \(\Oh \to 3-\Oh\) (Lemma 1) are the same construction on the two factors of phase space; N1 is their product. This predicts the form of the spin-weighted generalization: polarized fields are still conformal densities on \(S^2\), so the polarized N1 should hold with the same \(3-\Oh\) and the \(s\to-s\) flip of the spin-weighted pairing — a concrete target for the \(s=\pm2\) extension.
