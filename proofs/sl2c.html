N18 — The Lorentz-group dictionary: boosts as celestial conformal maps, unitarity of the boost operator, and the principal series

This page makes precise what the whole framework has been hinting at: the aberration kernel and boost operator are representation theory of the Lorentz group \(SL(2,\mathbb C)\) acting on the celestial sphere, the Doppler weight is a conformal dimension, and the physically natural bases are the unitary principal series. Every statement is verified numerically (harness 19), including the first computation of aberration kernels at complex Doppler weight.

1  The dictionary

(N18a) Boosts are celestial dilations U1 (exact)

In the stereographic coordinate \(z = e^{i\phi}\tan(\theta/2)\) on the celestial sphere, the aberration map \(\mu \to \mu' = \frac{\mu+\beta}{1+\beta\mu}\) is exactly

\[ \tan\tfrac{\theta'}{2} = e^{-\eta}\,\tan\tfrac{\theta}{2} \qquad\Longleftrightarrow\qquad z \;\to\; e^{-\eta} z : \]

a boost of rapidity \(\eta\) along \(\hat z\) is a dilation of the celestial plane — the simplest Möbius transformation. A Doppler-weight-\(d\), spin-\(s\) field is a conformal primary of dimension and spin \((\Delta, J) = (d, s)\): the Doppler factor is the conformal factor of the map (the aberration Jacobian \(\dd\hat y'/\dd\hat y = D^{-2}\) used throughout), and the verified transposition identity B3 (\(d \to 2-d\), inverse boost) is the shadow transform \(\Delta \to 2-\Delta\) of two-dimensional conformal field theory — as identified in N12. The kernel \({}^{d}_{s}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\beta)\) is therefore a matrix element of an \(SL(2,\mathbb C)\) representation of weight \((d, s)\) in the harmonic basis.

2  Unitarity of the boost operator

(N18b) General-\(d\) unitarity theorem new U2, U3

For a Doppler-weight-\(d\) field, define the weight-matched inner product \(\langle X, Y\rangle_d = \int \bar X Y\; \nu^{1-2d}\,\dd\nu\,\dd\hat y\) (for \(d = 0\): \(\nu\,\dd\nu\,\dd\hat y = \dd^3k/\nu\), the Lorentz-invariant photon phase-space measure; for \(d = 1\): the scale-invariant \(\dd\nu/\nu\)). Then

\[ \boxed{\;{}^{d}\Bo(\nu,\beta)^\dagger \;=\; {}^{d}\Bo(\nu,\beta)^{-1} \quad\text{on } L^2\big(\nu^{1-2d}\dd\nu \otimes S^2\big) \quad\text{for every } d\;} \]

Proof (three verified ingredients): on \(L^2(\nu^{1-2d}\dd\nu)\) integration by parts gives \(\Oh^\dagger = (2-2d) - \Oh\); the sphere transpose is the shadow (B3), \({}^{w}\Kk_{\ell'\ell}(-\beta) = {}^{2-w}\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(\beta)\); applying both to \({}^{d}\Bo = \Kk^{d+\Oh}(-\beta)\) gives weight \(2-d-\big((2-2d)-\Oh\big) = d+\Oh\) at argument \(+\beta\) — which is \({}^{d}\Bo^{-1}\) by the inversion law (B4). \(\blacksquare\) Numerically: the \(d=1\) kernel is an orthogonal matrix (row orthonormality, U2), and the explicit position-space boost of weight-\(d\) test fields preserves \(\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle_d\) for \(d = 0, 1, 2\) (U3). Physical reading: Lorentz transformations act unitarily on the photon field over invariant phase space — the boost operator is a unitary representation of the boost subgroup, for every Doppler weight.

3  The principal series, and why the weight drops out

(N18c) Mellin decomposition: every weight sees the same principal series new U4a/b

The unitary line of \(\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle_d\) consists of the scale-covariant modes \(\nu^{q}\) with \(q = d - 1 + ik\), \(k \in \mathbb R\) (these make \(|\nu^q|^2\nu^{1-2d}\dd\nu\) scale-invariant). On such a mode the boost operator evaluates to a kernel of weight

\[ d - q \;=\; 1 - ik \;\equiv\; \Delta(k), \]

independent of \(d\): whatever the Doppler weight of the field, its unitary Mellin modes are boosted by the aberration kernel at complex weight \(\Delta = 1 - ik\) — precisely the unitary principal series of \(SL(2,\mathbb C)\) (\(\Delta = 1 + i\lambda\) in the standard normalization). The Doppler weight is pure bookkeeping; the representation content is universal. We computed these complex-weight kernels (apparently for the first time) and verified both defining properties at \(\Delta = 1 - 0.7i\), \(\beta = 0.3\): the inversion law (U4a, truncation-limited \(10^{-18}\)) and unitarity \(\overline{\Kk^{\Delta}(-\beta)}^{\mathsf T} = \Kk^{\Delta}(+\beta)\) (U4b, exact) — the latter following structurally from B3 + B4 + \(\bar\Delta = 2-\Delta\) on the principal line.

4  First payoff: the Compton spectrum on the celestial line

(N18d) The Hermitian scattering matrix and its real spectrum new — first computation U5a–c

On the scattering-adjoint line \(q = -\tfrac32 + ik\) (the unitary line of the \(\nu^2\dd\nu\) measure of N12; the reflection \(q \to -3-q\) is \(k \to -k\), i.e. complex conjugation), the exact single-momentum Thomson operator

\[ T(k)_{\ell\ell''} = \Do_{\ell 0 \ell''} + \tfrac1{10}\Do_{\ell 2 \ell''} - \Big[\delta_{\ell\ell''} - \beta\big(C_{\ell''+1}\delta_{\ell,\ell''+1} + C_{\ell''}\delta_{\ell,\ell''-1}\big)\Big] \]

is Hermitian per element (U5a, \(4\times10^{-25}\) — N1 in its unitary frame), so the exact Compton “dispersion relation” \(\lambda_\ell(k;\beta)\) is real. First values (\(k = 0.7\), \(\beta = 0.3\), truncated at \(\ell \le 8\)):

\[ \lambda \;=\; \{\,0.000000,\; -0.693880,\; -0.756761,\; -0.853744,\; -0.959719,\;\dots\} \]

— a damping spectrum with a marginal (zero) mode, exact to \(10^{-14}\) and persisting at other \(k\) (U5c): the Mellin-space fingerprint of a conservation law of the Thomson round trip. Identifying this mode and mapping \(\lambda_\ell(k)\) across \((k, p)\) — which turns multiple scattering into exact exponentiation \(e^{\tau T(k)}\) — is the spectral-theory frontier that this dictionary opens; all required ingredients (closed forms at complex weight, Hermiticity, the F-calculus for thermal averages) are now in place and verified.

5  The N15 lemma as a trace identity, and the celestial bridge

In this language the one unproven lemma of N15 becomes transparent as a trace statement: the \(m\)-summed mixed diagonal is \(\mathrm{Tr}_{m}\big[\Pi_\ell\, A_{s_1}\, P_{\ell'}\, B_{s_2}\,\Pi_\ell\big]\), a rotational scalar built from representation operators, and the claimed \((s_1,s_2) \leftrightarrow (s_2,s_1)\) symmetry is a character-like cyclicity property that fails before tracing (P10b!) and holds after — exactly the behaviour of trace identities. What blocks naive cyclicity is that spin-0 and spin-2 kernels act on different bundles; the missing ingredient is the intertwining isometry between them (the \(\eth\)-operators). This is now a sharply posed representation-theory problem rather than a computational mystery.

Finally, the bridge outward: the modes \(\nu^{-1+ik}\) on the celestial sphere are exactly the conformal primary basis of the celestial-amplitudes programme [PSS17], with \(\Delta\) on the principal series.
