N4 — Sum rule \(\;\sum_{\ell'}\Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \delta_{\ell\ell''} - \beta\,[\dots]\)

Statement first given in [CR25, §4.2 (after Eq. 48)]; [RC26, Eqs. 10–11]; re-derived here in the site convention
\[ \sum_{\ell'} \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(\nu,\beta) \;=\; \delta_{\ell\ell''} \;-\; \beta\Big[C^m_{\ell''+1}\,\delta_{\ell,\ell''+1} + C^m_{\ell''}\,\delta_{\ell,\ell''-1}\Big] \qquad (s=0). \]

Remarkably, the right-hand side is a pure number — all \(\Oh\)-dependence cancels in the sum over the scattering multipole.

Proof

Work on power laws (Lemma L1). Two verified ingredients:

(a) Weight raising, row form (B6; harness check C7), applied at weight \(d = -1-q\):

\[ {}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\beta) = \gamma\,{}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\beta) - \gamma\beta\Big[C^m_{\ell+1}\,{}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell+1,\ell'}(\beta) + C^m_{\ell}\,{}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(\beta)\Big]. \]

This is the scalar-weight form of the boost-operator statement \({}^{-1}\Bo(\nu,-\beta)/\gamma = {}^{0}\Bo(\nu,-\beta) - \beta[\,C\,{}^{0}\Bo\,]\)-type reduction [CR25, Eq. 49].

(b) Inversion (B4; harness check C10): at any common weight \(a\), \(\sum_{\ell'} {}^{a}\Kk^m_{L\ell'}(\beta)\, {}^{a}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta) = \delta_{L\ell''}\) (two opposite boosts of the same magnitude undo each other).

Now insert (a) into the sum, with the common weight \(a = -q\) emerging:

\[ \sum_{\ell'} \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \sum_{\ell'} \frac{1}{\gamma}\,{}^{-1-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\beta)\; {}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta) = \sum_{\ell'} \Big\{ {}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell\ell'}(\beta) - \beta\big[C^m_{\ell+1} {}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell+1,\ell'}(\beta) + C^m_{\ell} {}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell-1,\ell'}(\beta)\big]\Big\}\, {}^{-q}\Kk^m_{\ell'\ell''}(-\beta). \]

Each of the three pieces collapses by (b):

\[ \sum_{\ell'} \Do^m_{\ell\ell'\ell''} = \delta_{\ell\ell''} - \beta\Big[C^m_{\ell+1}\,\delta_{\ell+1,\ell''} + C^m_{\ell}\,\delta_{\ell-1,\ell''}\Big], \]

which equals the boxed form after relabeling (\(\delta_{\ell+1,\ell''}\Rightarrow C^m_{\ell+1} = C^m_{\ell''}\); \(\delta_{\ell-1,\ell''}\Rightarrow C^m_{\ell} = C^m_{\ell''+1}\)). Since the result is \(q\)-independent it is an operator identity verbatim (Lemma L1(ii)). \(\blacksquare\)

Physical content

If the rest-frame Thomson cross-section were isotropic and total (i.e. if the collision term summed the projection over all rest-frame multipoles with unit weight), the round trip boost–project–boost-back would be the identity map up to the \(\mathcal O(\beta)\) dipole generated by the transformation of the optical depth — no spectral distortion at all. Scattering distorts spectra only because the cross-section weights \(\ell' = 0\) and \(\ell' = 2\) unequally (weights \(1\) and \(\tfrac1{10}\)) and discards \(\ell' \ge 3\). The sum rule is the photon-number-conservation backbone of the collision term, and it provides a strong numerical check on any table of Doppler-operator elements: at fixed \((\ell,\ell'')\), the \(\Oh\)-polynomial coefficients of \(\sum_{\ell'}\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\) (including the odd-\(\ell'\) elements not shown in Section 4) must telescope to the \(\Oh\)-independent constant above at every order in \(p\).

Verification

Check N4 of 02_doppler.wl: truncated at \(L_{\max} = 14\) intermediate multipoles at \(\beta=0.3\), deviation \(1.3\times10^{-17}\) (limited purely by the exponentially small tail \(\beta^{L_{\max}}\)), for \(m\in\{0,1\}\), \(\ell,\ell''\le2\).
