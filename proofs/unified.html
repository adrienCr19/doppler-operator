The unified formulation: it is all the aberration kernel

Thesis

The Doppler operator looks like it needs a theory of its own: a three-index object \(\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\) on a two-dimensional weight lattice, with bespoke raising recursions (N5/N6/N10), closed forms (N8), and — in the two independent developments, this site and the generation paper — a dozen separate-looking theorems. It needs none of that. It is a bilinear pairing of the ordinary aberration kernel — an object whose recursions and closed forms were established a decade ago (Dai & Chluba 2014; Chluba 2011). This page makes that precise. Three concrete payoffs:

  1. No new special function is needed to generate the operator. Every element — every closed form on this site — is built from aberration-kernel columns alone. (Contrast the thermal average, which genuinely does need a new transcendent, N9/N11 — a different problem; see the honesty note below.)
  2. The apparent degrees of freedom collapse. Outer indices, the shared middle index, the \((j,k)\) weight lattice, the spectral exponent \(q\), the two boost directions — five apparent labels — reduce to one two-index kernel family at one spectral parameter.
  3. Two independent developments are one calculus. Our N-results and the generation paper's ladders, gauge identity, factorization, and pure-physical relations are the same statement in different variables; the dictionary is the table below.

All boxed identities are verified end-to-end in verification/21_unified.wl (\(s=0\), \(m=0\)).

1  The one object — the aberration-kernel column

Definition (a shorthand, not a new function)
\[ \Kk_{ab}(q) \;\equiv\; {}^{-1-q}\Kk_{ab}(+\beta): \]

the site’s aberration kernel \({}^{d}\Kk_{ab}(\beta)\), with its Doppler weight locked to the physical value \(d = -1-q\) and its boost to \(+\beta\), read as a function of the spectral exponent \(q\). The single argument \(q\) (versus the usual \(\beta\)) signals this locked slice. Equivalently, on the operator level it is the return-leg boost operator on a power law: \(\Kk_{ab}(q) = \big[{}^{-1}\Bo_{ab}(\nu,-\beta)\big]\nu^q / \nu^q\) (weight \(-1\) = the SZ scattering rate; \(\Oh\to -q\) on \(\nu^q\)). It is not a new object — the generation paper calls this column \(F\); it is one element of the aberration kernel, whose theory is already complete. (Not to be confused with the thermal transcendent \(F(\mu,a,z)\) of N11, an unrelated object.)

2  The bilinear identity — and what it does and doesn’t say

Every Doppler element is a kernel pairing V1, exact
\[ \boxed{\;\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q) \;=\; \frac{(-1)^{\ell'+\ell''}}{\gamma}\; \Kk_{\ell\ell'}(q)\;\Kk_{\ell'\ell''}(q-1)\;} \]

Honest reading. The Doppler operator is defined as a product of two boost operators, so “it factorizes into two kernel elements” is close to the definition, not a discovery. The non-trivial content is the two refinements that make it useful: (i) by parity the two legs — a weight-\(-1\) boost along \(-\beta\) and a weight-\(0\) boost along \(+\beta\), which look like different objects — are the same family \(\Kk_{ab}\) evaluated at \(q\) and \(q-1\); and (ii) the middle index \(\ell'\) is a contraction index of the pairing, not a third free label. Raising any of \(\ell, \ell', \ell''\) is therefore raising an index of the one family \(\Kk\).

4  The one recurrence and the one seed

The column inherits the aberration kernel’s own three-term recurrence (Dai & Chluba; the \(\ell\)-raising B10). Applied to the row or the column of \(\Kk_{ab}\) it gives two forms, transposes of each other, whose only companion is the same column at a neighbouring \(q\) — this is where the weight lattice went (row \(\to q{+}1\), column \(\to q{-}1\)). With \(C_\ell = \ell/\sqrt{4\ell^2-1}\) (\(m=0\)):

Raise the row \(a\) V2a Raise the column \(b\) V2b
\[ \Kk_{ab}(q) = \frac{\gamma\,\Kk_{a-1,b}(q) - \Kk_{a-1,b}(q{+}1)}{C_a\,p} - \frac{C_{a-1}}{C_a}\,\Kk_{a-2,b}(q), \] \[ \Kk_{ab}(q) = -\frac{\gamma\,\Kk_{a,b-1}(q) - \Kk_{a,b-1}(q{-}1)}{C_b\,p} - \frac{C_{b-1}}{C_b}\,\Kk_{a,b-2}(q). \]
Seed, with \(J_w = \dfrac{(\gamma+p)^w-(\gamma-p)^w}{w}\)
\[ \Kk_{00}(q) = \frac{J_{2+q}}{2p} \qquad\Big(\text{general } m:\ {}^{d}\Kk^m_{mm} = \gamma^{-(m+d)}\,{}_2F_1\big(\tfrac{m+d}{2},\tfrac{m+d+1}{2};\tfrac32{+}m;\beta^2\big)\Big). \]

5  The symmetries

Column reflection, kernel parity; and their Doppler images V3
\[ \Kk_{ab}(q) = (-1)^{a+b}\,\Kk_{ba}(-4-q), \qquad \Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q) = \Do_{\ell''\ell'\ell}(-3-q)\;\text{(N1)}, \qquad \Do(-\beta) = (-1)^{\ell+\ell''}\Do(\beta)\;\text{(N2)}. \]

The column reflection is what makes the two recurrences mirror images; its image under the bilinear pairing is the Doppler reflection N1, which forces every diagonal element (\(\ell=\ell''\)) to be a polynomial in the diffusion operator \(\Dv = \Oh^2-3\Oh\).

6  Generation from the seed

Fill the \(\Kk\)-table from the seed by the two recurrences, then pair. Verified end-to-end — from the seed alone, no integrals — for \(\Do_{313}, \Do_{212}, \Do_{303}, \Do_{222}\) V4, exact:

K(0,0,q) = J[2+q]/(2p)                                    # seed (the diagonal kernel element)
K(a,0,q) = (γ K(a-1,0,q) - K(a-1,0,q+1))/(C_a p) - (C_{a-1}/C_a) K(a-2,0,q)   # raise row
K(a,b,q) = -(γ K(a,b-1,q) - K(a,b-1,q-1))/(C_b p) - (C_{b-1}/C_b) K(a,b-2,q)  # raise column
D(l,l',l'',q) = ((-1)^(l'+l'') / γ) K(l,l',q) K(l',l'',q-1)                   # pair

Each raise lowers an index and the seed carries \(1/(2p)\), so \(\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\) lands in \(\frac{1}{4\gamma p^{\,2+\ell+2\ell'+\ell''}}\,\mathbb Q[\gamma,p]\)-span\(\{J_{a+q}J_{b+q}\}\) — the N8 structure theorem, now the output of a four-line algorithm on a known object.

8  What this is, and what it is not

  • It is a reduction: the Doppler-operator generation problem is bookkeeping on the aberration kernel — one object, one recurrence, one seed, and the two projects’ theorems are its views. It tells you where to compute anything not yet written down (raise the kernel), and that no new function is needed to do so.
  • It is not a new hard theorem: the bilinear identity is close to the definition, and the kernel recurrence is prior work. The value is organizational — fewer objects, a known base, one dictionary.
  • It does not touch the genuinely new mathematics of the project, which lives after the electron average: the thermal Doppler operators are iterated Bessel antiderivatives \(F(\mu,a,z)\) (N9/N11), provably outside the classical special-function library. The generation problem reduces to the kernel; the averaging problem does not — and that boundary is itself one of the project’s results.
