Generating any Doppler operator from \(\Do_{000}\)

The one fact everything follows from

On power laws \(\nu^q\), every Doppler operator is a product of two legs — each leg a single boost-operator column \(\Kk_{ab}(q) \equiv {}^{-1-q}\Kk_{ab}(+\beta)\) (an aberration-kernel element, not a new object):

\[ \Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q) = \frac{(-1)^{\ell'+\ell''}}{\gamma}\;\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(q)\;\Kk_{\ell'\ell''}(q-1). \]

The three indices sit in definite slots: the outgoing \(\ell\) is the row of leg 1, the incoming \(\ell''\) is the column of leg 2, and the shared middle \(\ell'\) is the column of leg 1 and the row of leg 2 at once. A single three-term recurrence raises any index of a leg — so raising any index of \(\Do\) is one move on the right slot. That gives both answers below: how to raise each index (§2), and the easiest closed form of a general \(\Do_{hkl}\) (§3).

1  The leg (bracket) and its recurrence

Seed and the two recurrences T2, exact

Seed (\(J_w = \frac{(\gamma+p)^w-(\gamma-p)^w}{w}\), \(C_\ell = \ell/\sqrt{4\ell^2-1}\)):

\[ \Kk_{00}(q) = \frac{J_{2+q}}{2p}. \] \[ \textbf{raise the row } a:\quad \Kk_{ab}(q) = \frac{\gamma\,\Kk_{a-1,b}(q) - \Kk_{a-1,b}(q{+}1)}{C_a\,p} - \frac{C_{a-1}}{C_a}\,\Kk_{a-2,b}(q), \] \[ \textbf{raise the column } b:\quad \Kk_{ab}(q) = -\frac{\gamma\,\Kk_{a,b-1}(q) - \Kk_{a,b-1}(q{-}1)}{C_b\,p} - \frac{C_{b-1}}{C_b}\,\Kk_{a,b-2}(q). \]

Each references only smaller indices, with a single companion at \(q\pm1\). Both are the aberration kernel’s own \(\ell\)-raising relation (Dai & Chluba); they are transposes of one another.

2  How to raise each index of \(\Do\)

Raising an index of \(\Do\) = raising the corresponding leg-slot, then re-pairing. The three cases:

IndexSlot in \(\Do = \tfrac1\gamma\Kk_{\ell\ell'}(q)\Kk_{\ell'\ell''}(q{-}1)\)Raise by\(\Do\)-level relation
outgoing \(\ell\)row of leg 1row-recurrence (once)N5 / axis ladder P0
incoming \(\ell''\)column of leg 2column-recurrence (once)N6 / mirror axis ladder
middle \(\ell'\)column of leg 1 and row of leg 2both, togetherN10 (M) / P3

The middle index is the only hard one — because it is shared, raising it moves both legs, so the single relation (M) picks up cross-terms (the off-diagonal companions). See N10 for the four equivalent forms of the \(\ell'\)-step. The two outer indices each need just one recurrence. In terms of the physical operator, these are the site’s N5/N6 (with a weight-lattice companion) or the pure-physical P0/P1 below (companion eliminated by the gauge identity, every term a physical \(\Do\)).

3  Easiest closed form of a general \(\Do_{hkl}\) — the bracket route

Three steps T1–T4, exact
  1. Build the bracket table once from the seed by the two recurrences (§1). It is a small, reusable, one-parameter family — each entry a single \(J\)-combination.
  2. Pick the two legs you need: \(\Kk_{hk}\) and \(\Kk_{kl}\).
  3. Multiply: \(\displaystyle \Do_{hkl}(q) = \frac{(-1)^{k+l}}{\gamma}\,\Kk_{hk}(q)\,\Kk_{kl}(q-1)\).

No new integral, no re-derivation — every \(\Do\) is one product of two pre-built brackets. This is the cheapest route to a written-down closed form (it is exactly how N8/N16 were produced).

The closed forms this produces reproduce the previously-published tables exactly: their \(p\)-expansions equal the site’s Section-4 \(\hat O_\nu\)-polynomial tables (\(\ell'=0\) and \(\ell'=2\)) and the literature SZ operator [CR26, Eq. 11] through \(p^6\), as symbolic identities — 25_tables_match.wl (14 checks, all exact 0).

4  Pure-physical route (no kernels at all) — for a \(\Do\)-only recurrence

If you want to generate without ever forming a bracket — every intermediate a physical \(\Do\) — use the pure-physical scheme (paper P0–P4). It is nonlinear at the middle index but touches nothing but \(\Do\)'s:

  • (P0) axes: \(C_a\Do_{a00}(q) = \tfrac\gamma p\Do_{a-1,00}(q) - \tfrac1p\tfrac{J_{1+q}}{J_{2+q}}\Do_{a-1,00}(q{+}1) - C_{a-1}\Do_{a-2,00}(q)\) (and the mirror for \(\Do_{00b}\)) — build the outer axes from \(\Do_{000}\).
  • (P2) rank-one: \(\Do_{h\ell'l}(q) = \Do_{h\ell'0}(q)\,\Do_{0\ell'l}(q)/\Do_{0\ell'0}(q)\) — fill any fixed-\(\ell'\) plane from its border. With P0 this generates the entire \(\ell'{=}0\) plane.
  • (P3) climbs the middle index along the border (nonlinear); (N1) reflection \(\Do_{0\ell'l}(q)=\Do_{l\ell'0}(-3-q)\) supplies the other border.
  • (P1) outer pair combines both outer moves in one physical relation. See the reconciliation note.

Verified end-to-end: \(\Do_{303}\) built from \(\Do_{000}\) with no kernels (harness 22, PP4), and the whole scheme reproduces the closed forms of §3 exactly (harness 23). Use the bracket route (§3) to write a closed form; use this route when you want a self-contained \(\Do\)-to-\(\Do\) recurrence.

5  For the \(p\)-series: the rapidity ODE

\(\partial_\eta\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\) is a closed combination of \(\Do_{\ell\pm1,\ell'\ell''}\) and \(\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''\pm1}\) at fixed \(\ell'\) (N3); integrating from \(\Do|_{\eta=0}=\delta\) gives the Taylor series in \(p\) directly — the fastest route to the \(\mathcal O(p^8)\) tables.
