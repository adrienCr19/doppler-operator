Generating Doppler operators from Doppler operators

The generation problem: starting from the base element \(\Do_{000}\), produce any \(\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}(q)\) — closed form or series — using relations of the Doppler operator itself, with every intermediate object a physical \(\Do\). No boost or aberration kernels are formed or multiplied anywhere on this page.

1  The pure-physical scheme (P0–P4): closed forms from \(\Do_{000}\)

The \(\Do\)-only generation scheme verified end-to-end (harnesses 22, 23)

With \(J_w = 2\sinh(w\eta)/w\), \(C_\ell = \ell/\sqrt{4\ell^2-1}\), and the seed \(\Do_{000}(q) = J_{2+q}J_{1+q}/(4\gamma p^2)\):

  • (P0) axis ladders: \[ C_a\,\Do_{a00}(q) \;=\; \frac\gamma p\,\Do_{a-1,00}(q) \;-\; \frac1p\,\frac{J_{1+q}}{J_{2+q}}\,\Do_{a-1,00}(q{+}1) \;-\; C_{a-1}\,\Do_{a-2,00}(q), \] and the mirror for \(\Do_{00b}\) — build the two outer axes from \(\Do_{000}\) alone.
  • (P2) rank-one filling: \[ \Do_{h\ell'l}(q) \;=\; \frac{\Do_{h\ell'0}(q)\;\Do_{0\ell'l}(q)}{\Do_{0\ell'0}(q)} \] — any fixed-\(\ell'\) plane is filled from its border. With P0 this generates the entire \(\ell'{=}0\) plane; with N1 reflection, \(\Do_{0\ell'l}(q) = \Do_{l\ell'0}(-3-q)\) supplies the other border from the same data.
  • (P3) middle-index step: climbs \(\ell'\to\ell'{+}1\) along the border (nonlinear — ratios of physical elements). The fully explicit pure-\(\Do\) form is N25, Eq. (P3): \[ C_{\ell'+1}\Do_{h,\ell'+1,0}(q) = \frac{\Do_{\ell'+1,00}(q{-}1)}{p} \Big[\gamma\tfrac{\Do_{h\ell'0}(q)}{\Do_{\ell'00}(q{-}1)} - \tfrac{J_{q-1}}{J_q}\tfrac{\Do_{h\ell'0}(q{-}1)}{\Do_{\ell'00}(q{-}2)}\Big] - C_{\ell'}\tfrac{\Do_{\ell'+1,00}(q{-}1)}{\Do_{\ell'-1,00}(q{-}1)}\Do_{h,\ell'-1,0}(q). \] See N10 for the linear form and why it needs the off-diagonal companions.
  • (P1) outer pair: both outer moves combined in a single physical relation (reconciliation note).

Recipe for a general \(\Do_{hkl}\) in closed form: P0 up the axis to \(\Do_{h00}\); P3 up the middle index to the border elements \(\Do_{hk0}\), \(\Do_{0kl}\) (or N1 for the second); P2 to fill. Every intermediate is a physical Doppler operator, every output a finite \(J\)-combination. Verified end-to-end: \(\Do_{303}\) built from \(\Do_{000}\) this way (harness 22, PP4), and the scheme reproduces the N8 closed-form catalog exactly (harness 23), whose \(p\)-expansions in turn equal the published tables and the SZ operator [CR26, Eq. 11] symbolically (25_tables_match.wl).

2  How to raise each index — at the \(\Do\) level

Index\(\Do\)-level relationcharacter
outgoing \(\ell\)N5 (lattice companion) or the axis ladder P0 (pure physical)linear, three-term
incoming \(\ell''\)N6 or the mirror of P0 (equivalently N1 + P0)linear, three-term
middle \(\ell'\)N10 (M) (linear, with off-diagonal companions) or N25 (P3) (pure \(\Do\), nonlinear — explicit)the only hard one

The middle index is hard for a structural reason: a first-order move acts on one scattering leg at a time (cf.\ the rapidity flows), and \(\ell'\) is shared by both legs — so any linear \(\ell'\)-relation needs the off-diagonal companions, and the companion-free P3 is necessarily nonlinear.

3  For the \(p\)-series: the rapidity ODE

\(\partial_\eta\Do_{\ell\ell'\ell''}\) is a closed combination of \(\Do_{\ell\pm1,\ell',\ell''}\) and \(\Do_{\ell,\ell',\ell''\pm1}\) at fixed \(\ell'\) (N3); integrating from \(\Do|_{\eta=0}=\delta\) generates the Taylor tables directly — the fastest route to the \(\mathcal O(p^8)\) data product — and the all-index flow (N3′) moves \(\ell'\) too.
